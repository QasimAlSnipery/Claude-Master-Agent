# Poliomyelitis

## The Big Picture
Polio = enterovirus causing **acute flaccid paralysis** by destroying anterior-horn motor neurons. **>99% reduction since 1988** through vaccination; now endemic only in **Pakistan & Afghanistan** (wild type 1). Eradication strategy hinges on **OPV campaigns + AFP surveillance**.

## The Virus
- **Picornaviridae, Enterovirus**, single-stranded RNA, ~50 nm, **3 serotypes (1, 2, 3)**.
- Resistant to bile/acid, survives at −20°C; killed by heat, chlorine, UV, formaldehyde.
- **Type 2 eradicated 1999, type 3 in 2020**; type 1 remains.

## Epidemiology & Transmission
- **Reservoir: human only.** Mainly **children <5**.
- **Faecal-oral** (direct); also contaminated food/water, flies; rainy season.
- Virus enters mouth → multiplies in intestine → shed in faeces. **Most infected are asymptomatic carriers** who silently spread it.
- **A single confirmed paralytic case = an epidemic** (only 1/200 infections paralyse).

## Pathogenesis
Mouth → replication in pharynx/GI/lymphatics → blood → CNS → **destruction of anterior-horn motor neurons** (directly or via ischaemia/oedema).

## Clinical (incubation 3–21 d, avg 10)
- **~95% asymptomatic/mild** (fever, malaise, headache, vomiting).
- Aseptic meningitis / non-paralytic 1–2%.
- **Paralytic <1%:** acute-onset **asymmetric flaccid paralysis**, fever at onset, **legs > arms**, no progression after 2–3 days, **paralysis permanent**; respiratory-muscle paralysis → death (CFR 2–10%, rises with age).
- Outcome of paralysed: 30% full recovery, 30% mild, 30% moderate-severe, 10% die.
- **Precipitants of paralysis:** trauma, fatigue, pregnancy, **IM injection during febrile illness**, tonsillectomy.

## Diagnosis
Virus in **throat (36 h) and stool (72 h)**; persists in stool 3–6 weeks. Two stool specimens 24–48 h apart, within 14 days of paralysis onset.

## Prevention & Vaccines
- **OPV (Sabin, live)** — trivalent→now bivalent; induces **intestinal + circulating immunity**; key for eradication. **Contraindicated in immunocompromised + household contacts**; diarrhoea NOT a contraindication. Rare **VAPP** (~1/2.7 million).
- **IPV (Salk, inactivated)** — circulating antibodies only; in Iraq 2 doses (4 & 6 months) alongside OPV.

## Eradication Strategy (4 pillars)
1. **Routine immunisation** (OPV at 0,2,4,6 mo + boosters).
2. **National Immunisation Days (NIDs)** — 2 rounds OPV to all <5 regardless of status.
3. **Mopping up** — intensive campaigns in high-risk areas.
4. **AFP surveillance.**
- Polio is eradicable: effective vaccine, lasting immunity, no animal reservoir, limited environmental persistence.

## AFP Surveillance
- **AFP** = rapid-onset flaccid weakness. Report **all AFP <15 yrs (or any age if polio suspected) within 48 h**; collect **2 stool specimens 24–48 h apart within 14 days**.
- Diagnosis is clinical (notify immediately, don't wait for tests); notification is **obligatory**.
- Quality target: **≥2 non-polio AFP per 100,000 <15 yrs**. Region certified polio-free after **3 years no wild virus** with good surveillance.
- DDx of AFP: Guillain-Barré, transverse myelitis, traumatic neuritis, hypokalaemia, TB meningitis.

## Walk-Away Points
- **Faecal-oral, anterior-horn destruction, asymmetric flaccid paralysis (legs > arms), permanent.**
- **95% asymptomatic**; 1 paralytic case = epidemic; CFR up to 10%.
- **OPV = intestinal immunity** (eradication tool) but rare VAPP + CI in immunocompromised; IPV = injectable.
- Eradication = **routine + NIDs + mopping up + AFP surveillance**; report AFP <15 within 48 h, 2 stools.
