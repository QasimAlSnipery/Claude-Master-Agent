# PANCREAS (Surgery)

## Anatomy & physiology
- **Retroperitoneal.** Head = 30% mass; body + tail = 70%. Weighs ~80 g. **80–90% exocrine acinar tissue** (lobules).
- **Islets of Langerhans (endocrine)**: **75% B cells (insulin), 20% A cells (glucagon)**, rest D cells (somatostatin) + PP cells.

## Investigation
- **Serum enzymes**: **amylase = most widely used** (rises in hours, falls over 4–8 days); **lipase = more sensitive + specific** (less available).
- **Causes of raised amylase OTHER than pancreatitis**: upper GI perforation, mesenteric infarction, viscus torsion, retroperitoneal haematoma, ectopic pregnancy, **macroamylasaemia, renal failure, salivary gland inflammation**.
- Morphology: US, CT, MRI, ERCP, EUS, plain X-ray. Normal duct 2mm.

## Acute pancreatitis
### Definition (2 of 3)
1. **Abdominal pain**, 2. **amylase/lipase ≥3× normal**, 3. **characteristic CT findings**.
- **Mechanism**: premature activation of pancreatic enzymes → **autodigestion** → oedema, haemorrhage, necrosis. Inflammatory mediators → systemic complications (haemodynamic instability, bacteraemia, **ARDS**, GI haemorrhage, renal failure, **DIC**).

### Categories
- **Mild (interstitial oedematous)**: most patients, mortality ~1%.
- **Severe (necrotising)**: 5–10%; pancreatic necrosis + severe SIRS + multi-organ failure; **mortality 20–50%**.

### Epidemiology & aetiology
- 3% of abdominal pain admissions. Peak: young men + older women.
- **Biliary calculi 50–70% (commonest), alcohol 25%.**
- **"I GET SMASHED"**: **I**diopathic, **G**allstones, **E**thanol, **T**rauma, **S**teroids, **M**umps/malignancy, **A**utoimmune, **S**corpion sting, **H**ypercalcaemia/hyperlipidaemia/hereditary, **E**RCP (post), **D**rugs (azathioprine, valproate, thiazides, oestrogens). Also pancreas divisum, post-surgery.
- **Establish aetiology; if gallstones → cholecystectomy same admission.**

### Clinical
- **Pain = cardinal** (rapid, max in minutes, epigastric, **radiates to back ~50%**, refractory to analgesia). N/V/retching. Tachypnoea, tachycardia, hypotension.
- **SIRS** = ≥2 of: HR >90, temp <36/>38, RR >20 or pCO2 <32, WBC <4000/>12,000.
- Mild icterus (gallstone), swinging pyrexia (cholangitis), **Grey Turner's (flank) / Cullen's (umbilicus) sign** (haemorrhage), ileus/ascites, epigastric mass, guarding, **pleural effusion 10–20%**.

### Investigations (3 questions: diagnosis? severity? aetiology?)
- **Amylase ≥3× normal** diagnostic; normal doesn't exclude (late presentation); **lipase more sensitive/specific**. **CECT = best single imaging** if doubt.
- **Severity scoring**: at 24h/48h/7d. **Ranson + Glasgow (PANCREAS)** — **score ≥3 at 48h = severe**. Persisting organ failure = severe. **CRP >150 mg/L at 48h** = severity. BMI >30 = higher risk.
  - **Glasgow (PANCREAS)**: **P**aO2 <8kPa, **A**ge >55, **N**eutrophils WBC >15, **C**alcium <2.0, **R**enal urea >16, **E**nzymes LDH >600, **A**lbumin <32, **S**ugar glucose >10.
- **Atlanta classification (2013)**: **Mild** (no organ failure/complications); **Moderately severe** (transient organ failure <48h ± local complications); **Severe** (persistent organ failure >48h).
- **Imaging**: plain film — **sentinel loop, colon cut-off sign, renal halo**. US within 24h (gallstones, exclude cholecystitis, CBD). **CECT** for: doubt, distinguish interstitial vs necrotising (**Balthazar criteria**; underestimates necrosis first 72h), organ failure/sepsis, local complications. ERCP for gallstone pancreatitis + cholangitis (urgent).

### Management
- **Mild → conservative**: IV fluids, observation, brief fasting if nauseated, **NO antibiotics**, analgesia/antiemetics; CT only if deteriorating.
- **Severe → HDU/ICU**: analgesia, **aggressive fluid resuscitation**, O2, invasive monitoring, NG drainage (initially), **antibiotics if cholangitis** (prophylactic considered), CT if deteriorating, **ERCP within 72h for severe gallstone pancreatitis/cholangitis**, organ support, **enteral (NG) feeding** if nutrition needed.

### Complications
- **Systemic (first week)**: shock, arrhythmias, **ARDS**, renal failure, **DIC**, hypocalcaemia, hyperglycaemia, ileus.
- **Local (after first week)**:
  - **Acute peripancreatic fluid collection (APFC)**: early, no wall, sterile, usually resolves.
  - **Pancreatic necrosis** (no parenchymal enhancement on CECT) → **acute necrotic collection (ANC)** → after 4 weeks → **walled-off necrosis (WON)**. **Infected necrosis = mortality up to 50%**; **sterile necrosis should NOT be drained**.
  - **Pancreatic abscess** (circumscribed pus).
  - **Pseudocyst**: amylase-rich fluid in fibrous/granulation wall; follows mild pancreatitis, outside pancreas (unresolved APFC); **needs ≥4 weeks to form**. Complications: infection (abscess/sepsis), rupture (GI bleed/fistula/peritonitis), enlargement (obstructive jaundice/bowel obstruction/pain), **erosion into vessel → haemorrhage**.
  - Also: ascites, pleural effusion, portal/splenic vein thrombosis, pseudoaneurysm.

## Chronic pancreatitis
- Continuing inflammation → **irreversible morphological change**, pain ± permanent loss of function (may be painless). May be same spectrum as acute.

## High-yield one-liners
- **Diagnosis = 2 of 3 (pain, amylase/lipase ≥3×, CT). Lipase more sensitive/specific than amylase.**
- **Gallstones (50–70%) + alcohol (25%) = main causes; I GET SMASHED mnemonic.**
- **Severity: Ranson/Glasgow ≥3 at 48h = severe; CRP >150 at 48h; persisting organ failure.** Atlanta: mild/moderate/severe (by organ failure).
- **Grey Turner's (flank) + Cullen's (umbilicus) = haemorrhage.**
- **Mild → conservative, NO antibiotics. Severe → ICU + fluids + enteral feeding; ERCP <72h for gallstone + cholangitis.**
- **Necrosis: ANC → WON (4 wks); infected necrosis 50% mortality; sterile necrosis don't drain.**
- **Pseudocyst = amylase-rich, fibrous wall, ≥4 weeks; gallstone pancreatitis → cholecystectomy same admission.**
