# Cholera

## The Big Picture
Cholera = acute **watery diarrhoea** from **Vibrio cholerae** (toxin-mediated), spread **faecal-oral** via contaminated water/food. The danger is **rapid severe dehydration → hypovolaemic shock → death within a day**, yet it's preventable and easily treated — **rehydration saves >98%**. Iraq has cyclical outbreaks every 3–5 years.

## The Bacterium
- **Vibrio cholerae** — comma-shaped, Gram-negative, aerobic bacillus (Koch, 1883, Egypt).
- **H (flagellar) + O (somatic) antigens**; pathogenic serogroups **O1 and O139**. O1 serotypes Inaba/Ogawa; biotypes **classical & El-Tor** (7th pandemic = El-Tor, 1961 Indonesia).

## Pathogenesis
- Produces an **enterotoxin (exotoxin)** → activates **adenylate cyclase / cAMP** in small-intestine mucosa → massive secretion of fluid + electrolytes (isotonic to plasma).
- **Acts locally, non-invasive** → **no RBC/WBC in stool**; painless watery diarrhoea → dehydration → shock/renal shutdown.

## Transmission & Risk
- **Faecal-oral** via contaminated water/food (person-to-person rare). High infective dose (~10¹¹).
- **At risk:** children + elderly (severe), **blood group O** (more susceptible), reduced gastric acid.
- Outbreak factors: poor sanitation, contaminated water, flies, crowding/religious gatherings.
- **Incubation:** few hours–5 days (typically 2–3); carriers shed for months.

## Clinical
- Mostly mild/asymptomatic; **5–10% severe**.
- **Sudden painless watery diarrhoea** (fishy odour → **"rice-water" stool** within 24 h) ± vomiting; **fever typically absent** (present in children).
- Severe: stool >250 mL/kg → **≥10% body-weight loss = severe dehydration**, shock, death.
- **Children:** more severe, fever, shock, **hypoglycaemia → convulsions**; exclude rotavirus.

## Diagnosis
- Stool **microscopy** (gram stain + dark-field shows darting motility); culture on **alkaline TCBS agar**. Serology only for epidemic source tracing. In confirmed outbreaks, treat clinically without testing every case.

## Treatment
- **Rehydration is the priority** (2 phases: rehydration in 4 h + maintenance).
  - **Severe → IV Ringer lactate** (corrects metabolic acidosis, preferred over saline).
  - **ORS** for the rest at 500–1000 mL/h (**up to 80% treatable with ORS**).
- **Antibiotics** (shorten illness, reduce stool volume, 3–5 days): adults — **doxycycline/tetracycline**, cotrimoxazole, ciprofloxacin; **children/pregnant — azithromycin** (also erythromycin, furazolidone). Start when suspected.
- **Zinc** for children (10 mg <6 mo, 20 mg 6 mo–12 yr, 2 weeks).

## Mortality
- Untreated severe: **50% fatal**; with rehydration **<2%**; with good care **<0.2%**. Most deaths within first day.

## Outbreak Control & Prevention
- Verify diagnosis, **notify**, early case finding, isolated treatment centre, rehydration, epidemiological investigation, sanitation, chemoprophylaxis (household contacts), vaccination.
- **Enteric precautions** (isolation, gowns/gloves, hand hygiene). Safe water + hygiene education.
- **Oral cholera vaccines:** inactivated (**Dukoral** WC-rBS — 2 doses, 2-yr protection; **Shanchol/Euvichol** bivalent — 2 doses, 3-yr) and **live (Vaxchora)**. No longer required for most travellers.

## Walk-Away Points
- **V. cholerae enterotoxin → cAMP → rice-water diarrhoea**, no fever, non-invasive (no blood/WBC).
- **Dehydration kills** — **Ringer lactate IV (severe) + ORS** rescues >98%; antibiotics adjunct.
- Diagnose on **TCBS agar**; blood group O more susceptible.
- Prevent: safe water, sanitation, **oral vaccines**, enteric precautions; Iraq = cyclical outbreaks.
