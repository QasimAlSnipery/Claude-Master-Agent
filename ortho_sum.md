# PELVIS & LOWER LIMB FRACTURES

## PELVIS
- Pelvic ring = 2 innominate bones + sacrum; anterior = **symphysis pubis**, posterior = **SI joints**. Stability from bone + ligaments (sacroiliac, sacrospinous, sacrotuberous, symphyseal).
- **Serious injuries**: soft-tissue, **blood loss (2–3 L)**, sepsis, ARDS; **mortality ~10%** if soft tissue involved.
- **Assessment**: ABCD first; severe pain, swelling/bruising (perineum/scrotum), urinary extravasation signs, perineal widening = disruption. **Blood at external meatus = urethral rupture → do NOT catheterize.** Rectal exam, sciatic nerve check.
- **Imaging**: AP pelvis (all polytrauma) + inlet/outlet/oblique views; CT for complex; urethrogram/cystogram.

### Isolated fractures
- **Avulsion** (sportsmen): AIIS (rectus femoris), ASIS (sartorius), pubis (adductor longus), ischial tuberosity (hamstrings) → rest.
- **Direct** (ischium/iliac blade) → bed rest. **Stress** (osteoporotic, pubic rami, painless) → isotope scan.

### Pelvic ring fractures (disrupted at 2 points)
- **AP compression (Open book)**: frontal collision; pubic rami fracture or symphysis disruption (**>2cm = unstable**), SI disruption.
- **Lateral compression (closed/buckle)**: side impact; pubic rami + SI/sacral fracture.
- **Vertical shear**: fall on one leg; innominate displaced vertically; **severe, unstable, organ injury, retroperitoneal haematoma**.
- **Management**: ABC, treat shock, **rapid pelvic binder/clamp**, laparotomy if needed; urologist for bladder/urethra. Stable open book (<2.5cm) → binder; **unstable open book (>2.5cm) → external fixation / symphyseal plate**; vertical shear → ext fix + SI screws.

### Acetabular fractures
- Femoral head driven into pelvis (side blow / dashboard). Types: **wall** (with hip dislocation), **anterior column** (ilio-pectineal line, not weight-bearing), **posterior column** (ilio-ischial line, weight-bearing, post hip dislocation), transverse, complex.
- Exclude sciatic/femoral nerve injury. **Non-op** if <3mm / not weight-bearing / elderly → traction 6 wks. **ORIF** if displaced/unstable/retained fragment. Complications: AVN, sciatic nerve, heterotopic bone, OA.

## HIP DISLOCATION
- **Posterior = 90% (commonest)**; dashboard injury (hip+knee flexed). **Leg: shortened, adducted, internally rotated, flexed.** Check **sciatic nerve**.
- **Thompson-Epstein**: I (none/small chip), II (large wall), III (comminuted wall), IV (column), V (femoral head).
- **Treatment: URGENT reduction under GA within 6 hours** (traction + flex hip/knee 90° + rotate → clunk); post-reduction CT. Type I → traction; II → ORIF wall; full weight-bearing after 12 wks.
- **Complications**: **sciatic nerve 10–20%**, vascular (superior gluteal a.), **AVN (10% if immediate reduction, 40% if delayed)**, myositis ossificans, OA. **Early reduction ↓ AVN.**

## FEMORAL NECK — INTRACAPSULAR
- Females, 7th–8th decade; osteoporosis, DM, alcohol. Fall on trochanter or twisting. **Leg externally rotated + short.**
- Levels: subcapital, transcervical, basal.
- **Garden classification**: I (incomplete, impacted/valgus), II (complete, undisplaced), III (complete, partial displacement, varus), IV (complete, full displacement).
- **Poor union** (interrupted blood supply to proximal fragment, synovial fluid, no periosteum) → **no role for conservative treatment**.
- **Treatment**: **<70 yrs → closed reduction + internal fixation** (cannulated screws / DHS) — perfect reduction + rigid fixation. **>70 low-demand → hemiarthroplasty (bipolar)**. **THR**: >50 active/healthy, Garden III–IV, OA, non-union, metastatic.
- **Complications**: **mortality 20% at 4 months**; **AVN femoral head**, non-union, OA.

## INTERTROCHANTERIC — EXTRACAPSULAR
- **Older patients, osteoporotic women (F:M 6:1)**; **unite easily, rarely osteonecrosis** (extracapsular). Fall on trochanter. **Leg short + externally rotated.**
- **Kyle classification** I–IV (I undisplaced → IV severe comminution/reverse oblique/subtrochanteric).
- **Treatment: ALWAYS surgery** (early mobilization). **Dynamic hip screw (DHS) = stable**; **proximal femoral nail (PFN) = unstable/reverse oblique/subtrochanteric**; THR if severe comminution/OA/failed fixation.
- **Prognosis: 25–30% mortality in 1st year** (worse: male, intertrochanteric>intracapsular, op delay >2 days, >80 yrs, ≥2 comorbidities). Malunion common (varus).

## FEMORAL SHAFT
- Strong muscles → severe displacement. **High energy (young, RTA — 1–2 L blood loss), low energy (elderly/pathological), child abuse.**
- Types: spiral (FFH), transverse/oblique (direct), comminuted, segmental.
- **Clinical: ATLS** (shock, other injuries); examine neurovascular, hip, pelvis. Imaging: 2 views, 2 joints; CT for concomitant (neck, acetabulum, plateau).
- **Emergency**: Thomas splint / skeletal traction.
- **Definitive**: children <5 → traction + spica; 5–10 (<50kg) → flexible IM nail; **adults → locked IM nailing (standard)**; plating for distal/intra-articular/ipsilateral neck/growing child; **ext fix for compound type III/multiple/vascular**; pathological → IM nail + cement.
- **Complications**: shock, **fat embolism + ARDS** (multiple fractures), thromboembolism, infection; malunion, delayed/non-union.

## DISTAL FEMUR (supracondylar)
- **Bimodal**: young (high energy) / elderly osteoporotic (low energy). Supracondylar, intercondylar (T/Y), comminuted.
- **Displacement: distal fragment into extension (gastrocnemius) → endangers POPLITEAL ARTERY**; varus (adductor magnus); shortening (hamstring/quads).
- **Clinical: examine distal pulses.** CT for intra-articular (Hoffa fracture).
- **Treatment**: non-op rare (non-displaced/non-ambulatory, hinged brace). Operative: **retrograde IM nail** (no intra-articular, osteoporotic), **locking plate** (comminuted), **dynamic condylar screw** (intra-articular no comminution).
- **Complications**: **popliteal artery damage** (even minimal displacement → explore/repair), stiffness, non-union, malunion.

## KNEE DISLOCATION (emergency)
- High violence → cruciate + collateral rupture. Gross deformity. **Exclude popliteal artery + common peroneal nerve.**
- **Urgent reduction under GA**, back slab 15° flexion, monitor circulation 1 week. Open reduction if irreducible/open/vascular/obese.
- **Complications: popliteal artery 40–50% (immediate repair + fasciotomy)**, common peroneal nerve 25% (neuropraxia), instability, stiffness.

## PATELLA
- Largest sesamoid; part of extensor mechanism. **Direct** (fall/dashboard → undisplaced/comminuted) or **indirect** (passive flexion + quads contraction → transverse displaced).
- **Test active knee extension** (intact = extensor mechanism OK); aspirate → haemarthrosis + **fat droplets**.
- **Treatment**: undisplaced + intact extensor → POP cylinder in extension. **Displaced transverse / extensor failure → ORIF tension band (2 K-wires + figure-of-8)**. Comminuted non-salvageable → patellectomy.

## TIBIAL PLATEAU
- Periarticular; **male 40s (high energy), female 70s (falls)**. Varus/valgus + axial load (**bumper fracture**). Associated meniscal/ACL injury.
- **Schatzker classification**: I (lateral cleavage, young), II (cleavage + depression, older), III (pure central depression, osteoporotic), IV (medial condyle — peroneal nerve risk), V (bicondylar), VI (plateau + metaphysis-diaphysis dissociation).
- **Clinical: doughy haemarthrosis; neurovascular exam (Type IV → common peroneal neuropraxia); exclude compartment syndrome.** CT for articular depression.
- **Treatment**: I non-displaced → aspirate + cast; displaced → 2 cancellous screws. II/III → if depression <5mm conservative; severe → elevate + bone graft + buttress plate. IV → medial buttress plate. V/VI ("**Span, Scan, Fix**") → buttress plates or circular ext fixator (Ilizarov).
- **Complications**: compartment syndrome, neurovascular, blisters; stiffness, OA.

## TIBIA/FIBULA SHAFT
- **Most common long bone fracture; subcutaneous → often compound (open).**
- **Twisting** → spiral, low energy, minimal soft tissue, bone penetrates skin from within. **Direct/angulatory** → transverse, high energy, comminution, more compound.
- **Always check for compartment syndrome.**
- **Treatment**: conservative (low energy, closed, pediatric) → MUA + full POP (midthigh→metatarsal), functional brace at 4 wks. **Operative: closed locked IM nailing = standard**; percutaneous elastic nail (children); ext fix (compound/infected/bone gap); locking plate (metaphyseal).
- **Complications**: vascular (proximal), **compartment syndrome**, infection (open), malunion, delayed/non-union (common — poor soft tissue), stiffness.

## ANKLE (Malleolar / Pott's)
- Twisting with foot anchored. Pushed malleolus → **oblique**; pulled → **transverse**.
- **Danis-Weber (by fibular fracture level)**: **A** (below syndesmosis, transverse lateral malleolus — internal rotation/adduction), **B** (at syndesmosis, oblique — external rotation, ± deltoid rupture), **C** (above syndesmosis — abduction, syndesmotic disruption, ± posterior malleolus).
- **Imaging**: AP, lateral, **30° oblique (mortise)** view — check medial joint space, syndesmosis (diastasis).
- **Treatment**: conservative if isolated non-displaced medial/lateral malleolus or posterior malleolus <25% / <2mm step → below-knee slab then cast 6–8 wks. **Operative (displaced A/B, all C)**: **ORIF lateral malleolus first (most important)**, then medial (malleolar screws/tension band), syndesmotic screw if unstable, posterior malleolus if large.
- **Complications**: vascular (if subluxation), wound breakdown; malunion, **non-union (medial malleolus)**, stiffness, OA.

## HIGH-YIELD ONE-LINERS
- **Pelvis: open book >2.5cm = unstable → ext fix; blood at meatus = urethral injury, don't catheterize; pelvic binder for shock.**
- **Posterior hip dislocation = 90%, leg flexed/adducted/internally rotated; reduce <6h; AVN risk (early reduction ↓).**
- **Femoral neck (intracapsular) = Garden classification; <70 → fixation, >70 → hemiarthroplasty; AVN risk; no conservative.**
- **Intertrochanteric (extracapsular) = DHS (stable)/PFN (unstable); unites well, no AVN; 25–30% 1-yr mortality.**
- **Femoral shaft = locked IM nail; watch fat embolism + blood loss.**
- **Supracondylar femur + knee dislocation → POPLITEAL ARTERY.** Knee dislocation = 40–50% popliteal injury.
- **Patella = test active extension; transverse displaced → tension band.**
- **Tibial plateau = Schatzker; bumper fracture; exclude compartment syndrome.**
- **Tibia shaft = commonest long bone, often open; IM nail; compartment syndrome.**
- **Ankle = Danis-Weber A/B/C; fix lateral malleolus first; mortise view.**
