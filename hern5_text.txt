Umbilical Hernia
A defect in the fascial sheath just beneath the umbilicus.it coulde be
*Congenital which occurs in childern because of incomplete closure
of the umbilical orifice.
The majority close spontaneously during the first year of life.
Surgical repair should only be carried out if :
1-the hernis has not disappeared by the age of 3 years.
2-the fascial defect is greater than 1.5 cm in diamater.
*Aquired umbilical hernia:
Its usually secondary to increase intra abdominal pressure.the most
common causes are:
1- pregnancy.
2- ascitis.
3-ovarian cyst.
4-fibroid.

Para-Umbilical hernia
It occurs just above or just below the umbilicus.Its more
common in obese females.Predisposing factors are
multiple pregnancies and obesity.
The neck of the sac is usually narrow and therefore there
is high risk of strangulation.
The most common content is omentum then intestine.
Treatmant:
1-Content of sac freed from its wall,excision of the sac
and the fascial defect is repaired by upper flap
overlapping the lower,a two layer overlapping repair there
by doubling the strength of repair(Mayo repair).
2-If the defect is more than 4cm, or recurrent hernia then
mesh repair is done.

Epigastric Hernia
This is usually a small protrusion through the linea Alba
in the upper part of the abdomen.
It consist of the extraperitoneal fat only but may contain
omentum or small bowel.
It may painful because of trapping and ischemia of the
extraperitoneal fat.
Treatment:
Enlarging the defect,excision of the fat then simple
suture of the defect.
If the defect is more than 4cm in diameter then mesh
repair is preferable.

Incisional Hernia
This occurs through a defect in the scar of a previous
abdominal incision.
Aetiology :
*Age :wound healing is poor in the older patients.
*Obesity
*Postoperative wound infection.
*Postoperative wound haematoma.
*Raised intra-abdominal pressure postoperatively.
e.g. Coughing,straining,constipation,ileus.
*Steroid therapy.
*Type of incision: midline vertical wounds have a higher
incidence than transverse incisions.
Poor suturing technique.

Incisional Hernia
Sign & symptoms:
*A swellig protrude through the wound.
*It may occur up to 5 years postoperatively.
*Many are large and involve the whole incision
consequently the neck of the sac is wide and the risk of
strangulation is rare.
*If the defect is small there is a greater risk of
strangluation.
Treatment
Pallative : abdominal belt.
Preoperative meaure: reduce body weight,treat
cough,improve nutritional status.stop smoking.
Surgery : excision of the sac, closure of the defect.
Large hernia :Mesh repair

Diaphragmatic Hernia
Traumatic :
It follows chest or abdominal trauma. Its rare.
Left diaphragm affected more than right and accompained by
herniation of stomach and spleen.
Hiatus:
1-Sliding.
The gastroesophageal junction itself slides through the defect into
the chest.
2-Para-esophageal.
The gastroesophageal junction remains fixed while another portion of
the stomach moves up through the defect into the chest. This can be
dangreous as they may allow the stomach to rotate and obstruct.
