# Monkeypox & Chickenpox

## The Big Picture
Two pox-like vesicular rash illnesses that are easily confused. **Monkeypox** = orthopoxvirus (DNA), zoonotic, with **lymphadenopathy + centrifugal rash all in one stage**. **Chickenpox (varicella)** = herpesvirus (VZV), highly contagious, with **crops of lesions in different stages ("waves") + central rash**. Key discriminator: **lymphadenopathy present in monkeypox, absent in chickenpox.**

## Quick Comparison
| | Monkeypox | Chickenpox |
|---|---|---|
| Virus | **Orthopoxvirus (Poxviridae), dsDNA** | **Varicella-zoster (herpesvirus)** |
| Lymphadenopathy | **Yes (distinctive)** | No |
| Rash distribution | Whole body, face + palms/soles, **centrifugal** | One region, **central/trunk** |
| Rash stages | All lesions **same stage** | Lesions in **different stages (waves)** |

## Monkeypox
- **Zoonotic** orthopoxvirus, central/west African rainforest; smallpox-like but milder. Discovered in monkeys 1958; first human case Congo 1970. Mortality ~1/100 (younger worse).
- **Transmission:** enters via broken skin/respiratory/mucous membranes.
  - **Animal→human:** bites, scratches, bushmeat, direct contact.
  - **Human→human:** lesions, body fluids, respiratory droplets, contaminated bedding.
- **Clinical:** incubation **6–13 days (5–21)**.
  - **Invasion (1–4 d):** fever, headache, **lymphadenopathy (distinctive)**, back pain, myalgia.
  - **Rash (within 1–3 d of fever):** face (95%), palms/soles (75%), oral mucosa (70%); evolves **macule → papule → vesicle → pustule → crust**; self-limiting 2–4 weeks.
- **Diagnosis:** **PCR (NAAT)** from vesicle/crust swab = preferred. (Blood often inconclusive — short viraemia.)
- **Prevention/treatment:** isolation, standard precautions, household disinfection; **smallpox vaccine ~85% effective** (Imvanex, ACAM2000); antiviral **tecovirimat (ST-246)**, cidofovir; vaccinia immune globulin.

## Chickenpox (Varicella)
- **Highly contagious** VZV; vesicular rash + fever; progresses **macules → papules → vesicles → crusts**; usually self-limiting but can be severe.
- Endemic worldwide; **late winter/spring** in temperate areas.
- **Transmission:** **respiratory droplets + airborne**, vesicle fluid contact, fomites. **Infectious 1–2 days before rash until all lesions crust.**
- **Incubation 10–21 days (avg 14–16).**
- **Clinical:** prodrome (fever, malaise, worse in adults) → rash 1–2 days later in **crops/different stages**.
- **Chickenpox vs shingles:** chickenpox = primary infection, generalised; **shingles = reactivation, single dermatome, severe pain (postherpetic neuralgia)**.
- **Diagnosis:** clinical; **PCR** (vesicle fluid) for atypical/severe/immunocompromised.
- **Prevention:**
  - **Varicella vaccine (live attenuated):** 12–15 mo + 4–6 yr; >90% immunity after 2 doses. **Zoster vaccine** for >50 yrs.
  - **Isolation** until all lesions crust (~7–10 days).
  - **Post-exposure:** **vaccine within 72 h**; **VZIG** for high-risk (pregnant, immunocompromised, neonates) — best within 96 h; acyclovir if late.
  - Hand hygiene, respiratory precautions, universal childhood vaccination.

## Walk-Away Points
- **Monkeypox = DNA orthopoxvirus + lymphadenopathy + same-stage centrifugal rash**; chickenpox = **herpesvirus, no lymphadenopathy, different-stage central rash**.
- Both diagnosed by **PCR** from vesicles.
- **Smallpox vaccine ~85% protects against monkeypox**; varicella vaccine (live) prevents chickenpox.
- Chickenpox infectious **until all lesions crust**; **shingles = reactivation in one dermatome**; VZIG for high-risk exposure.
