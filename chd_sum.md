# CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE (Children)

## Epidemiology
- Most childhood heart disease is **congenital**. **8 per 1000** liveborn have significant cardiac malformation; **30% need intervention in 1st year**.
- Minor abnormalities (e.g. bicuspid aortic valve) up to 53/1000.
- 9 most common anomalies = 80% of lesions; 10–15% complex; 10–15% have non-cardiac abnormality.

## Atrial Septal Defect (ASD)
- Mostly sporadic. **Types**: ostium secundum (commonest, central septum/foramen ovale), ostium primum (= **partial AVSD**, with regurgitant left AV valve), sinus venosus.
- **Symptoms**: commonly **none**; recurrent chest infections/wheeze; **arrhythmias (4th decade+)**.
- **Signs**: loud S1, **wide & FIXED splitting of S2**, soft ejection systolic murmur (grade I–II); partial AVSD → apical pansystolic murmur (AV valve regurgitation).
- **Investigations**: CXR (cardiomegaly RA/RV, ↑pulmonary vascularity). **ECG: secundum = RBBB + right axis deviation; partial AVSD = SUPERIOR QRS axis** (negative in aVF). **Echo = mainstay**.
- **Treatment**: secundum → **catheter occlusion device** (surgery if large); partial AVSD/sinus venosus → surgery. Done **age 3–5 yrs** to prevent right HF + arrhythmias.

## Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD)
- **Most common CHD (30%)**. Types by position (membranous, perimembranous, inlet, muscular) and size (small/large).
- **Small VSD**: usually asymptomatic; **loud pansystolic murmur at lower left sternal edge (loud = SMALLER defect)**; quiet P2.
- **Large VSD**: dyspnoea, feeding difficulty, poor growth, sweating, recurrent chest infections, **HF in early infancy**; tachypnoea, hepatomegaly. Signs: active precordium, **soft pansystolic murmur or none (= LARGE defect)**, apical mid-diastolic murmur (↑mitral flow), **loud P2 (pulmonary hypertension)**. Cyanosis usually absent (unless PHT → Eisenmenger).
- **Investigations**: CXR (small = normal; large = cardiomegaly + pulmonary plethora/oedema). ECG (small = normal; large = **biventricular hypertrophy**). Echo = diagnostic.
- **Treatment**: small → reassurance + IE prophylaxis. Large → treat HF (**diuretics + ACE inhibitors**) + **surgical closure**.

## Patent Ductus Arteriosus (PDA)
- Ductus connects pulmonary artery → descending aorta; normally closes after birth. PDA = failed closure by **1 month** post-due-date. Common in **prematures**. **6–8% of CHD; F:M 2:1**.
- **Small PDA**: asymptomatic, normal heart size.
- **Large PDA**: symptomatic (HF, growth retardation), **bounding peripheral pulses, wide pulse pressure**, cardiomegaly, **machinery continuous murmur (max 2nd left ICS)**, systolic thrill.
- **Investigations**: CXR (large = LV/LA cardiomegaly, ↑pulmonary markings). ECG (large = LV/biventricular hypertrophy). Echo = main test.
- **Complications**: **infective endocarditis** (small + large); HF + emboli (large). Spontaneous closure after infancy extremely rare.
- **Treatment**: **transcatheter closure**, or surgical ligation/division (before 1st year). [Preterm: indomethacin/ibuprofen.]

## Transposition of Great Arteries (d-TGA)
- **5% of CHD**. Aorta arises from RV (anterior, right of PA); PA from LV → **2 parallel circulations**. **Survival depends on mixing** (patent foramen ovale + ductus + VSD). Half have VSD. **M:F 3:1; commonest CHD in infants of diabetic mothers**.
- **Clinical**: **cyanosis is predominant + always present** — life-threatening, presents **day 2 (ductal closure)**. Loud single S2; usually no murmur.
- **Investigations**: CXR = **"egg-shaped heart", narrow mediastinum**. ECG = normal neonatal RV dominance. Echo confirms.
- **Treatment**: **PGE (prostaglandin) infusion to keep ductus open**; **balloon atrial septostomy (Rashkind)**; **arterial switch (Jatene) = treatment of choice (first 2 weeks** — delay → ↓LV mass). Mustard operation; PA banding if VSD.

## Tetralogy of Fallot (TOF)
- **5–7% of CHD**. **4 components**: 1. **RV outflow obstruction (infundibular PS)** · 2. **VSD** · 3. **overriding aorta** (dextroposition + septal override) · 4. **RV hypertrophy**.
- **Clinical**: mild obstruction → HF, no cyanosis at birth; severe → **neonatal cyanosis (PDA-dependent)**. Older children: **cyanosis, clubbing, exertional dyspnoea, squatting, growth delay**.
- **Hypercyanotic ("tet") spells**: paroxysmal cyanosis, hyperpnoea, restless, gasping; common in **first 2 years**, mornings/after crying; **murmur DECREASES** during spell; prolonged → severe hypoxia/acidosis, rarely fatal.
- **Signs**: **loud harsh ejection systolic murmur at left sternal edge** (from day 1); murmur shortens + cyanosis increases as obstruction worsens; clubbing in older children.
- **Investigations**: CXR = **"boot-shaped" heart, oligaemic lungs, right-sided aortic arch (20–25%)**. ECG = **RVH, right axis, P pulmonale**. Echo diagnostic.
- **Treatment**: medical initially → **definitive surgery ~6 months** (close VSD + relieve RVOT obstruction). Very cyanosed neonate → **modified Blalock–Taussig shunt** (subclavian → PA). **Tet spells**: knee-chest position, **morphine, IV propranolol, fluids, bicarbonate, O2**; severe → paralysis + ventilation.

## High-yield one-liners
- **ASD = wide FIXED split S2; secundum → device closure; primum/AVSD = superior axis.**
- **VSD = commonest CHD; loud murmur = small defect; large → HF + loud P2 + surgery.**
- **PDA = machinery murmur + bounding pulses + wide pulse pressure; transcatheter closure; preterm = indomethacin.**
- **TGA = cyanosis day 2, egg-shaped heart; prostaglandin + balloon septostomy + arterial switch (first 2 wks). Diabetic mother.**
- **TOF = PS + VSD + overriding aorta + RVH; boot-shaped heart; tet spells (murmur decreases); BT shunt then repair ~6mo; spells = knee-chest + morphine + propranolol.**
- **Duct-dependent cyanotic lesions → prostaglandin keeps PDA open.**
