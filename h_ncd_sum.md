# Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs) & Lifestyle

## The Big Picture
NCDs = chronic, non-infectious, slow-progressing diseases that **kill 41 million people/year (74% of all deaths globally)**. The big four — **cardiovascular, cancer, chronic respiratory, diabetes** — cause >80% of premature NCD deaths and share **modifiable lifestyle risk factors**. Up to 80% of heart disease/stroke/T2DM and a third of cancers are **preventable**.

## Definition
- **Not transmitted person-to-person**, long duration, slow progression, often permanent with residual disability, non-reversible pathology, need long-term care/rehabilitation.
- **Acute** = short episode, return to normal; **chronic** = long, permanently incapacitating.

## Burden
- **41 million deaths/year (74% global)**; 17 million die before 70 ("premature"); **77–86% in low/middle-income countries**.
- **Top causes:** **cardiovascular 17.9M > cancer 9.3M > chronic respiratory 4.1M > diabetes 2.0M** (>80% of premature NCD deaths).

## Types
Cardiovascular, cancer, chronic respiratory (COPD, asthma), diabetes, obesity, mental/nervous diseases, musculoskeletal (arthritis), accident sequelae, blindness, senility.

## Risk Factors (shared, modifiable)
- **Tobacco use, unhealthy diet, physical inactivity, harmful alcohol** + air pollution.
- **Lifestyle** = behavioural patterns from psycho-social/environmental factors, built gradually over years → hard to change.
- Driven by **urbanisation, industrialisation, modernisation** (sedentary jobs, processed food, ads, consumerism).

## Prevention
- **Up to 80% of CVD/stroke/T2DM + >⅓ cancers preventable** by eliminating shared risks.
- Detection, screening, treatment, palliative care = key response components.
- Tackle unhealthy lifestyle: physical activity, healthy diet, no tobacco, limit alcohol, stress + personal safety.

## NCDs in Iraq (challenges)
- Epidemiological transition (infectious → chronic); rising prevalence (ageing, urbanisation, poor diet, **high smoking in men**).
- **Weak healthcare system** (conflict, underinvestment), drug/specialist shortages, low public awareness, financial burden, conflict/displacement, pollution/war toxins, ageing population.

## Solutions
Strengthen healthcare (train specialists, infrastructure), **public education + screening programs**, policy (tobacco control, taxes on unhealthy food, labelling), address social determinants, international collaboration.

## Walk-Away Points
- NCDs = **74% of global deaths**; big four = **CVD > cancer > respiratory > diabetes** (>80% premature deaths).
- Shared modifiable risks: **tobacco, diet, inactivity, alcohol** → up to **80% preventable**.
- Lifestyle disease driven by urbanisation/modernisation; change is slow + needs persistence.
- Response: **detection, screening, treatment, palliation** + policy + education.
