Why Back Pain and Sciatica Keep Coming Back

The drawer. Most people with chronic back pain and sciatica know it by heart. Everything in it has a story. None of them ended the way you hoped.

Here is what nobody explains: ibuprofen doesn’t fix back pain. It borrows against it. Every pill buys temporary quiet — and charges interest the moment you stop. The pain signal was never resolved. Stretching and physical therapy work on the mechanical structure — your muscles, posture, and spinal load. For some kinds of back pain, that is the right approach. But the pain that comes back every morning, that never fully quiets, that has been there every day for years — that is not a mechanical problem. It is a chemical one. You can do every stretch perfectly and still hurt because what is driving the pain is happening inside your nervous system, not in your hamstrings. CBD and turmeric target entirely different pathways. Glucosamine works on cartilage. You didn’t fail those treatments. They were aimed at the wrong problem.

“That shooting fire down your leg. The stiffness that takes twenty minutes to clear. The back pain that comes back the moment the pill wears off. All of it traces to one thing — and most doctors have never once explained it.”

Inside your nervous system there is a type of immune cell called a glial cell. When a nerve gets compressed — the way the sciatic nerve does in lower back and leg pain — glial cells activate and flood the area with inflammatory signals. Under normal circumstances, they switch back off once the threat is gone. After 50, something changes. They get stuck in the “on” position. The original compression may have resolved. But the cellular alarm keeps firing — constantly sending discomfort signals through the lower back and down the sciatic nerve, day after day, for years. That is why the shooting pain returns every time the medication wears off. That is why the mornings never fully clear. Not because your spine is collapsing. Because a cellular switch that should have turned itself off a long time ago is still running.
