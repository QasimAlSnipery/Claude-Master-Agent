# Viral Hepatitis (A–E)

## The Big Picture
Five hepatitis viruses, two transmission patterns: **A & E = faecal-oral (acute only, no chronicity)**; **B, C, D = blood/body fluids (can become chronic → cirrhosis/HCC)**. Viral hepatitis is the **2nd leading infectious cause of death** (1.3 million/yr, mostly **B 83% + C 17%**). Master the **serology**, who becomes chronic, and prevention (vaccine for A & B; cure for C).

## Overview Table
| Virus | Spread | Chronic? | Prevention |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Faecal-oral | No | Vaccine, hygiene |
| **B** | Blood/sexual/perinatal | **Yes** | Vaccine + Ig |
| **C** | Blood (percutaneous) | **Yes (70–85%)** | No vaccine; **curable** |
| **D** | Blood (needs HBV) | Yes | HBV vaccine |
| **E** | Faecal-oral | No | Safe water |
- All are **RNA except HBV (DNA)**.

## Hepatitis A
- Picornavirus; **incubation ~30 days**. **Jaundice rare in young (<10% in <6 yr), common in adults (70–80%)** — severity ↑ with age.
- No chronic sequelae; rarely fulminant. **Diagnosis: anti-HAV IgM (acute), IgG (past/immunity)**.
- Transmission: close contact, contaminated food/water (shellfish, infected food handlers). **Prevention: vaccine + immunoglobulin (post-exposure within 14 days)**; highest attack rate 5–14 yr.

## Hepatitis B
- DNA virus; **incubation 60–90 days**. **Chronicity is age-dependent: perinatal 30–90%, adults 2–10%** (younger = fewer symptoms but more chronic).
- Progression → **cirrhosis + hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC)**; ~887,000 deaths/yr.
- **Serology:** **HBsAg** = infection; **anti-HBs** = recovery/immunity/vaccine; **anti-HBc IgM** = acute; anti-HBc IgG = past/chronic; **HBeAg** = active replication/infectivity; **HBV-DNA** = replication (monitor therapy). **Chronic = HBsAg >6 months. Window period: HBsAg gone, anti-HBs not yet up, only anti-HBc.**
- **Fluids:** high in blood/serum/wound exudate; moderate semen/vaginal fluid; low saliva.
- Transmission: **sexual, parenteral (IVDU, healthcare), perinatal** (HBeAg+ mothers — main route in high-prevalence areas).
- **Treatment:** nucleoside/nucleotide analogues (**tenofovir, entecavir**, lamivudine), interferon; not curative.
- **Prevention:** **recombinant vaccine 3 doses (0, 1, 6 months), IM deltoid** (>90% protective, declines after 40); **HBIG** within 48 h post-exposure + to neonates of HBsAg/HBeAg+ mothers; blood screening, safe sex.

## Hepatitis C
- RNA; **incubation 6–7 weeks**. **Most become chronic (70–85%)** → cirrhosis/HCC. Jaundice only 30–40% acute; **no protective antibody**.
- **Diagnosis: anti-HCV** (takes ≥4 wk — not for acute), **HCV-RNA (PCR)** most reliable (acute + monitoring). **Genotype 1 most prevalent worldwide.**
- Transmission: **IV drug use, transfusion, haemodialysis, needlestick** (sexual less than HBV).
- **Treatment: direct-acting antivirals (sofosbuvir + ledipasvir etc.) — SVR >95% in 12 weeks = cure.** **No vaccine.**

## Hepatitis D (Delta)
- **Defective RNA virus — needs HBV (HBsAg) to replicate.**
- **Co-infection (HBV+HDV together):** severe acute, low chronicity. **Superinfection (HDV on chronic HBV):** high risk of severe chronic liver disease.
- Prevention = **HBV vaccine** (prevents co-infection).

## Hepatitis E
- RNA; faecal-oral, **contaminated water** outbreaks; incubation ~40 days. No chronicity in healthy (chronic in immunocompromised).
- **High mortality in pregnant women (15–25%)**. Diagnosis: anti-HEV IgM. Prevent via safe water.

## Clinical Course (general)
- **Prodromal/pre-icteric:** malaise, anorexia, nausea, distaste for cigarettes, RUQ pain (HBV/HCV may add arthralgia, rash, urticaria — serum sickness).
- **Icteric:** dark urine, jaundice, pale stool, tender hepatomegaly.
- **Resolution:** jaundice improves 3–6 weeks.
- **Investigations:** ↑ ALT (>AST) even before jaundice, ↑ bilirubin, ↑ ALP, normal albumin (long half-life), **prolonged PT = poor prognosis**, bilirubinuria early; serology for type.
- **Complications:** fulminant hepatitis, cholestatic (esp. A), chronic/cirrhosis/HCC (B, C, D).
- **Management: supportive** (no specific Rx for acute), rest, avoid hepatotoxic drugs + alcohol; watch for fulminant.
- Overall mortality ~0.5% (3% if >60; **HEV 20% in pregnancy**).

## Walk-Away Points
- **A & E faecal-oral, acute only; B, C, D bloodborne, chronic** → cirrhosis/HCC.
- **HBV chronicity is age-dependent** (perinatal worst); serology: HBsAg/anti-HBs/anti-HBc/HBeAg.
- **HCV usually chronic, no vaccine, but DAAs cure (SVR >95%)**.
- **HDV needs HBV**; **HEV deadly in pregnancy**.
- Vaccines: **A and B** (B = 0,1,6 IM deltoid); supportive care for acute; ALT>AST, PT = prognosis.
