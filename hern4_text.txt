Hernia
A hernia is a protrusion of a viscus or part of a viscus through an
abnormal opening in the walls of its containing cavity .
Composition of a hernia
1-The sac:
Its a diverticulum of peritoneum and is made up of three parts(the
mouth,the neck,the body and the fundus of the sac)
2-The covering of the sac:
Its derived from the layers of abdominal wall through the sac pass.
3-The content of the sac:
Can be empty or contain
omentum(omentocele),intestine(enterocele),potion of circumference
of intestine(Richters hernia),portion of bladder
,ovary,Meckels diverticulum(Litters hernia).

Aetiology
Hernia occure at sites of weakness in the wall. It can be:
*Congenital.
*Aquired.
Risk factors:
Any increase in the intra abdominal pressure like
*lifiting heavy weights.
*chronic cough.
*constipation.
*straning on micturation(BPH).
*pregnancy.
*obesity.
=====PAGE 4 [T]=====
All hernias are either:
1- Reducable: spontaneously or manually.
2-Irreducable: the content of the sac can not be reduced.
Irreducable hernia can be:
*Incarcerated:
There are adhesions between the sac and the contents but there is
no obstruction & no interference with the with blood supply of the
content.
*Obstructed:
A hollow viscus is trapped with in the sac and obstruction occurs.
The blood supply remains intact. This is a common cause of small
bowel obstruction.
*Strangulated:
The arterial blood supply to the content of the sac i compromised. In
such a hernia unless surgical releif is undertaken the contents of the
sac will become gangrenous.

Femoral hernia
The defect is in the transversalis fascia overlying the femoral ring at the
entry to the femoral canal.
The hernia passes through the femoral canal and present in the groin
below and lateral to the pubic tubercle.
Its more common in females.
The femoral sheath is a condensation of the deep fascia (fascia lata)
of the thigh and contains, from lateral to medial, the femoral artery,
femoral vein, and femoral canal. The femoral canal is a space medial
to the vein that allows for venous expansion and contains a lymph
node (node of Cloquet). Other features of the femoral triangle include
the femoral nerve, which lies lateral to the sheath.
Boundaries of The Femoral ring:
*anterior:Inguinal ligament
*posterior: iliopsoas, pectineal, and long adductor muscles (floor).
*Medial : lacunar ligament
*Lateral : femoral vessle(vein)

Managment and repair of the hernia:
Treatment of hernia depends whether it is reducable or irreducable
and possibly strangulated.
*Reducable: surgery.(mostly but not always)
*Irreducable: All acutely irreducible hernias need emergency
treatment because of the risk of strangulation.An attempt to push the
hernia back can be made.
If strangulation suspected the hernia should not be reduced.
Surgery:
INDIRECT:(herniotomy)
The sac is dissected free from the cord structures and creamsteric fibers.
Sac should be open away from any herniated contents. Contents are then
reduced, and the sac is ligated deep to inguinal r.ing with an absorbable
suture. In infants and children only herniotomy done.
DIRECT:
Too broadly based for ligation and should not be opened, simple freed
from transversalis fibers and inverted.

Herniorraphy(Inguinal floor reconstruction)
Some method of reconstruction of the inguinal floor is necessary in all
adult hernia repairs(both direct &indirect)to prevent recurrence.
Herniorraphy(Inguinal floor reconstruction)
1.Primary tissue repair:
*Bassini repair: inferior arch of transversalis fascia (TF) or conjoint
tendon is approximated to shelving portion of inguinal ligament.
*McVay: TF is sutured to cooper ligament.
*Shouldice: TF is incised and reapproximated.
2.Open tension free repair: (mesh repair)
*Lichtenstein repair &Patch and Plug technique: Mesh is used to
reconstruct inguinal floor.
*Mesh plug technique : place mesh in the hernial defect
3.Laproscopic & preperitoneal repair:
TAPP (transabdominal prepeitoneal procedure).
TEP (Total extraperitoneal repair.

Femoral hernias should be repaired very soon after the
diagnosis has been made because of the high risk of
strangulation.
