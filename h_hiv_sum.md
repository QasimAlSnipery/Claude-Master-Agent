# Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV)

## The Big Picture
HIV = an **RNA retrovirus** that infects **CD4 cells**, progressively destroying cellular immunity until **AIDS** (opportunistic infections/cancers). Zoonotic origin (chimps → HIV-1). Diagnosed by **antibody + p24 antigen** (PCR in infants/early). Monitored by **CD4 count + viral load**. Treated with lifelong **ART** — goal **undetectable = untransmittable**.

## Virology
- **Retrovirus, lentivirus family**, enveloped RNA, ~100 nm. **HIV-1** (global pandemic) from chimpanzees; **HIV-2** (West Africa, slower, less transmissible) from sooty mangabey. Group M, subtype C (sub-Saharan Africa/India) = half of infections.
- **Infects CD4 cells:** T-helper lymphocytes, monocyte-macrophages, dendritic, microglia. **gp120 binds CD4** + co-receptor **CCR5 or CXCR4**.
- **CCR5 delta-32 homozygotes are immune.**
- **Reverse transcriptase** makes DNA → **integrase** inserts as **proviral DNA** (lifelong); infected CD4 cells survive ~1 day.

## Epidemiology
- First recognised 1981 (Pneumocystis pneumonia in gay men); named HIV 1986.
- 2024: ~**40.8 million living with HIV**; **53% women/girls**; 1.3 million new infections (down 40% since 2010).

## Transmission
- **Sexual** (commonest globally), **blood** (transfusion, IV drug sharing, needlestick), **mother-to-child** (in utero/perinatal/breastfeeding).
- **Risk per exposure:** blood transfusion **90%**, vaginal delivery **15%**, receptive anal **1.4%**, needlestick **0.3%**, vaginal sex 0.05–0.1%, breastfeeding 0.5%/month.
- ↑ risk: high viral load, genital ulcers/STIs, uncircumcised partner, deep injury/visible blood.

## Diagnosis
- **Antibody tests (ELISA/rapid)** — two positive immunoassays confirm. **p24 antigen** detects earlier (before antibody). **PCR (HIV RNA)** = most sensitive; used for **infants** (maternal antibody persists ≤15 months) and primary infection.
- Trend = **universal testing**.

## Monitoring
- **CD4 count** — immune-suppression marker (normal >500). **200–500** = low opportunistic risk; **<200 = severe immunosuppression, AIDS-defining risk**.
- **Viral load** — quantitative HIV RNA PCR; guides ART response; **undetectable = no sexual transmission**. >100,000 copies = fast CD4 decline.

## Clinical Stages (WHO)
- **Primary HIV** (2–4 wk post-exposure) — mononucleosis-like: fever, **maculopapular rash + mucosal ulceration** (suggests HIV over EBV), pharyngitis, lymphadenopathy. Diagnose by **RNA/p24** (antibodies appear 2–12 wk later).
- **Stage 1** — asymptomatic / persistent generalised lymphadenopathy.
- **Stage 2** — moderate weight loss, recurrent URTI, herpes zoster, fungal nails.
- **Stage 3** — severe weight loss, chronic diarrhoea/fever >1 mo, **oral candidiasis, oral hairy leukoplakia, pulmonary TB**.
- **Stage 4 (AIDS)** — oesophageal candidiasis, **Pneumocystis pneumonia, Kaposi's sarcoma, cerebral toxoplasmosis, cryptococcosis, extrapulmonary TB, HIV wasting/encephalopathy**. Staging never reverts.

## Treatment (ART)
- **Combination (usually 3 drugs)**, daily, **for everyone, start ASAP**. Cannot cure but → undetectable viral load, longer life, less transmission.
- **Classes:** NNRTIs (efavirenz), NRTIs (tenofovir, emtricitabine, lamivudine, zidovudine), **protease inhibitors** (darunavir), **integrase inhibitors** (dolutegravir, bictegravir), entry/fusion inhibitors (maraviroc).

## Prevention
- Condoms/fewer partners, clean needles, treat mother early + avoid breastfeeding (perinatal).
- **PEP** — within **72 h** of exposure (tenofovir + emtricitabine + PI/integrase inhibitor).
- **PrEP** — daily tenofovir + emtricitabine for ongoing high-risk; regular HIV testing.

## Walk-Away Points
- HIV = retrovirus infecting **CD4 via gp120 + CCR5/CXCR4**; reverse transcriptase + integrase = lifelong proviral DNA.
- Diagnose **antibody + p24** (PCR for infants/early); monitor **CD4 (<200 = AIDS risk) + viral load**.
- **AIDS = stage 4** opportunistic diseases (PCP, Kaposi, toxoplasmosis, TB).
- **ART for all, undetectable = untransmittable**; **PEP <72 h, PrEP** for high risk.
