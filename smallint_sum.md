# THE SMALL INTESTINE (Surgery)

## Anatomy & physiology
- **Functions**: nutrient digestion/absorption (carbs, protein, fat, **B12**, minerals); GALT (immune); endocrine (**secretin, somatostatin, CCK, motilin, peptide YY, GLP, neurotensin**); barrier (brush border, tight junctions, **defensins**); enterohepatic bile circulation; intestinal adaptation.
- Landmarks: DJ flexure (**ligament of Treitz**), ileocaecal valve, **plicae circulares (valvulae conniventes)**, **Peyer's patches**.
- **Motility**: intrinsic (**Cajal cells = pacemaker**) + external neuro-hormonal.

## Diverticular disease

### Meckel's diverticulum
- **Most prevalent congenital GI anomaly.** **"Rule of 2s"**: 2% population, ~2 inches (3–5cm), ~2 feet (60cm) from ileocaecal valve, M:F equal, often <2yrs. **60% heterotopic mucosa (60% gastric)**. From persistent **vitelline (omphalomesenteric) duct** (8th week).
- **Presentation (4–6% complications)**: asymptomatic; **GI bleeding (children, painless, maroon stool)**; diverticulitis (mimics appendicitis); peptic ulceration; obstruction (intussusception, volvulus, band); **Littre's hernia** (in inguinal/femoral hernia); carcinoid.
- **Diagnosis**: incidental, barium, **Tc-99m scan (if gastric mucosa)**. **Treatment: diverticulectomy if symptomatic** (asymptomatic = controversial).

### Duodenal diverticulum
- Mostly **periampullary (juxtapapillary)**; ↑with age, rare <40; acquired. Causes **cholangitis, choledocholithiasis, chronic pancreatitis, Oddi dysfunction**; problem during ERCP. Treat: asymptomatic → leave; bleeding → surgery.

### Jejunal diverticulum
- Multiple; **connective tissue disease**. **Malabsorption, anaemia, steatorrhoea, B12 deficiency**; acute abdomen if inflamed.

## Blind loop syndrome
- Stasis → abnormal bacterial flora → **malabsorption (fat + B12), anaemia, steatorrhoea, weight loss**. Causes: long afferent loop (Polya gastrectomy), jejunal diverticula, stricture.

## Inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn's focus)
- UC, **Crohn's (regional enteritis)**, indeterminate (15%).
- **Etiology**: unknown; geography, smoking (↑Crohn's), OCP, family history 10–30%, autoimmune + mucosal barrier defect, ?Mycobacterium paratuberculosis.
- **Extra-intestinal**: **sclerosing cholangitis (40–60%)**, anaemia, DVT, **arthropathy (sacroiliitis, ankylosing spondylitis)**, ocular (iritis, uveitis, episcleritis), skin (**erythema nodosum, pyoderma gangrenosum**), finger clubbing, renal stones.

### Crohn's disease
- **F>M; age 15–30 + 55–60**; mouth→anus, **terminal ileum 60%**, rectal sparing 40%. **Smoking ↑ risk 3×**.
- **Pathology**: **non-caseating granulomatous, transmural**; deep serpiginous ulcers, **skip lesions + rectal sparing**, **cobblestone**, adhesions/fibrosis/stricture/fistula, mesenteric thickening.
- **Clinical**: acute (mimics appendicitis, diarrhoea precedes pain); chronic (mild diarrhoea, RIF pain + mass, **food fear, weight loss**); perianal disease (abscess, fistula); growth retardation in children.
- **Investigations**: sigmoidoscopy/colonoscopy (aphthous ulcers, skip), **barium = "string sign of Kantor"**, CT (fistula/abscess), **MRI (perianal)**.
- **Treatment**: medical (**steroid + 5-ASA**, antibiotics if abscess, **azathioprine = steroid-sparing**, nutrition). **Surgery doesn't cure — for complications only**: obstruction (35%), bleeding, perforation, failed medical, fistula/abscess (30–38%), fulminant colitis, malignancy, perianal. **Stricturoplasty**; segmental/ileocaecal resection.

## Intestinal infections
- **Amoebiasis (Entamoeba histolytica)**: **bottlenecked ulcer (lower sigmoid/upper rectum)**; dysentery, amoebic mass (mimics cancer). **Metronidazole + diloxanide furoate.**
- **Typhoid/paratyphoid**: lower ileum ulcer (longitudinal); complications = ileus, **haemorrhage, perforation**.
- **Intestinal TB**: ileum + proximal colon. **Ulcerative** (secondary to pulmonary TB, transverse ulcers) vs **hyperplastic** (ileocaecal, narrowing — unlike Crohn's rarely fistula). Barium = filling defect terminal ileum. **Chemotherapy**; surgery if obstruction.
- **Actinomycosis**: after appendectomy; **sulphur granules**; penicillin.

## Other
- **Pneumatosis cystoides intestinalis**: gas cysts in bowel wall; 15% idiopathic, 85% associated (COPD, asthma). Treat: high O2, metronidazole.
- **Traumatic rupture**: blunt/penetrating; laparotomy; small clean → primary closure, damaged → resection.
- **Small bowel perforation**: iatrogenic (ERCP) commonest; TB, typhoid, Crohn's, ischaemia, neoplasm, NSAIDs.

## Intestinal fistula
- Abnormal communication between epithelialized surfaces. Internal vs external (enterocutaneous). **Low <200 mL/day, high >500 mL/day.** **>80% iatrogenic** (anastomotic dehiscence).
- **90% close spontaneously in 5 weeks** (10–15% mortality from sepsis).
- **FRIEND (factors preventing closure)**: **F**oreign body, **R**adiation, **I**nflammation (IBD), **E**pithelialization, **N**eoplasm, **D**istal obstruction.
- **Therapy**: stabilization (IVF, nutrition, antibiotics, skin protection, drainage, somatostatin) → investigate → definitive surgery (2–3 months).

## Small bowel tumours
- **Rare** (only 1.1–2.4% of GI malignancies despite 90% of mucosa) — dilution of carcinogens, rapid transit, low bacteria, IgA, apoptosis. More common in duodenum.
- **Benign**: **adenoma (commonest)**, lipoma, GIST. Symptomatic → obstruction, bleeding, intussusception.
- **Peutz-Jeghers syndrome**: familial **hamartomatous polyposis (jejunum)** + **mucocutaneous melanosis (lips/oral)**; bleeding, intussusception; rarely malignant.
- **Malignant**:
  - **Adenocarcinoma** (35–50% of small bowel cancer): 5th–6th decade; risk = Crohn's, coeliac, Peutz-Jeghers, FAP, HNPCC. Treat: resection.
  - **Carcinoid** (20–40%): from enterochromaffin cells (appendix, ileum); produces **serotonin → 5-HIAA in urine**. **Carcinoid crisis** (only with metastases): flushing, diarrhoea, hypotension, **right heart valve fibrosis (pulmonary/tricuspid)**. Treat: resection + lymphadenectomy if >2cm; **somatostatin** for symptoms.
  - **Lymphoma** (10–15%): Western (B-cell), **Mediterranean (alpha-chain)**, coeliac-associated (T-cell).
  - **GIST**: **KIT (CD117) mutation**, from Cajal cells; mostly stomach; resection; **imatinib (tyrosine kinase inhibitor)** for metastatic/unresectable.

## High-yield one-liners
- **Meckel's = rule of 2s, 60% gastric mucosa, Tc-99m scan, painless GI bleed in children; Littre's hernia.**
- **Crohn's = transmural non-caseating granuloma, skip lesions, cobblestone, string sign; surgery for complications only; FRIEND for fistula.**
- **Amoebiasis = bottlenecked ulcer + metronidazole; TB = ulcerative vs hyperplastic.**
- **Small bowel tumours rare; adenoma commonest benign; Peutz-Jeghers = hamartoma + mucocutaneous melanosis.**
- **Carcinoid = serotonin/5-HIAA, crisis with metastases (right heart fibrosis); GIST = KIT + imatinib.**
