# Vector-Borne Infections

## The Big Picture
Vectors = living organisms (mostly bloodsucking insects) that carry pathogens between hosts. They cause **>17% of all infectious diseases and >700,000 deaths/year**. Know the **vector → disease** pairings, the two transmission modes, and the control/outbreak framework. Malaria (parasite, Anopheles) and dengue (virus, Aedes) are the headline diseases.

## Transmission Modes
- **Biological** (most important) — arthropod ingests pathogen in a blood meal, pathogen **multiplies inside the vector**, then transmitted at next bite (e.g. malaria, dengue).
- **Mechanical** — vector physically carries pathogen on body/gut (no multiplication) — **housefly, cockroach** contaminating food.

## Key Vector → Disease Table (memorise)
- **Anopheles mosquito** → Malaria (Plasmodium). **Aedes** → Dengue, Yellow fever, Chikungunya, Zika. **Culex** → Encephalitis/West Nile.
- **Sandfly (phlebotomus)** → Leishmania. **Tsetse/Triatomine** → Chagas (Trypanosoma cruzi).
- **Rodent fleas** → Plague (Yersinia pestis). **Lice/fleas** → Typhus (Rickettsia). **Dog tick** → Rocky Mountain fever.
- **Housefly** → cholera, dysentery, typhoid (food contamination). **Snail** → Schistosomiasis (skin penetration).

## Yellow Fever
- Acute **viral haemorrhagic** disease (Aedes); "yellow" = jaundice from haemolysis; severe in a few, ~half of those die in 7–10 days; endemic tropical Africa + Central/South America.
- **Prevention:** **single-dose vaccine = lifelong immunity** (no booster); not <9 months old; effective in 10 days (80–100%). + vector control (remove standing water, larvicide).

## Malaria
- 2022: ~**249 million cases, 608,000 deaths**; **94% in WHO African Region**; **children <5 = ~80% of deaths**.
- **4 species:** P. **vivax**, P. **falciparum** (deadliest), P. malariae, P. ovale.
- **Diagnosis:** **blood film (thick + thin)** — gold standard; serology; **RDT** (rapid test).
- **Chemoprophylaxis** — for travellers from non-endemic areas, soldiers/labour in endemic zones (short-term, side effects).
- **Vaccines:** **RTS,S/AS01** (4 doses, efficacy 25–50%) and **R21** — both for resident children, high public-health impact.

## Communicable Disease Control Program (4 steps)
1. **Recognition** — diagnose + confirm (clinical + lab).
2. **Notification** — report to local/international authorities. Limited by concealment (stigma/fear of isolation), misdiagnosis, incomplete reporting → overcome with skill, case definitions, simple forms.
3. **Find the source** — travel history, time sequence, incubation period.
4. **Find the extent** — how common/serious in community.

## Outbreak Management
- **Aims:** ↓ primary cases, ↓ secondary cases + mortality, reduce morbidity, prevent recurrence (fix the cause).
- **Steps:** prepare outbreak plan (what/where/when), call **Outbreak Control Meeting** (confirm outbreak, agree case definition, decide investigation + control + who to inform).

## Walk-Away Points
- **Biological** transmission = pathogen multiplies in vector (the important one).
- Memorise vector pairs: **Anopheles–malaria, Aedes–dengue/yellow fever, sandfly–leishmania, rodent flea–plague**.
- **Yellow fever vaccine = single dose, lifelong**; malaria diagnosis = **blood film**.
- Control program: **recognise → notify → source → extent**; outbreak = plan + control meeting.
