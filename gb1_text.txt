Embryology
-The liver primordium arise at 3rd week from distal end of foregut
called hepatic diverticulum.
-The connection between hepatic diverticulum and foregut
( duodenum) narrows and forming bile duct (choledochus)
-From the bile duct ,a small ventral bud outgrowth formed & this
gives rise to cystic duct and gal bladder.
-In early fetal life GB is totally intrahepatic
-Initially GB is hollow organ but as a result of its epithelial
proliferation it become temporarily solid, later by vacuolization
of its epithelial cords form definitive lumen.

Sphincter of Oddi
-Complex structure of thick coat of circular smooth muscle surround
common duct at ampulla of Vater.
-Is 4-6mm in length, basal resting pressure 13 mmHg above duodenal p.
-Its function:-
1-Regulate bile flow & pancreatic flow to duodenum
2-Prevent regurgitation of duodenal content to BD
3-Divert bile to gall bladder.
-Has phasic contraction 4/mint.
-Relaxation occur when CCK enzyme increase so phasic contraction
decrease & decrease basal pressure so bile flow to duodenum.

-500-1000 ml/day
-Liver produce +/- 40 ml /hour
-Composed of 97% water, electrolytes as plasma, proteins, lipid, bile
salts 1-2%, pigments 1%
-Color of the bile is duo to bilirubin diglucuronide from Hb, which is
10 x more concentration than in plasma, this enter intestine and by
bacteria converted to Urobilinogen.
-Bile secretion is under control:-
1-Neurogenic ( vagus +, splanchnic N. -) .Antral distention cause GB
contraction & sphincter of Oddi relaxation. So, by vagotomy ,
decrease CCK response on GB , so GB size ,volume increases.

Enterohepatic circulation of the bile
-Primary bile salts ( cholate & deoxy cholate) synthesized in liver from
cholesterol, conjugated with Taurine & Glycerin, pass to duod. then
terminal ileum.
-In terminal ileum 80% reabsorbed to liver, 20% deconjugated
(dehydroxylated )by gut bacteria forming secondary bile acids
( deoxycholate& lithochelate), they absorbed in colon to liver, then
conjugated again and pass to bile.
-Cholehepatic shunt
Hepatocytes produce bile pass to canaliculi , later via
cholangiocytes return to hepatocytes instead to pass
to small intestine.

-1/4000,male=female live birth
-BD at birth destroyed by inflammation which cause intra-hepatic BD
dilatation, later on liver cirrhosis & portal hypertension
-Associated anomalies in 20% include cardiac lesions, situs inversus,

-If the child untreated die before age 3 years because of hemorrhage

Type 2- atresia of CHD

Gall stones (GS) diseases ( Cholelithiasis)
Prevalence& incidence:-
-Common disease of GIT, commonest surgery in westerns countries.
-Rare in Africa & south India
-F > M 3:1 , but in old age F = M
-1st relatives has 2X risk
-Gall stones without symptoms diagnosed incidentally by US, CT or
laparotomy.
-Saints triad( gal stone, hiatus hernia, diverticulosis of colon)
-Asymptomatic pts. with gal stones:
3% /year become symptomatic & this become recurrent.
-So, 1/3 of asymptomatic patients in 20 years remain symptoms free.
-By autopsy 11-36% has gal stones.
-Complicated gal stones 3-5% / year occur.

Acute cholecystitis
Pathogenesis:-
-Duo to gall stones in 90-95% & occur with other acute systemic
disease.
- <1% duo to tumor obstructing cystic duct.
-Obstruction of the cystic duct by stone is initial stage, but why
inflammation occur it is unknown.
-Inflammatory process occur by mucosal toxins lysolecithin ( product
of lecithin) & bile salts & platelet activating factors.
-Cause thick wall, reddish, sub-serosal hemorrhage & peri
cholecystic fluid.
-5-10% ischemia & necrosis occur.
-Most frequently stone dislodged & inflammatory resolves.
