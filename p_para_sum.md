# The Parathyroid Glands (Dr Sarmad)

## The Big Picture
Four small glands from the **3rd & 4th pharyngeal pouches**; **PTH** is the master calcium regulator (half-life 3–5 min). Low Ca / high phosphate → ↑PTH. Learn the **hypo- vs hyperparathyroid** pictures, the **"bones, stones, groans, thrones, psychiatric overtones"** mnemonic, the **3 types of hyperparathyroidism**, **MEN syndromes**, and localisation studies.

## Anatomy & Physiology
- Develop from **3rd & 4th pharyngeal pouches**.
- **Chief cells** sense low ionised Ca / high phosphate → secrete **PTH**.
- **PTH** raises Ca (bone resorption, renal Ca reabsorption, activates vit D), lowers phosphate; half-life **3–5 min**.

## Hypoparathyroidism
- **Causes:** **surgical** (thyroidectomy/parathyroidectomy), autoimmune, **genetic (DiGeorge — failed 3rd/4th pouch)**, pseudohypoparathyroidism (Albright — end-organ resistance, normal/high PTH).
- **Features (hypocalcaemia):** involuntary muscle contraction, **tetany, Chvostek's sign** (facial twitch on tapping facial nerve), **Trousseau's sign** (carpopedal spasm with BP cuff).
- Blood: **↓Ca, ↓PTH, ↑phosphate**.

## Hyperparathyroidism
- **Mnemonic:** **Bones** (pain), **Stones** (renal calcium stones), **Thrones** (constipation), **Abdominal groans**, **Psychiatric overtones** (depression). + muscle weakness.

### Types
- **Primary (PHPT)** — **adenoma commonest (>80%)**. ↑Ca, ↓phosphate, ↑PTH.
- **Secondary (SHPT)** — **CKD / low vitamin D** → ↓Ca, ↑phosphate, **↑PTH** (compensatory).
- **Tertiary (THPT)** — end-stage kidney disease; **autonomous hyperplasia** → ↑Ca, ↑PTH.

### MEN Syndromes
- **MEN 1 (PPP):** **P**ituitary + **P**ancreatic (gastrinoma) + **P**arathyroid adenoma.
- **MEN 2A (PPM):** **P**arathyroid hyperplasia + **P**heochromocytoma + **M**edullary thyroid cancer.
- **MEN 2B:** Pheochromocytoma + Medullary thyroid cancer + **mucosal neuromas/ganglioneuromas** (no parathyroid).

## Bone & Other Effects
- **Osteitis fibrosa cystica** — **brown tumours**, subperiosteal resorption, distal clavicle tapering, **"salt and pepper" skull**.
- **Renal stones >80%**, neuromuscular dysfunction, muscle weakness.

## Localisation (before minimally-invasive surgery)
- **Sestamibi scan** — most accurate/reliable.
- **Ultrasound** — cheap, non-invasive, operator-dependent.
- **4D-CT** — anatomical + functional (rapid uptake/washout vs nodes).
- Invasive (reoperation only): FNA + PTH assay, selective venous sampling.

## Walk-Away Points
- **PTH raises calcium**; hypoparathyroid → **tetany, Chvostek, Trousseau** (↓Ca, ↓PTH, ↑PO₄).
- Hyperparathyroid = **bones, stones, groans, thrones, psychiatric overtones**.
- **Primary = adenoma (>80%)**; secondary = CKD/low vit D; tertiary = autonomous in ESRD.
- **MEN 1 = PPP, MEN 2A = PPM**; **Sestamibi** = best localiser.
