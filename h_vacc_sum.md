# Vaccines & the Expanded Programme of Immunization (EPI)

## The Big Picture
Immunity = tolerate "self," eliminate "non-self." Vaccines train that response. Master the **live vs inactivated** split (and which is which), the **EPI** (WHO 1974, 6 diseases), the **Iraqi schedule**, the **tetanus toxoid schedule for women**, and key **adverse events/contraindications**.

## Vaccine Types
- **Live attenuated** — weakened organism, **must replicate**, response ≈ natural infection, usually **one dose**. *Disadvantages:* severe reaction in **immunocompromised**, antibody interference, fragile (cold chain).
  - Viral: **measles, mumps, rubella, varicella, yellow fever, rotavirus, oral polio (OPV)**. Bacterial: **BCG, oral typhoid**.
- **Inactivated** — can't replicate, safer, but **multiple doses + boosters**, mostly humoral.
  - Whole: IPV (polio), hepatitis A, rabies; pertussis, cholera. **Toxoid: diphtheria, tetanus.** Subunit: hepatitis B, HPV, influenza. Polysaccharide/conjugate: pneumococcal, Hib.
- **Rule:** the more similar to the disease organism, the better the response.

## EPI
- **WHO launched 1974**, originally **6 diseases: diphtheria, pertussis, tetanus, polio, TB, measles**.
- "**Expanded**" = more diseases + more children + wider coverage.
- **Targets:** under-5 children, women of childbearing age (15–49), special groups.

## Iraqi Schedule (current)
- **0–1 wk:** OPV0, HepB1, **BCG**.
- **2 mo:** OPV1, **PENTA** (DPT+HepB+Hib), Rota1, PCV1.
- **4 mo:** OPV2, PENTA, Rota2, IPV1, PCV2.
- **6 mo:** OPV3, PENTA, IPV2, PCV3, Rota3.
- **9 mo:** Measles1 + **Vit A 100,000 IU**.
- **12 mo:** MMR1.
- **18 mo:** DPT + OPV 1st booster, **Vit A 200,000 IU**, MMR2.
- **4–6 yr:** DPT + OPV 2nd booster.

## Tetanus Toxoid (women 15–49)
- 5 doses for lifelong protection: 1st (no protection), 2nd after 1 month (covers ~3 yr), 3rd after 6 months (5 yr), 4th after 1 year (10 yr), **5th after 1 year (lifelong)**.

## Adverse Events (high-yield)
- **Pertussis** → **persistent inconsolable cry**, unusual screams, convulsion, hypotonic-hyporesponsive episode.
- **OPV** → **VAPP** (vaccine-associated paralytic polio).
- **Measles** → rarely Guillain-Barré, **SSPE**, thrombocytopenia.
- **BCG** → lymphadenitis, osteitis. **Most serious contraindication = anaphylaxis to prior dose.**

## Other Vaccines
- **Hib** — polysaccharide **conjugate**; T-cell dependent, effective <18 mo; 2,4,6 mo + booster; **never before 6 weeks**; high-risk adults (asplenia, immunodeficiency, HIV).
- **Pneumococcal** — leading cause of bacterial meningitis/pneumonia in children. **PCV** (conjugate) for <2 yr (2,4,6 mo + booster); **PPSV23** for >65/chronic illness. **PCV13** = 13 serotypes.
- **Influenza** — TIV/QIV (inactivated) or **LAIV (live, intranasal)**; **annual** (strains change); priority: <5, >65, pregnant, HCW, chronic disease; **not <6 months**; first-timers 6 mo–8 yr get **2 doses 4 weeks apart**. **Egg allergy no longer a contraindication.**

## Pregnancy & Deferral
- **Inactivated influenza safe in pregnancy**; live vaccines (MMR, varicella) avoided.
- Defer for **serious acute febrile illness**; mild illness / antibiotics → vaccinate.

## Walk-Away Points
- **Live = MMR, varicella, yellow fever, rotavirus, OPV, BCG**; toxoid = tetanus/diphtheria.
- **EPI 1974, 6 diseases**; Iraqi schedule: **BCG/OPV0/HepB at birth, measles 9 mo, MMR 12 mo**.
- **Pertussis → inconsolable cry; OPV → VAPP; measles → SSPE**; anaphylaxis = main contraindication.
- Influenza **annual, not <6 mo**, egg allergy OK now.
