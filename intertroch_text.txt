Intertrochanteric Fractures
• Older patients than intracapsular fractures,
osteoporotic women, female : mail ratio 6:1.
• They unite easily, rarely cause Osteonecrosis .
• Risk factors: Same as intracapsular
Mechanism of injury:
• Fall on the greater trochanter.
• Indirect twisting injury.
• Pathological fractures.
• In young high energy trauma.

Clinical Features
• Old female, after fall she is unable to stand.
• The leg is short and externally rotated.
• Examine for concomitant medical diseases and
possible cause of pathological fracture.

Plain Radiography
❖ Kyle classification:
1. Type I: Undisplaced, uncomminuted.
2. Type II: Displaced with comminution of lesser trochanter.
3. Type III: Displaced with comminution of lesser and greater
trochanter.
4. Type IV: Sever comminution with subtrochanteric extension, also
reverse oblique.

CT and MRI
• Clinical evidence with normal plain radiograph.
• Isolated greater trochanter fracture.
• Pathological fracture.

Treatment
Always surgery:
1. Early mobilization of the patient to avoid
fracture complications (local and systemic).
2. To get the best position in AP
and Lateral planes because
malunion is very common.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 8
=====PAGE 9 [T]=====
Surgical Treatment
Closed reduction under fluoroscopy control and
internal fixation by:
•
Dynamic hip screw (stable fractures).
• Proximal femoral nail (unstable fractures, reverse oblique,
subtrochanteric extension).
• Total hip replacement (severe comminution, OA hip, failed internal
fixation)
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 9
=====PAGE 10 [T]=====
Complications:
1. General (DVT, PE, pneumonia, bed sores).
2. Failed fixation.
3. Malunion (Varus).
4. Non union.
Prognosis (25-30% mortality in the first year):
✧ Male>female.
✧ Inter trochanteric> intracapsular fracture.
✧ Operative delay >2 days.
✧ Age>80years.
✧ Two or more pre existing medical disease.

Fracture Shaft Femur
Strong muscles make displacement severe and reduction
difficult.
Mechanism of injury:
• High energy trauma: young adults,
associated with life threatening conditions (Motor vehicle
accidents, RTA, FFH).
• Low energy trauma: Elderly shaft fractures,
fall when standings, pathological fractures.
• Gun shot injuries.
• Children's may be victims of child abuse.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 11

Fracture Types
• Spiral fracture:indirect injury (FFH).
• Transverse and oblique: angulatory or direct
force (RTA, MVA).
• Comminuted: combined forces.
• Segmental.

Displacement
(cid:0) Proximal and mid shaft fractures: proximal shaft
flexes and abduct (iliopsoas, gluteus medius and
minimus).
(cid:0) Distal fractures: proximal shaft adduct (adductors),
distal fragment in extension (gastrocnemius).
1-2 liters of blood losses if closed, open fracture more .

Clinical Features
❖ ATLS should be initiated.
❖ General examination (shock, Other injuries).
❖ Pain, Swelling ,deformity, shortening and ecchymosis.
❖ Examine the neurovascular system, hip and pelvis.
IMAGINGS
• Plain radiograph:
Two views, Two joints, Two sites
(site, type, comminution, displacement,…..).
• CT: Concomitant fractures:
(femoral neck, acetabulum,
tibial platue)

=====PAGE 21 [T]=====
Complications
• Early:
a- Shock
(Hypovolemic, Neurogenic, Septic).
b- Fat embolism and ARDS
(bilateral, multiple fractures).
c- Thromboembolism.
d- Infection (Open, Plate fixation).
• Late:
a) Delay union and non union(100days): <10%.
b) Malunion(>15 ْangulations, shortening> 2cm).
c) Joint stiffness.
d) Refracture and implant failure.
