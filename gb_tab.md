# GALL BLADDER & BILIARY TREE — Clinical Revision Table
Bold = high-yield. Dr Mezjda Rashaan.

## ANATOMY & INVESTIGATIONS
| Item | Key points |
|---|---|
| GB | 7–12cm, 30–50mL; **Hartmann's pouch**; **no submucosa/muscularis mucosa**; crypts of Luschka |
| Cystic duct / artery | **Valves of Heister**; cystic artery 90% from RHA in **Calot's triangle**; **Moynihan's hump** danger |
| CBD / Oddi | Opens at **ampulla of Vater** via sphincter of Oddi (relaxes with **CCK**) |
| Investigations | **US = standard** (stones/jaundice); **MRCP = gold standard biliary tree/CBD**; **ERCP = therapeutic**; HIDA (acute cholecystitis/leak); CT for malignancy |

## GALLSTONES
| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| Risk | **5 F's (Fat, Female, Forty, Fertile, Family)**; F:M 3:1; haemolysis, ileal disease |
| Types | **Cholesterol** (west, 80%, large/solitary, radiolucent); **Pigment black** (haemolysis/cirrhosis, in GB); **pigment brown** (infection/parasites, in ducts, Asia) |
| Effects | GB: cholecystitis, mucocele, empyema, gangrene, carcinoma. Ducts: jaundice, cholangitis, pancreatitis. Bowel: **gallstone ileus** |

## CHOLECYSTITIS &amp; CHOLANGITIS
| Condition | Features | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| **Acute cholecystitis** | Gallstone-blocked cystic duct; persistent RUQ pain, fever, **Murphy's +ve**, ↑WBC; **US 95%** | Conservative (IVF/antibiotics) → **90% respond → interval cholecystectomy**; non-response/gangrene → surgery |
| **Chronic cholecystitis (biliary colic)** | Pain 1–5h after fatty meal; normal WBC/LFT; US stones/sludge | Avoid fatty meals; cholecystectomy if diabetic/recurrent. **Mucocele → palpable non-tender GB** |
| **Empyema / acalculous** | Empyema = pus; acalculous = ICU/trauma/burns, **20% mortality** | Drainage / cholecystostomy → cholecystectomy |
| **Cholangitis** | Obstruction + infection. **Charcot's triad = pain+fever+jaundice**; **Reynolds pentad +shock+confusion** | IVF + antibiotics; 15% → biliary decompression (ERCP/PTC) |
| **Choledocholithiasis** | 6–12% of gallstone pts; obstructive jaundice/pancreatitis. **Courvoisier: palpable GB+jaundice ≠ stone (cancer)** | **MRCP** dx; **ERCP + papillotomy** |

## OTHER BILIARY DISEASE
| Condition | Key points |
|---|---|
| Sclerosing cholangitis | Intra/extrahepatic stricture; **2/3 with UC**; cholangiocarcinoma 10–20%; ERC "beading"; stent/transplant |
| Recurrent pyogenic cholangitis | Orient; bacteria + parasites → brown stones, strictures, abscess |
| Parasites | Ascaris (via ampulla), **Clonorchis (BD cancer)**, **Fasciola** (water cress, eosinophilia, triclabendazole), hydatid |
| Choledochal cyst | Female 6:1, premalignant → excision + Roux-en-Y |
| Biliary atresia | 1/4000; jaundice from birth; **Kasai procedure**; untreated dies &lt;3yr |
| BD injury/stricture | Mostly **iatrogenic (cholecystectomy)**; → Roux-en-Y hepaticojejunostomy |

## BILIARY TUMOURS
| Tumour | Key points |
|---|---|
| **Cholangiocarcinoma** | M=F, 50–70yr; risk **PSC, UC, choledochal cyst, Clonorchis**; hilar = **Klatskin** (Bismuth-Corlette); **painless jaundice + pruritus**; CA19-9; resectable 10–20%, median survival 18mo |
| **Gallbladder carcinoma** | **F:M 5:1; &gt;90% gallstones**; adenocarcinoma; risk stones &gt;3cm (10×), polyp &gt;10mm, **porcelain GB**; spreads to liver early, 40% metastatic at dx; cholecystectomy ± hepatectomy by depth |
