=====PAGE 3 [T]=====
Shoulder Impingement
Syndrome
• • Mechanical compression of rotator
cuff (mainly supraspinatus)
• • Subacromial bursa & LH biceps
affected
• • Pain during overhead movements
=====PAGE 4 [T]=====
Causes &
Risk
Factors
=====PAGE 5 [T]=====
Clinical Features
& Tests
• Symptoms:
• • Painful arc (60–120°)
• • Night pain
• • Weak abduction
• Special Tests:
• • Neer test – pain on forced flexion
• • Hawkins–Kennedy – pain on internal rotation
• • Empty can – supraspinatus weakness
=====PAGE 6 [T]=====
Management
• Conservative:
• • Activity modification
• • NSAIDs
• • Physiotherapy (rotator cuff strengthening)
• • Subacromial steroid injection
• Surgical:
• • Arthroscopic subacromial decompression
• • Remove spur
• • Repair cuff tears if present
=====PAGE 7 [T]=====
Frozen
shoulder
• A self-limiting painful stiffness of the shoulder due to
chronic inflammation and fibrosis of the joint capsule,
• Loss of active and passive ROM. ( mainly ER)
=====PAGE 8 [T]=====
• Age 4—50 years
• common among females
• Strong association with DM ( 2—30% )
=====PAGE 9 [T]=====
Casues
Idiopathic
Seconary .. 1-Trauma
2- surgery
3- immobilization
=====PAGE 10 [T]=====
• Pathophysiology .
• Chronic synovial inflammation → fibroblast
proliferation
• Excess type III collagen → capsular thickening
• Involves rotator interval, coracohumeral
ligament, and axillary pouch
=====PAGE 11 [T]=====
• Clinical Features
• Painful stiffness—gradual onset
• Night pain common
• Loss of passive external rotation is the
hallmark
• Global decreased ROM (flexion, abduction)
=====PAGE 12 [T]=====
• Phases
• Freezing (Painful) – severe pain, progressive
stiffness (2–9 months)
• Frozen (Stiff) – pain decreases, stiffness
dominates (4–12 months)
• Thawing – gradual return of ROM (6–24 months)
=====PAGE 13 [T]=====
• Diagnosis:
• - Clinical diagnosis
• - X-ray: rule out arthritis
• - MRI: thickened capsule (not always needed)
Diagnosis &
Management • Management:
• - NSAIDs
• - Physiotherapy (stretching)
• - Intra-articular steroid injection (early)
• - MUA / arthroscopic release if refractory
=====PAGE 14 [T]=====
Calcific Tendinitis
of Shoulder
• • Calcium hydroxyapatite
deposits in rotator cuff
• • Most commonly
supraspinatus
• • Causes acute or chronic
shoulder pain
=====PAGE 15 [T]=====
• Etiology:
• • Tendon hypoxia → fibrocartilaginous
metaplasia
• • Calcium deposition
Etiology &
• • Assoc. with DM & thyroid disease
Phases
• Phases:
• 1. Formative
• 2. Resting
• 3. Resorptive (severe pain)
• 4. Post-calcific
=====PAGE 16 [T]=====
Clinical Features
& Imaging
• Clinical:
• • Sudden severe pain
• • Night pain
• • Painful arc
• • Limited ROM
• Imaging:
• • X-ray: dense circumscribed calcification
• • Ultrasound: size + location
• • MRI: edema/inflammation
=====PAGE 17 [T]=====
Management
• Conservative:
• • NSAIDs, rest
• • Physiotherapy
• • Subacromial steroid injection
• • US-guided barbotage
• • Shock-wave therapy
• Surgical:
• • Arthroscopic removal
• • Repair cuff if torn
=====PAGE 18 [T]=====
Tennis Elbow (Lateral
Epicondylitis)
• • Overuse of ECRB tendon at lateral
epicondyle
• • Repetitive wrist extension &
gripping
=====PAGE 19 [T]=====
Clinical Features
• • Lateral elbow pain
• • Worse with gripping/lifting
• • Tender over lateral epicondyle
• • Pain on resisted wrist extension
• • Positive Maudsley test
=====PAGE 20 [T]=====
Special Tests
• • Cozen’s test – resisted wrist
extension → pain
• • Mill’s test – passive wrist flexion →
pain
• • Maudsley test – resisted middle
finger extension → pain
=====PAGE 21 [T]=====
Management
• • Rest, NSAIDs
• • Physiotherapy (eccentric
strengthening)
• • Counterforce brace
• • ESWT
• • PRP injection
• • Surgery only if >6–12 months
conservative failure

Radial Longitudinal
Deficiency
• • Failure of radial development causing radial
deviation
• • Associations: VACTERL, Holt–Oram, Fanconi
anemia
• • Bayne I–IV classification
• • Treatment: Splinting, stretching,
centralization/radialization, pollicization

Syndactyly
• • Failure of digital separation (web-
space fusion)
• • Associations: Apert syndrome,
Poland syndrome
• • Simple/complex,
complete/incomplete types
• • Treatment: Surgical release at 6–12
months

=====PAGE 26 [T]=====
Thumb
Hypoplasia
• • Underdeveloped thumb of varying
severity
• • Associations: Holt–Oram,
VACTERL
• • Blauth I–V classification
• • Treatment: Opponensplasty, web
deepening,
• pollicization for severe types
=====PAGE 27 [T]=====
Macrodactyly
• • Congenital enlargement of
one or more digits
• • Associations: Proteus
syndrome, NF1
• • Static vs progressive forms
• • Treatment: Soft-tissue
debulking, epiphysiodesis,
amputation in severe cases
