Hepatic steatosis or fatty liver is defined as intrahepatic
TAG of at least 5% of liver weight or 5% of
hepatocytes containing lipid vacuoles
History
➢ Nonalcoholic fatty liver disease was first described in
the 1950s when fatty liver was first characterized in a
group of obese patients.
➢ In 1980, Ludwig and colleagues at the Mayo Clinic
described 20 obese, diabetic, nonalcoholic patients
who had similar findings on liver biopsy to patients
with alcoholic liver disease, and the term
nonalcoholic steatohepatitis was introduced.

New Terms ( Nomenclature)
• Steatotic liver disease or SLD will now be used to
encompass various causes of fat accumulation in
the liver
• Metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver
disease (MASLD) will replace non-alcohol related
fatty liver disease (NAFLD).
• NASH the more serious form of NAFLD will now be
called Metabolic-associated steatohepatitis (MASH).

Radiographic examination
The most commonly used imaging technique in the
diagnosis of NAFLD is ultrasonography (US) with remarkable
sensitivity but without accurate quantification of the
degree of steatosis.
➢ The sensitivity and specificity of ultrasound in patients of
any degree of steatosis is 64% and 97% respectively.
➢ However, in patients with steatosis >30% , the sensitivity
and the specificity of US. increase to 90 % and 100%.
=====PAGE 22 [TEXT]=====
➢ Computed tomography (CT) imaging can accurately
detect and quantify the amount of steatosis in patients.
For steatosis > 30%, the sensitivity and specificity are
82% - 93% and 100%, respectively.
➢ Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) ;
the most accurate available technique in the diagnosis
and quantification of hepatic steatosis. MRI shows a good
correlation with histological examination and the
sensitivity and specificity are 100% and 92.3%,
respectively.

Several panel markers have been created, using
combinations of clinical and biochemical parameters in
order to generate clinical models of fibrosis.
▪ BAAT score and combines four clinical variables: body
mass index (>28 kg/m2), age (>50 years), ALT (>2x
normal), serum triglycerides (>150 mg/dl).
▪ Fibro Test combines 5 biochemical markers including 2-
macroglobulin, Apo lipoprotein A1, haptoglobulin, total
bilirubin, and GGT.
▪ NAFLD fibrosis scoring system uses six commonly
measured parameters including age, hyperglycemia,
BMI, platelet count, albumin level, and AST/ALT ratio.

liver biopsy
Liver biopsy remains the most appropriate( Gold standard)
tool for diagnosis of NAFLD and the evaluation of
histological features.
Indications of liver biopsy:
✓ Age older than 45 years,
✓ presence of obesity,
✓ Type 2 DM or other risk factors of metabolic syndrome
✓ AST/ALP ratio greater than one are associated with
advanced liver fibrosis.
