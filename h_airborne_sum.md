# Air-Borne Diseases — Diphtheria & Mumps

## The Big Picture
Air-borne pathogens enter via the respiratory tract and spread by **droplets, droplet nuclei, or dust**. Control targets the **agent, transmission, host**. This lecture details two: **diphtheria** (toxin-mediated bacterial, vaccine-preventable, dangerous membrane) and **mumps** (viral parotitis, MMR-preventable, orchitis risk).

## Air-Borne Transmission (3 routes)
- **Droplets** — large, settle fast, short distance, only reach **upper** respiratory tract.
- **Droplet nuclei** — evaporated, small/light, travel far, reach **bronchioles/alveoli (lower tract)**.
- **Dust** — organisms persist, acts as reservoir.

## Control (3 headings)
- **Agent** — eliminate reservoir, clean/disinfect.
- **Transmission** — ventilation, UV air disinfection, avoid crowding, masks, personal hygiene.
- **Host** — **immunisation** (measles, influenza, pertussis), immunoglobulins (passive), **chemoprophylaxis** (e.g. isoniazid for TB).

## DIPHTHERIA
- **Acute bacterial** infection of tonsils/pharynx/nose/larynx by **Corynebacterium diphtheriae** releasing a **cytotoxin** (the toxin causes the disease). Asymptomatic colonisation more common than disease.
- **Hallmark:** adherent **whitish/grey pseudomembrane** + surrounding inflammation; sore throat + cervical lymphadenopathy; severe → **"bull neck"** + airway obstruction (may need tracheostomy).
- **Toxin effects:** **myocarditis, heart block, CHF**; later neuropathy + muscle weakness. **CFR 5–10%.**
- **Reservoir:** human. **Air-borne** (contact with patient/carrier); contaminated milk vehicle. **Incubation 2–5 days**; infectious ~2 weeks (antibiotics stop shedding fast); rare carriers 6 months.
- **Diagnosis:** clinical (membrane) + microscopy/culture.
- **Occurrence:** colder months, unvaccinated (Russia outbreak 1990–97, >150,000 cases).
- **Prevention:** **toxoid vaccine** (DTP/PENTA) — 3 doses from 2 months + boosters (2nd year, before school).
- **Treatment:** **diphtheria antitoxin ASAP** (don't wait for results) + antibiotics (penicillin/azithromycin) — antibiotics are NOT a substitute for antitoxin.
- **Contacts:** culture + surveillance 1 week, **single benzathine penicillin** or erythromycin/azithromycin regardless of immunisation; booster if >5 yrs since last dose; food handlers off work.

## MUMPS
- **Paramyxovirus.** Incubation **14–24 days**; communicable 6 days before to ~6 days after symptoms; **droplet/saliva** spread.
- **Clinical:** infection of **parotid (salivary) glands** — swelling + pain over 1–3 days, worse swallowing/chewing/acidic juice; often unilateral then bilateral. Fever, headache, anorexia. **1 in 3 asymptomatic.**
- **Complications:** **orchitis** (adolescent/adult males, ~7–10 d after parotid swelling — sterility rare even if bilateral), oophoritis, **meningitis/encephalitis**, pancreatitis, mastitis, deafness.
- **Diagnosis:** clinical; serology (mumps IgM, IgG rise) or culture (saliva/urine).
- **Treatment:** supportive (no antibiotics — viral): antipyretics, soft bland diet, warm/cold packs, fluids, avoid acidic juice; hospitalise severe.
- **Prevention:** **MMR** (live); 2 doses; if coverage <75% risk of age-shift to adults (more complications); coverage >90% → herd immunity. **Contraindications:** pregnancy, neomycin allergy, immunocompromised. **No proven link to autism.**

## Walk-Away Points
- Transmission: **droplets = upper tract/short range; droplet nuclei = lower tract/long range.**
- **Diphtheria:** toxin → pseudomembrane + myocarditis; **antitoxin ASAP** + antibiotics; toxoid vaccine (DTP).
- **Mumps:** parotitis; **orchitis** in adult males; MMR prevents; supportive treatment only.
