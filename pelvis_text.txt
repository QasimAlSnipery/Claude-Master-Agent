Antero-posterior Compression (Open Book)
❖ Frontal collision between pedestrian and a car.
❖ Fracture pubic rami or disruption of symphysis
(> 2cm is unstable).
❖ SI joint disruption or fracture ilium post.

=====PAGE 13 [T]=====
Lateral Compression Injuries (Buckle Type,
Closed Book):
❖ Side on impact (RTA), fall from height.
❖ Pubic rami on one or both sides anteriorly are broken.
❖ SI sprain, sacral or ilial fractures posteriorly.

Vertical Shear:
❖ Fall from height on one leg.
❖ The innominate bone on one side is displaced

Management
• Airway clearance.
• Breathing.
• Circulation,2-3l loss (occult or obvious bleeding).
▪ Shock.
▪ Rapid applications of pelvic binder or clamp.
▪ Laparotomy.
• Intra-abdominal injury.
• Bladder and urethral injuries (urologist).
• Treatment of the fracture.
10/11/24 AREEWAN SAEED 16
=====PAGE 17 [T]=====
Treatment of the fracture
• Isolated and minimally displaced fractures:
Bed rest until the pain subside then partial wt. bearing
using crutches.
• Stable open book (<2.5 cm seperation)
Bed rest and canvas splint or pelvic binder to close the book
and Thereafter weight bearing as tolerated.

• Unstable open book
>2.5 cm symphysis separation, SI separation, displaced sacral

=====PAGE 23 [T]=====
Column fractures
• Anterior column:
extend from the symphysis pubis alone the superior
pubic ramus across the acetabulum to the anterior part
of the ilium; it’s uncommon, not involving the weight
bearing part of the acetabulum.
10/11/24 AREEWAN SAEED 23
=====PAGE 24 [T]=====
• Posterior column:
extend from the ischium across the posterior acetabular
socket to the greater sciatic notch, involves the weight
bearing part of the acetabulum (post. Hip dislocation).
10/11/24 AREEWAN SAEED 24
=====PAGE 25 [T]=====
• Transverse fractures:
involve both columns, separating an
iliac portion above from pubic and Ischial
part below.
• Complex fractures:
combination of the others.
Clinical features:
❑ Severe inj. (RTA,FFH), Shock.
❑ Associated fractures (calcanium, Knee, femur).
❑ Pain, inability to move, bruises, limb internally rotated
(post. Dislocation).
Exclude sciatic &femoral nerve inj.

Imaging
• Plain radiography: AP, oblique, inlet view:
❖ Ilio-pectineal line disrupted in ant. Column fractures.
❖ Ilio-ischial line disrupted in posterior column fractures.
• CT scan: helpful if operation is indicated

=====PAGE 27 [T]=====
Treatment
• Emergency treatment:
(shock, dislocations).
• Femoral skeletal traction(10kg).
• Non operative treatment:
1. <3mm displacement.
2. If not involving wt. bearing part.
3. Elderly or medical contraindication.
Longitudinal ±lateral traction for 6 weeks followed by
PWB for further 6 weeks.

Operative Treatments
• Displaced fractures, unstable hips, retained bone in the
joint.
• ORIF by rigid plate and screws; followed by PWB after 7
days &physiotherapy for 6 months.
