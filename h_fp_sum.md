# Contraception & Family Planning

## The Big Picture
**Contraception** = intentional prevention of pregnancy; **family planning** = letting individuals/couples achieve their **desired number, spacing, and timing** of children. FP saves lives by avoiding high-risk pregnancies and benefits **women, children, families, and communities**. It is a **human right**.

## Definitions
- **Contraception** — intentional prevention of pregnancy by artificial or natural means.
- **Family planning (WHO)** — anticipate + attain desired number of children and spacing/timing of births, via contraceptive methods + treatment of infertility.

## FP Situation (Iraq, I-WISH 2021)
- Modern FP 74%, traditional methods 26%, all-method prevalence 43.5%, **unmet need 56.6%**.

## High-Risk Pregnancies (FP especially important)
Women who are **<18 or >35**, pregnant **<2 years after a birth** or **<6 months post-abortion/miscarriage**, **high parity (>4 children)**, certain health problems, or no skilled care. Risk multiplies with multiple factors.

### Why each is risky
- **Adolescents (<18):** >70,000 maternal deaths/yr in 15–19s; **<15 = 5× death risk**; prematurity, infant death, unsafe abortion.
- **Spacing too close (<2 yr):** ↑ maternal morbidity/mortality, prematurity, low birth weight, neonatal death.
- **>35:** ≥2× death risk, miscarriage, hypertension, diabetes, small/disabled/stillborn babies.
- **High parity (>4):** death, **anaemia, postpartum haemorrhage, uterine rupture**.
- **Existing disease:** reproductive cancers, cardiovascular, diabetes with vascular complications, sickle cell, liver disease, schistosomiasis/TB/HIV.

## Benefits of FP
- **Women:** lower maternal death, anaemia, poor outcomes, unsafe-abortion complications; non-contraceptive benefits (protect from some STIs, cancers); better education/economic opportunity.
- **Children:** longer breastfeeding (nutrition, immunity, bonding), less child illness/death, more resources per child.
- **Families/communities:** more resources per child, less economic strain, stronger families, women's status + gender equality.

## Timing, Spacing, Limiting (key messages)
- **Too early** → delay first pregnancy to **20**.
- **Too soon after birth** → wait at least **24 months**.
- **Too soon after abortion/miscarriage** → wait **≥6 months**.
- **Too late** → plan before **35**.
- **After 4 children** → risk rises with each pregnancy.

## Human Rights & Choice
ICPD Cairo 1994: "every person counts." FP is most effective when clients make **free, informed choices** respecting their fertility intentions; protects the right to reproductive health; advances SDGs.

## Walk-Away Points
- **Contraception = prevent pregnancy; FP = desired number/spacing/timing** of children.
- High-risk: **<18 or >35, spacing <2 yr, >4 children, existing disease** — FP most important here.
- Spacing rules: **first pregnancy ≥20, ≥24 months between births, ≥6 months post-abortion, plan before 35**.
- FP benefits women + children + families; it is a **human right** based on **free informed choice**.
