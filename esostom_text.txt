Esophagus
• Normal oesophagus when it is full of barium has smooth
outline, and show few parallel vertical lines “mucosal
folds” when partially distended
• Normal indentations on esophagus are seen from aortic
arch, left atrium.
• Normal contraction of esophagus is inverted V shape
downward, but tertiary contractions (corkscrew
oesophagus) giving the oesophagus an undulated
appearance.
• In CT esophagus is collapsed normally.

Strictures & Narrowing of the oesophagus
• Oesophageal carcinoma: usually first diagnosed at
upper GI endoscopy and confirmed by biopsy. Barium
swallow shows an irregular lumen with shouldered
edges and is often several centimeters in length. CT ,
EUS & FDG-PET/CT are used for staging and assess
the response of the tumour to treatment.
• Benign tumours: Leiomyomas cause a smooth,
rounded indentation into the lumen of the oesophagus.

Strictures & Narrowing of the oesophagus
• Peptic strictures: are found at the lower end of the oesophagus, on
Barium they are short and have smooth outlines with tapering ends.
• Achalasia: is neuromuscular abnormality resulting in failure of
relaxation at the cardiac sphincter, in barium swallow appears as
smooth, tapered narrowing, always at the lower end of the
oesophagus, dilatation of the oesophagus above site of narrowing
which often shows absent peristalsis. Plain X ray : may show dilated
esophagus with food residue, lungs may show consolidation and
bronchiectasis from aspiration.
• Corrosive strictures: are the result of swallowing corrosives such as
acids or alkalis. They are long strictures that begin at the level of the
aortic arch. As with other benign strictures usually smooth with
tapered ends on barium swallow, but may be irregular

Peptic ulcer
• Gastric ulcers may be benign or malignant so
confirmation at gastroscopy is routinely undertaken
whereas duodenal ulcers are almost invariably
benign.
• Ulcers are identified as projections of barium
beyond the mucosal profile. With duodenal
ulceration, the duodenal cap (bulb) may be very
deformed by scarring. Folds are reaching ulcer.
10/27/2025 Soran Hisham 15
=====PAGE 16 [T]=====
Gastric carcinoma
• In the past, high quality barium meal examination has been used as a
screening tool in some countries to detect early gastric carcinoma.
However, gastroscopy has now almost completely taken over this role.
• Gastric cancer can present as:
1-Ulcer: At barium examination, gastric carcinoma typically produces an
irregular filling defect, alteration of the normal mucosal pattern “do not
reach ulcer crator”, ulcer is within outline of stomach rather than
projecting beyond the wall.
2- focal gastric wall thickening.
3- linitis plastica: diffuse wall thickening, loss of distensibility “rigid
stomach”, loss of normal gastric folds
• CT, EUS, sometimes staging laporoscopy are used for staging .

Hiatus Hernia
• A hiatus hernia is a herniation of the
stomach into the mediastinum through the
oesophageal hiatus in the diaphragm.
• The commoner type is the sliding hiatus
hernia, where the GEJ and a portion of the
stomach are situated above the diaphragm.
The cardiac sphincter is usually
incompetent, so reflux from the stomach to
the oesophagus occurs and leads to
ulceration or peptic stricture.
• In a rolling or para-oesophageal hernia,
the fundus of the stomach herniates
through the diaphragm, but the GEJ often
remains competent below the diaphragm.
