# SURGICAL ASPECTS OF INFLAMMATORY BOWEL DISEASE

## Overview
- **IBD = idiopathic intestinal inflammation.**
- **Ulcerative colitis**: rectum + colon; **mucosa + submucosa only**; **rectal bleeding, tenesmus, mucus** (covers stool); **90% confined to rectum** but may extend; bloody diarrhoea + urgency; severe → anaemia, hypoproteinaemia, electrolyte disturbance.
- **Crohn's disease**: **chronic full-thickness (transmural)**, any part **mouth → anus**, **discontinuous (skip lesions)**.
- **Indeterminate colitis (10–15%)**: features of both; treatment differs.

## Clinical & severity
- Depends on diagnosis, location, extent. Proctitis = no systemic upset. Colitis = bloody diarrhoea + urgency. Children → impaired growth.
- **Severity classification (UC)**:
  - **Mild**: <4 stools/day, no toxicity.
  - **Moderate**: >4 stools, mild anaemia, ↑ESR/CRP.
  - **Severe**: >6 bloody stools, **fever, tachycardia, anaemia, hypoalbuminaemia (ominous)**.
  - **Fulminant**: >10 movements, continuous bleeding, distension → **toxic megacolon (emergency surgery to avoid perforation)**.

## Extra-intestinal manifestations
- **Arthritis (~15%, large joint), sacroiliitis, ankylosing spondylitis, sclerosing cholangitis, erythema nodosum, pyoderma gangrenosum, uveitis, episcleritis.**

## Cancer risk (UC)
- Increases with duration: **~1% at 10 yrs → 10–15% at 20 yrs → 20% at 30 yrs.** **Pancolitis >10 yrs → endoscopic screening** for silent dysplasia.

## Surgery for UC
- **Lifetime colectomy risk ~20%.**
- **Indications**: severe/fulminant failing medical therapy, chronic disease (anaemia/urgency/tenesmus), **steroid-dependent**, drug intolerance, growth retardation, **neoplastic change (dysplasia/carcinoma)**, sclerosing cholangitis, extraintestinal manifestations, haemorrhage/stenosis.
- **Emergency**: **subtotal colectomy + end-ileostomy** (safest; rectosigmoid remnant as mucous fistula).
- **Elective**: subtotal colectomy + ileostomy; **proctocolectomy + permanent end-ileostomy**; **restorative proctocolectomy + ileal pouch-anal anastomosis (IPAA)**; subtotal colectomy + ileorectal anastomosis.
- **IPAA**: usually 2-stage with covering loop ileostomy. Complications: pelvic infection (anastomotic leak), SBO (10–15%), pouch-vaginal fistula, **pouchitis** (→ metronidazole/ciprofloxacin + probiotics).
- **VTE prophylaxis**: IBD = **3× VTE risk** (higher in UC); post-op LMWH up to 28 days.

## Surgery for Crohn's disease
- **Surgery does NOT cure** — manages complications. **Preserve healthy gut (avoid short bowel syndrome); minimal resection.**
- **Ileocaecal resection** (terminal ileal disease) — primary anastomosis (ileostomy if unwell/infected/depleted).
- Segmental resection (strictures); colectomy + ileorectal anastomosis (rectal sparing); subtotal colectomy + ileostomy; temporary loop ileostomy; **panproctocolectomy + permanent ileostomy** (severe); **stricturoplasty**.
- **Perianal Crohn's**: commonest = **perianal abscess** → fistula. Surgery controls infection; **seton** (allows biologic initiation); **infliximab/adalimumab + seton**; LIFT, advancement flaps, fibrin glue.

## High-yield one-liners
- **UC = mucosal, continuous from rectum; Crohn's = transmural, skip lesions, mouth→anus.**
- **Severity: mild <4 / moderate >4 / severe >6 bloody stools; fulminant >10 → toxic megacolon (emergency surgery).**
- **UC cancer risk ↑ with duration (20% at 30yrs); pancolitis >10yrs → screening.**
- **UC surgery curative (colectomy); emergency = subtotal colectomy + end-ileostomy; elective = IPAA.**
- **Crohn's surgery NOT curative (complications only); minimal resection; perianal = seton + biologics.**
- **IBD = 3× VTE risk → LMWH 28 days; pouchitis → metronidazole.**
