# Maternal & Reproductive Health

## The Big Picture
Reproductive health = **complete physical, mental, social wellbeing** in all matters of the reproductive system — including a safe sex life and the freedom to **decide if, when, and how often to reproduce**. Core themes: components of SRH, **key indicators (MMR, TFR, contraceptive prevalence)**, the **three-delay model**, leading causes of maternal death, and 2030 targets.

## Definitions
- **Reproductive health** (WHO) — complete wellbeing, not just absence of disease, in reproductive matters; ability to reproduce + freedom to decide.
- **Reproductive rights** — couples/individuals decide **freely + responsibly the number, spacing, timing** of children, with information + means, free of discrimination/coercion.

## Milestones
- **1978 Alma-Ata** — Primary Health Care declaration ("Health for All"); MCH + family planning.
- **1987 Safe Motherhood Initiative** — reduce maternal mortality.
- **1994 ICPD (Cairo)** — comprehensive sexual + reproductive health.
- **2000 MDGs → 2015 SDGs.**

## Components of SRH
Family planning/contraception, safe motherhood + newborn care, **STI/HIV prevention**, infertility management, adolescent SRH, gender-based violence, gynaecological cancer (cervical/breast), miscarriage care.

## Key Indicators
- **Globally tracked: Maternal Mortality Ratio (MMR), Total Fertility Rate (TFR), contraceptive prevalence.**
- **MMR** = maternal deaths per **100,000 live births**.
- **TFR** = average children per woman in her lifetime.
- Others: ANC coverage, **skilled birth attendance**, unmet need for FP, perinatal mortality, low birth weight, sex ratio.

## Maternal Mortality
- **Definition (WHO):** death while pregnant or **within 42 days** of pregnancy end, from causes related to/aggravated by pregnancy (not accidental).
- **Direct causes:** haemorrhage, eclampsia, complications of miscarriage, sepsis.
- **Indirect causes:** pre-existing/aggravated disease (CVD, anaemia, renal, metabolic, mental).
- **Five leading causes:** **obstetric haemorrhage, hypertension/(pre)eclampsia, sepsis, venous thromboembolism, cardiac conditions.**

## Three-Delay Model
1. **Delay in deciding to seek care.**
2. **Delay in reaching the facility.**
3. **Delay in receiving appropriate treatment at the facility.**

## Targets (2030 / SDG)
- **MMR <70 per 100,000 live births.**
- **Neonatal mortality ≤12 per 1,000**; **under-5 ≤25 per 1,000**.
- **ENAP-EPMM 90/90/80/80:** 90% ≥4 ANC contacts, 90% skilled birth attendance, 80% postnatal care within 2 days, 80% districts with emergency obstetric + small/sick newborn care.

## Walk-Away Points
- Reproductive health = wellbeing + **freedom to decide number/spacing/timing**; rights are central.
- Key indicators: **MMR (per 100,000), TFR, contraceptive prevalence**.
- Maternal death = within **42 days**; top causes = **haemorrhage, hypertension, sepsis, VTE, cardiac**.
- **Three-delay model**: decide → reach → receive care. 2030 target **MMR <70**.
