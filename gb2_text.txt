Chronic cholecystitis ( biliary colic)
-Duo to obstruction of cystic duct causing GB distention& increasing tension.
-May have normal GB or chronic inflammatory cells in mucosa with
hypertrophy of or normal cystic duct mucosa or atrophied & epithelium
protruding to the muscle coat forming ( Aschoff-
Rokitansky sinuses).
Clinical features:-
-Abrupt pain continue 1-5 hours, then no pain, normal ,re-occur
-Pain at epigastrium, right hypochondrium , or back occur at night or after
heavy meal
-+/-N, V
-Atypical presentation
-50% associated with meal, pain Rt. LQ or LT UQ
-Bloating & belching with pain attacks

Acalculous cholecystitis
-Cause unknown but bile stasis & GB distention with ischemia may be
implicated as causative factor.
-Without stone acute & chronic occur with same clinical features
-Some has nonspecific inflammation; others has one of the
cholecystoses
-Seen after major surgery or trauma , burn ( critically ill patients in
ICU), if diagnosis missed in them mortality rate is 20%
-Its pathology is edema of the serosa & muscle layer with patchy
thrombosis of the arterioles & venules.

Cholangitis
-Is ascending bacterial infection in associated partial or complete
obstruction of BD.
-Bile is sterile, but become infected when mechanical obstruction occur
as only bacterial contamination of the bile without obstruction will
not cause clinical cholangitis.
-Most common m.o. (E.coli, Klebsiella pnum., Streptococcus faecalis,
Enterobacter., Bacteroides Fragilis)

1-Intraluminal (stones, stent , parasite, instrumentation as ERCP,

2-Intramural duo to stricture(B. or M.), partially obstructed as
complication of the biliary-enteric anastomoses.
3-Extramural as tumor (B. or M.),enlargement LN.

Clinical features
-Mild intermittent form to self limited to fulminating duo to potentially
life threaten septicemia.
-Typically older females common, who has gall stones.
-Charcot's triad ( pain ,fever, jaundice) in 2/3 of the patients.
-If progress rapidly causing septicemia & disorientation known as
Reynolds pentad (pain, fever, jaundice, shock, mental state
changes).
-Atypical cases has little fever, jaundice, pain.
-Occur in elderly ,whom they get unremarkable symptoms until
collapse with septicemia.
-Patients with indwelling stent rarely get jaundice.

choledocholithiasis
-6-12% of patients with gall stones get CBD stones.
-80% stones of CBD associated with infection rather than stones coming
from GB.
-Primary biliary stones common in Asian population usually pigmented
stones, associated with stasis which cause infection, this
encourage stone formation.
-Secondary biliary stone common in Western population usually
cholesterol stones, GB stone pass to CBD, may occur years after
cholecystectomy duo to development of new pathology as BD
infection, infestation by Ascaris lumbricoides, Clonorchis sinensis.
-Stone may passed or impacted or intermittent act as Ball-valve at
ampulla.

- These stone cause repeated cholangitis, stricture, more stone

Sclerosing cholangitis
-Uncommon inflammatory stricture of extra-& intrahepatic biliary tree
and may involve GB & pancreas.
-It has 2 types:-
1-Primary type unknown etiology
-It is progressive disease-causing liver cirrhosis.
2-Secondary sclerosing cholangitis may be duo to :-
-Stone
-Acute cholangitis
-Previous biliary surgery
-Toxic agents
-In 2/3 of cases is associated with ulcerative colitis or
Riedel's thyroiditis or retroperitoneal fibrosis.
