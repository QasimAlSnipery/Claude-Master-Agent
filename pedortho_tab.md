# PEDIATRIC ORTHOPEDICS & PERIPHERAL NERVE INJURIES — Revision Table
Bold = high-yield.

## PEDIATRIC HIP & FOOT
| Condition | Key facts | Clinical / signs | Diagnosis | Treatment |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **DDH** | Girls 7:1, L&gt;R, bilateral 20%; breech, tight swaddling | **Ortolani (reduces), Barlow (dislocates)**; limited abduction, asymmetric creases, waddling gait | **US &lt;6mo, X-ray &gt;4mo**; ↑acetabular angle, broken Shenton's, Hilgenreiner/Perkins lines | **0–6mo Pavlik harness**; 6–18mo closed/open reduction + spica; 18mo–4yr open + osteotomy; bilateral &gt;4yr no op |
| **Perthes** | **Idiopathic AVN femoral head**; boy 4–8, bilateral 10% | Painful/painless limp, **pain referred to knee**, +Trendelenburg, ↓abduction+IR | X-ray: ↑density, flattening, fragmentation, **mushroom head, sagging rope** | **&lt;6yr conservative; &gt;6yr containment** (brace ≥1yr or osteotomy) |
| **Irritable hip (transient synovitis)** | Boy 2:1, age 3–9 | Acute painful hip, ↓IR, healthy ± low fever, lasts 5–10d | **Diagnosis of exclusion** (CBC/ESR/CRP/X-ray); DDx septic arthritis | Bed rest, NSAID, weight-bearing aids |
| **Femoral anteversion (in-toe)** | Commonest in-toe cause | Squinting patellae, TV position, clumsy gait | Clinical, US, axial CT | Improves with growth; osteotomy rare (not &lt;8yr) |
| **Clubfoot (CTEV)** | 1–3/1000, boy 2:1, bilateral 50% | **Equinus + heel varus + forefoot adduction/supination**; check DDH + spina bifida | Clinical (obvious at birth) | **Ponseti** (&gt;90%): casts → Achilles tenotomy → abduction brace |

## PERIPHERAL NERVE INJURY — DEGREES
| Degree | Lesion | Cause | Recovery |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Neurapraxia** | Demyelination | Mechanical pressure | **Full recovery** |
| **Axonotmesis** | Axon interruption | Closed fracture/dislocation | Variable |
| **Neurotmesis** | Nerve division | Open wounds | **Needs surgical repair** |

## NERVE LESIONS
| Nerve | Cause | Deficit |
|---|---|---|
| **Radial** | Humerus #, tourniquet, Saturday night/crutch palsy | **Wrist drop**, loss MCP extension, sensory dorsum thumb base; splint wrist extended |
| **Ulnar** | Wrist laceration / cubital tunnel | **Claw hand**, interossei+hypothenar wasting, **Froment's +ve**, ulnar 1½ finger loss (high = less claw, ulnar paradox) |
| **Median** | Wrist cut / forearm # | Thenar wasting, weak abduction/opposition, radial 3½ finger loss; high = **pointing index** |
| **Erb's palsy** | Birth traction, C5–6±7 | **"Waiter's tip"** (adducted, internally rotated, pronated) |
| **Femoral** | Gunshot, thigh bleed, traction | Weak knee extension, anterior thigh/shin numbness, ↓knee jerk |
| **Sciatic** | Hip dislocation/surgery, pelvic # | Paralysis below knee, weak knee flexion, **foot drop**, absent ankle jerk |
| **Common peroneal** | Varus stress, splint pressure | **Foot drop** + sensory loss (except sole + shin) |
| **Deep peroneal** | Anterior compartment syndrome | Foot drop + 1st web space loss → **urgent fasciotomy** |

## NERVE — TREATMENT PRINCIPLES
| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Closed (neurapraxia/axonotmesis) | Wait weeks for spontaneous recovery |
| Open | Early suture / nerve graft |
| No recovery 18–24 mo | **Tendon transfer** |
| While awaiting | Full ROM 2×/day, protect skin, splint to prevent contracture |
