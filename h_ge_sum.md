# Gastroenteritis (Food Poisoning)

## The Big Picture
Gastroenteritis = inflammation of the stomach + small/large intestine, usually **infectious** (food/water-borne), causing **diarrhoea ± vomiting, cramps**. Most self-limit in ~3 days; **the great danger is dehydration**, especially in children/elderly. **Viruses (norovirus, rotavirus) = ~70% of cases.** Cornerstone of treatment = **rehydration (ORS)**.

## Definitions
- **Gastroenteritis** — GI inflammation (infectious or non-infectious: drugs, toxins).
- **Food poisoning** — foodborne GI infection/inflammation.
- **Diarrhoea (WHO)** — ≥3 loose/watery stools/24 h; **acute if <14 days**.

## Burden
- ~1.6 million deaths/year; every child gets ≥1 rotavirus episode by age 5; major killer of under-5s.

## Causes
- **Viral (~70%, commonest):** **norovirus** (commonest worldwide, adults), **rotavirus** (infants/young children), astrovirus, adenovirus.
- **Bacterial (10–20%):** E. coli (incl. **O157:H7**), Salmonella, Shigella, **Campylobacter**, Listeria.
- **Parasitic (<10%):** Giardia (water), Cryptosporidium, Entamoeba.
- **Non-infectious:** NSAIDs/antibiotics/chemo/digoxin/colchicine, food intolerance (lactose, gluten), heavy metals/seafood toxins.

## Pathophysiology (toxin vs invasion)
- **Non-inflammatory/watery** — preformed toxins (**S. aureus, B. cereus, C. perfringens**) or enterotoxins (**ETEC, V. cholerae**); viral enterocyte invasion.
- **Inflammatory/bloody** — mucosal invasion (**Salmonella, Shigella, Campylobacter**), Shiga toxin (E. coli O157:H7), cytotoxin (C. difficile).

## Clinical
- Diarrhoea (watery or bloody), vomiting (esp. viral), cramps, fever, malaise, **dehydration** (the main danger).
- Concern features: **fever, blood/mucus in stool, severe abdominal pain/swelling**.

## Diagnosis
- Mostly **clinical** — specific cause not needed unless outbreak/complications.
- If needed: **stool** microscopy (Giardia/Entamoeba cysts), antigen tests, **culture** (Salmonella/Shigella/Campylobacter/E. coli), toxin tests (S. aureus, C. diff), viral PCR; bloods for dehydration/electrolytes.

## Treatment
1. **Rehydration (most crucial):** **ORS** (mild-moderate), **IV fluids** (severe). ORS per litre: NaCl 3.5 g, KCl 1.5 g, trisodium citrate 2.9 g, glucose 20 g.
2. **Diet:** BRAT (banana, rice, apple sauce, toast); avoid fatty food/caffeine.
3. **Drugs:** antiemetics (severe vomiting); **antidiarrhoeals NOT for infectious GE** (loperamide); **antibiotics only for confirmed bacterial** (ciprofloxacin) / parasites (metronidazole).
4. **Probiotics** (Lactobacillus) in recovery.

## Prevention
- **Hygiene** — **handwashing** (before food, after toilet), clean surfaces.
- **Water safety** — safe/bottled water when travelling.
- **Food safety** — cook thoroughly, separate raw meat board, avoid raw meat/fish.
- **Rotavirus vaccine** (live oral): **RotaTeq (3 doses: 2,4,6 mo)** or **Rotarix (2 doses: 2,4 mo)**.

## Walk-Away Points
- Mostly **viral (norovirus/rotavirus ~70%)**; **dehydration is the killer**.
- Watery (toxin/viral) vs bloody/inflammatory (Salmonella/Shigella/Campylobacter/STEC).
- Treat with **ORS/IV rehydration**; **no antidiarrhoeals in infectious GE**; antibiotics only if confirmed bacterial.
- Prevent: handwashing, safe water/food, **rotavirus vaccine** (oral live).
