# Energy & Macronutrients

## The Big Picture
The three **macronutrients — carbohydrate, protein, fat** — supply energy and build/maintain the body. Energy balance = intake vs expenditure. **Carbs 4 kcal/g, protein 4 kcal/g, fat 9 kcal/g.** Key clinical theme: **fat type matters** — saturated/trans raise cardiovascular risk, unsaturated/omega-3 protect.

## Energy Basics
- Energy values: **carbohydrate 4 kcal/g, protein 4 kcal/g, fat 9 kcal/g** (~37 kJ/g), alcohol 7 kcal/g.
- **Energy balance** = intake vs expenditure (basal metabolic rate + activity + thermogenesis).
- Requirements rise with growth, pregnancy, **lactation (+~500 kcal)**, and disease/hypermetabolism (cancer, sepsis).
- Measured by indirect calorimetry; estimated by equations.

## Carbohydrates
- Main energy source; regulate blood glucose. Should be ~**45–65% of calories**.
- **Fibre** — important for gut health; fermented by colonic microbiota → **short-chain fatty acids**; adds bulk, feeds beneficial bacteria; soluble vs insoluble.
- Avoid glucose infusion at physiological maximum rates during nutritional support.

## Fats (Lipids)
- **Definitions:** fats = solid at room temp (animal); oils = liquid (vegetable); lipids = water-insoluble, mostly **triacylglycerols (triglycerides, 90–95%)**, phospholipids, sterols (cholesterol).
- **Roles:** energy (main store), structural (cell membranes, insulation), metabolic (precursor for hormones, bile, vitamin D; carry fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, K).
- Intake ~**25–30% of calories**, saturated minimised.

### Fatty Acid Types
- **Saturated (SFA)** — no double bonds, solid, animal (butter, lard, meat, cheese, coconut/palm oil) → **↑ plasma cholesterol**.
- **Unsaturated (UFA)** — has double bonds, liquid, vegetable.
  - **Monounsaturated (MUFA)** — one double bond (olive oil, avocado).
  - **Polyunsaturated (PUFA)** — multiple double bonds; includes **essential fatty acids** → **↓ plasma cholesterol**; **omega-3 anti-inflammatory**.
- **Cis vs Trans:** natural unsaturated fats are cis; **trans fats** = industrially "partially hydrogenated" oils (bakery/snacks/margarine) — **worst for health**: ↑ LDL, ↓ HDL, ↑ CHD risk (2% trans intake → 23% ↑ CHD).
- **Essential fatty acids (EFAs)** — PUFA the body can't make (linoleic n-6, α-linolenic n-3) → diet → elongated to **long-chain PUFA (AA, EPA, DHA)**.

### Roles of EFA/PUFA
- Membrane stability, fluidity, enzyme/receptor function.
- **Eicosanoids** (from arachidonic acid): prostacyclins (inhibit platelets), thromboxanes (aggregate platelets), leukotrienes (inflammation); **n-3 derived eicosanoids ↓ CHD risk**.
- **Brain/retina rich in AA + DHA**; critical in 3rd trimester + premature infant formulas.

## Protein
- Roles: **muscle maintenance, immune response, enzymes, structure**.
- Made of amino acids (essential must come from diet). Quality depends on amino-acid completeness (animal = complete).
- Needs rise in growth, pregnancy, stress/hypermetabolism.

## Walk-Away Points
- Energy: **carb 4, protein 4, fat 9 kcal/g**; balance intake vs expenditure.
- **Carbs 45–65%, fat 25–30%** of calories; fibre feeds microbiota → SCFAs.
- **Fat type matters: saturated/trans ↑ cardiovascular risk; unsaturated/omega-3 protect**; trans fats are worst.
- **Essential fatty acids (n-3, n-6)** → eicosanoids, brain/retina (DHA), must come from diet.
