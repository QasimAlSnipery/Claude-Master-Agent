# Child Health Care & Growth Monitoring

## The Big Picture
Child health care = comprehensive services to promote optimal **growth + development in under-5s**. Children are ~40–45% of the population and much of their morbidity/mortality is **avoidable**. The cornerstone is **growth monitoring** — serial weighing plotted on **growth charts (Z-scores)** to catch malnutrition early ("most malnutrition is invisible").

## Why Preventive Services for Children
- Children = **40–45% of population**.
- Avoidable morbidity/mortality: accidents/injuries, poisoning, **SIDS**, vaccine-preventable infections.

## Goals (4 levels)
- **Promotive:** health education, breastfeeding, immunisation awareness.
- **Preventive:** immunisation, developmental screening, nutritional supplements.
- **Curative:** manage ARI, diarrhoea, malnutrition.
- **Rehabilitative:** chronic illness/disability + family counselling.

## Parts of the Program
Growth monitoring, **CDD (diarrhoea control)**, breastfeeding, **EPI (immunisation)**, family planning, food fortification/supplementation, female education, **ARI control**. (Mnemonic-ish: GOBI-FFF style.)

## Growth vs Development
- **Growth** = quantitative (size, weight, height, head circumference).
- **Development** = qualitative (abilities/functions). **Domains:** gross motor, fine motor, language, social/emotional, cognitive.

### Developmental Milestones
- **2 mo:** head control, follows objects, social smile.
- **6 mo:** sits with support, transfers objects, babbles.
- **9 mo:** crawls, "mama/dada," stranger anxiety.
- **12 mo:** walks with support, first words, waves bye.

## Newborn Assessment
- **Apgar score** — status immediately after birth + response to resuscitation.
- **Late newborn care** — detailed exam within 48 h by paediatrician.

## Growth Monitoring
- **Serial weighing + plotting on growth chart/child health card** to detect malnutrition early.
- **Two chart types:**
  - **Z-score chart** — SDs from the mean (0 = median = 50th percentile; ±1 = 15th/85th; ±2 = 3rd/97th).
  - **Percentile chart.**
- **Z-score indicators:**
  - **WAZ (weight-for-age)** — underweight/malnutrition.
  - **HAZ (height-for-age)** — **stunting** (chronic).
  - **WHZ (weight-for-height)** — **wasting** (acute).
  - Classification: −1 to 0 normal; −2 to −1 mild; −3 to −2 moderate; −4 to −3 severe.
- **Head circumference** — recorded at birth; **>97th or <3rd percentile → detailed assessment** (macro/microcephaly).
- **Regular weight gain = best sign of healthy growth**; flat/falling line = malnutrition.

## Malnutrition
- Body gets wrong amount of nutrients (too little/much/imbalanced); very prevalent in under-5s in developing world.
- **"Iceberg phenomenon"** — most malnutrition invisible; only 1–2% show clinical signs.
- **Causes:** inadequate breastfeeding/complementary feeding, repeated infection, poor maternal knowledge, poverty/food insecurity.
- **Severe protein-energy malnutrition:**
  - **Marasmus** — non-oedematous, severe wasting, "skin and bones," very low weight.
  - **Kwashiorkor** — oedematous, muscle wasting masked by **generalised oedema**, fatty tissue preserved.

## Walk-Away Points
- Child health care = promotive/preventive/curative/rehabilitative for under-5s.
- **Growth monitoring** = serial weighing on growth chart; **Z-score 0 = 50th percentile, ±2 = 3rd/97th**.
- **WAZ underweight, HAZ stunting (chronic), WHZ wasting (acute)**.
- Malnutrition = **iceberg** (mostly invisible); **marasmus (wasting) vs kwashiorkor (oedema)**.
