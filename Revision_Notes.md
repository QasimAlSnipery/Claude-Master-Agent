# 1. Pancreatic Disorders

## Acute Pancreatitis
- **Pathophysiology**: premature intracellular trypsinogen activation → proteases digest pancreas. NF-κB activation → inflammation. Severity = balance of proteolytic enzymes vs antiproteolytic factors.
- **Causes**: gallstones, alcohol, pancreatic duct obstruction (also drugs, ERCP, hyperCa, hyperTG).
- **Clinical**: severe constant **upper abdominal pain radiating to back**, builds 15–60 min; N/V; epigastric tenderness; guarding/rebound ABSENT early (retroperitoneal); quiet bowel sounds (ileus). Severe → hypoxia, hypovolaemic shock. **Grey Turner's** (flanks) / **Cullen's** (periumbilical) = haemorrhage.
- **Investigations**: **↑serum amylase or lipase + US/CT swelling**. Lipase preferred. Amylase normalises 24–48h; persistently high = pseudocyst. CECT 6–10d for necrosis.
- **Severity**: **CRP >210 mg/L in first 4 days = severe (80% accuracy)**. Amylase has NO prognostic value. Scores: Glasgow-Imrie, Ranson, APACHE II.
- **Management**: opiate analgesia, crystalloid resuscitation, severe → HDU/ICU. O2, insulin for hyperglycaemia, IV Ca. **Early enteral feeding** (NG=NJ). LMWH prophylaxis. **NO prophylactic antibiotics**; infected necrosis → carbapenem/quinolone + metronidazole. Cholangitis/jaundice + severe → **urgent ERCP**. **Cholecystectomy within 2 weeks** of resolution.
- **Complications**: **pseudocyst** (matures ~6 wks; >6cm rarely resolves → may erode vessels), pancreatic ascites, pleural effusion, necrosis, abscess, multi-organ failure. Infected necrosis/abscess → endoscopic drainage or MIRP necrosectomy.

## Chronic Pancreatitis
- **Definition**: fibrosis + destruction of exocrine tissue; DM in advanced disease.
- **Causes**: ~80% alcohol; tropical (malnutrition, cassava).
- **Clinical**: middle-aged alcoholic men; almost all have abdominal pain (eased leaning forward); weight loss, steatorrhoea (>90% exocrine loss); DM 30% (70% if calcific); erythema ab igne.
- **Management**: alcohol avoidance; pain (NSAIDs → opiates, pregabalin/TCA, pancreatic enzymes, coeliac plexus block, total pancreatectomy last resort); malabsorption (low-fat diet + enzymes + **PPI**); complications.

## Autoimmune Pancreatitis
- Mimics cancer, **responds to steroids**. ↑IgG4, sausage-shaped pancreas. Excellent steroid response, some need azathioprine.

## Pancreatic Cancer
- **90% ductal adenocarcinoma**; early invasion. Risk: age, smoking, chronic pancreatitis, hereditary (HNPCC, FAMMM).
- **Clinical**: central abdominal pain → back, weight loss, **obstructive jaundice**, cachexia. 60% head. **Courvoisier**: palpable GB + jaundice.
- **Investigations**: US + CECT; EUS/laparoscopy staging; ERCP to stent CBD (palliative).
- **Management**: surgical resection only cure (5-yr ~12%); adjuvant gemcitabine. Only 10–15% resectable. Palliative FOLFIRINOX (median 11mo). Overall survival 3–5%.

**High-yield**: CRP >210 = severe AP; amylase no prognostic value; cholecystectomy within 2 wks; no prophylactic antibiotics; Courvoisier; Grey Turner/Cullen.

# 2. Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD)

## Definition & types
- Idiopathic GIT inflammation (exclude infection/ischaemia/radiation/vasculitis). **Crohn (CD)** + **Ulcerative colitis (UC)**; indeterminate colitis 10–15%. Microscopic colitis = separate.

## Risk factors
- Genetic (family history, monozygotic twins, Jewish). Environmental: developed countries. **Smoking + appendectomy = risk for CD but PROTECTIVE for UC.**

## CD vs UC
| Feature | Crohn | UC |
|---|---|---|
| Location | Mouth→anus, terminal ileum | Rectum + colon |
| Spread | Discontinuous (**skip lesions**) | Continuous from rectum |
| Depth | **Transmural** | Mucosa/submucosa |
| Histology | Non-caseating **granuloma** | Crypt abscesses |
| Bleeding | Less | **More (hematochezia)** |
| Fistula/stricture | Common | Rare |
| Toxic megacolon | Rare | Yes |
| Cancer risk | 1–3% | 5–20% |
| Smoking | Harmful | Protective |

## Clinical
- **UC**: bloody diarrhoea, tenesmus, urgency. Proctitis 25%, left-sided 25%, pancolitis 50%.
- **CD**: abd pain, diarrhoea, weight loss, fistula (transmural), perianal disease, RLQ mass.

## Severity (Truelove-Witts)
| Param | Mild | Severe |
|---|---|---|
| Bowel movements | ≤4 | ≥6 |
| Fever | Absent | >37.5°C ×2d |
| HR | <100 | >100 |
| Hb | Normal | ≤75% |
| ESR | <30 | >30 |

## Extra-intestinal manifestations
- Oral aphthous ulcers, arthralgia, ankylosing spondylitis/sacroiliitis, uveitis/scleritis/episcleritis, **pyoderma gangrenosum, erythema nodosum**, **PSC (UC-associated)**. **Heart NOT a common EIM.**

## Diagnosis
- Colonoscopy + biopsy; exclude C. diff/CMV. **UC**: continuous, crypt abscesses. **CD**: skip lesions, cobblestone, granulomas; CT/MR enterography for small bowel.
- **Mayo endoscopic score** (UC): 0 normal → 3 severe (spontaneous bleeding/ulceration).

## Treatment
- **UC**: mild-mod → **5-ASA** (topical/oral) first-line; moderate → oral steroids (budesonide MMX); severe → IV steroids → ciclosporin/infliximab/colectomy. Biologics: anti-TNF, vedolizumab.
- **CD**: 5-ASA little benefit; budesonide; steroids induce not maintain → **AZA/6-MP, methotrexate**, anti-TNF. Natalizumab (PML risk if JC+).

## Health maintenance
- **CRC surveillance from 8 years** (extensive), then 1–2 yrly. Osteoporosis (steroids >3mo → DEXA). B12 (ileal CD). Vaccines (avoid live on immunosuppression). TB + hep screen before anti-TNF.

## Microscopic colitis
- Watery non-bloody diarrhoea, elderly, normal endoscopy → biopsy. DDx: IBS, coeliac, Crohn. **Budesonide = drug of choice**; 5-ASA not effective.

**MCQ answers** (lecture): 1-C, 2-D, 3-C, 4-A(UC), 5-B(Crohn skip), 6-B(Crohn transmural), 7-B(Crohn granuloma), 8-C(both EIM), 9-A(5-ASA not in CD), 10-D(attenuated vaccines), 11-D(5-ASA not for MC), 12-D(natalizumab PML), 13-A(Crohn), 14-E(8 yrs), 15-E, 16-D(heart).

# 3. Myo-pericardial Diseases

## Myocarditis
- Infectious/toxic/autoimmune. 4 presentations: fulminant, acute (→DCM), chronic active (giant cells), chronic persistent.
- Sudden death in young athletes. **Ix**: cardiac MRI, troponin, ECG nonspecific. **Tx**: supportive; **NO steroid benefit**.

## Cardiomyopathy
| Type | Features | Tx |
|---|---|---|
| **Dilated (DCM)** | LV dilatation, ↓contraction; **≥25% inherited AD**; HF, arrhythmia, thromboembolism | β-blocker + ACEi/ARB, **ICD ± CRT** |
| **Hypertrophic (HCM/HOCM)** | **Most common**; asymmetric septal hypertrophy → LVOT obstruction + SAM. **Echo diagnostic.** Jerky pulse | β-blocker, myectomy/alcohol septal ablation. **AVOID digoxin/vasodilators** |
| **Restrictive** | Stiff ventricles; **amyloidosis commonest**; endomyocardial biopsy | Symptomatic, poor prognosis |

## Pericardial disease
- **Acute pericarditis**: chest pain → shoulders, worse lying/inspiration; **friction rub (to-and-fro)**; ECG **widespread ST↑ + PR↓**. Tx: **aspirin/NSAID** ± colchicine. Purulent → antibiotics + drainage.
- **Effusion**: quiet heart sounds; ECG low voltage + **electrical alternans**; globular CXR. Large → aspirate.
- **TB pericarditis**: malaise, weight loss, fever; **anti-TB + prednisolone**.
- **Constrictive pericarditis**: thick/fibrotic/calcified; follows TB/viral/RA; **CT for calcification**; loop diuretic + spironolactone; **pericardiectomy** (50% disappointing).
- **Cardiac tamponade**: EMERGENCY; acute HF from compression; **hypotension + tachycardia + raised JVP**, pulsus paradoxus; **urgent pericardiocentesis**.

**High-yield**: steroids NO in myocarditis, YES in TB pericarditis. Tamponade = emergency pericardiocentesis. HCM jerky pulse. Pericarditis ECG = widespread ST↑ + PR↓.

# 4. Acute Parenchymal Liver Disease

## Definition & causes
- Sudden hepatocyte necrosis. Causes: viral hepatitis (A-E, CMV, EBV), drugs (rifampicin, paracetamol, halothane), alcohol, non-viral infection, poisons (Amanita, CCl4), metabolic (Wilson, haemosiderosis, fatty liver of pregnancy), ischaemic (shock, Budd-Chiari), Reye's.

## Drug metabolism
- Liver converts fat-soluble → water-soluble. **Phase I** (oxidation, cytochrome P450) → **Phase II** (conjugation). Large → bile; small → urine.
- **Enzyme INDUCERS**: alcohol, barbiturates, carbamazepine, phenytoin, rifampicin, primidone.
- **Enzyme INHIBITORS**: cimetidine, INH, ketoconazole.

## Hepatotoxic patterns
| Pattern | Drugs |
|---|---|
| Cholestasis | estrogen, chlorpromazine |
| Cholestatic hepatitis | NSAIDs, statins, co-amoxiclav |
| Acute hepatitis | INH, rifampicin |
| NASH | amiodarone |
| Venous outflow obstruction | busulfan, azathioprine |
| Fibrosis | methotrexate |

## Acute hepatic damage — 2 mechanisms
- **Dose-related/predictable** = paracetamol. **Idiosyncratic/immune** (fever, rash, arthralgia, eosinophilia) = halothane.
- Co-amoxiclav → **ductopenia**.

## Cirrhosis cautions
- NSAIDs (hepatorenal + varices), ACEi (hepatorenal), codeine/anxiolytics (encephalopathy). **Paracetamol ≤3g/day safe**.

# 5. Autoimmune Hepatitis (AIH)

- Unknown etiology; **young women, ↑IgG, autoantibodies**. Associated with other autoimmune disease.
- **Antibodies**: AIH = ANA 80% + ASMA 70%; **PBC = AMA 95%**.
- **Types**: I (ANA/ASMA, HLA-DR3/4), **II (anti-LKM1, children)**, III (anti-SLA).
- **Clinical**: insidious fatigue/jaundice; 25% acute. Amenorrhoea rule. Spider naevi, hepatosplenomegaly.
- **Associations**: Hashimoto's, Coombs+ haemolytic anaemia, UC, GN.
- **Diagnosis**: exclude viral/drug; **↑IgG invariable**; biopsy = **interface hepatitis**. IAIHG scoring (female, ALP:AST, IgG, autoantibody titre, AMA −4, histology, etc.).
- **Management**: **prednisolone 40mg → taper + azathioprine** (10-yr survival 27%→63%). Steroids don't prevent cirrhosis. Maintain ≥2 yrs after LFTs normal.
- **Prognosis**: most → cirrhosis; untreated 50% die 5yr → 10% with therapy.

# 6. Alcoholic Liver Disease

## 3 types
| Type | Reversible | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Fatty liver | Yes | >90% |
| Alcoholic hepatitis | Yes | 10–35% |
| Cirrhosis (micronodular) | No | 10–20% |

- **1 unit = 8g** alcohol. **40–60g/day ×10–15yr → hepatitis.**
- **Pathophysiology**: P450 inducer, TNF-α, ↑NADPH → fat; acetaldehyde toxic; endotoxin → fibrosis.
- **Risk**: amount/duration, continuous > binge, **women lower threshold**, HCV, iron.
- **Alcoholic hepatitis**: **Mallory bodies** (suggestive not pathognomonic), PMN, fever, jaundice, **WBC↑**, **AST/ALT >2** (transaminases <400), PT↑.
- **Cirrhosis**: only 15–20% of heavy drinkers; women more prone; enzymes ~normal.
- **Investigations**: **macrocytosis without anaemia, ↑GGT**, unexplained rib fracture.
- **Prognosis**: **Maddrey DF ≥32 = severe**; Glasgow score >9. Poor: encephalopathy, ascites, renal failure, bilirubin >20.
- **Treatment**: **stop alcohol** (most important), high calorie + thiamine. **DF >32 → steroids** (65%→85% survival; avoid if bleeding/infection/renal failure). Pentoxifylline.

**High-yield**: AST/ALT >2 = alcoholic; macrocytosis + ↑GGT; DF ≥32 → steroids; only 15-20% get cirrhosis.

# 7. Fulminant Hepatic Failure

- **Definition**: severe hepatic failure + encephalopathy in **previously normal liver**.
- **Classification**: hyperacute (<7d, viral/paracetamol), acute (1–4wk), subacute (4–12wk, HCV).
- **Etiology**: 60–70% viral; paracetamol 50% UK; 25% drug. Rare: Wilson, AIH, Budd-Chiari, fatty liver of pregnancy.
- **Clinical**: jaundice + encephalopathy within 2 wks; fetor; **cerebral oedema in 80% of deaths**.

## Encephalopathy grades (West Haven)
| Grade | Findings |
|---|---|
| Minimal | only on testing |
| 1 | mild ↓awareness, psychomotor slowing |
| 2 | disoriented to time, **asterixis** |
| 3 | gross disorientation, asterixis |
| 4 | coma |

- **Ix**: cause (toxicology, viral, copper, autoantibodies, US Doppler). **PT = best severity marker**; AST falls as liver dies.
- **Complications**: infection 80%, ↑ICP, renal failure, hypoglycaemia, lactic acidosis.
- **Treatment**: ICU supportive. **Cerebral oedema → mannitol** (steroids no value). Coagulopathy → vit K/FFP. **Paracetamol → NAC** (even after 10h, <36h). Transplant 1-yr survival 60–70%.
- **Prognosis (King's College)**: Non-paracetamol = PT>100, OR 3 of (HCV/drug, age <10/>40, jaundice→enceph >7d, bilirubin >18, PT>50), OR factor V <15%, OR grade 3/4. **Paracetamol = pH<7.3, OR PT>100 + creatinine>3.4 + grade III/IV.** Mortality without transplant 70–80%.

# 8. MASLD (Metabolic Associated Steatotic Liver Disease)

- **New names (2024)**: SLD umbrella; **MASLD replaces NAFLD; MASH replaces NASH**.
- **Steatosis** = ≥5% liver fat.
- **Diagnosis**: steatosis + ≥1 cardiometabolic factor (overweight, dysglycaemia/T2DM, ↑TG, ↓HDL, ↑BP) + no secondary cause.
- **Secondary causes**: drugs (MTX, amiodarone, steroids, tamoxifen), surgery, inborn errors, IBD.
- **Pathophysiology**: insulin resistance → FFA lipotoxicity → inflammation → fibrosis. Adiponectin protective.
- **Screening (T2DM)**: **FIB-4, NAFLD fibrosis score, Fibroscan (VCTE)**.
- **Clinical**: usually asymptomatic, fatigue, RUQ pain.
- **Investigations**: US (>30% steatosis 90/100%), **MRI most accurate**, Fibroscan for fibrosis. **Biopsy = gold standard**. **AST/ALT >1 = advanced fibrosis**.
- **Management**: **lifestyle = most effective**; weight loss ≥5% (steatosis), ≥7–10% (MASH/fibrosis). Mediterranean diet, exercise. Pharmacotherapy: **GLP-1RA, SGLT2** (diabetic); **resmetirom** (MASH-targeted); statins; bariatric.

**High-yield**: NAFLD→MASLD, NASH→MASH; T2DM highest risk; MRI best imaging; biopsy gold standard; lifestyle first.

# 9. Pituitary Gland & Disorders

## Anatomy & investigation principles
- Sella turcica; optic chiasm above; cavernous sinus lateral (CN III/IV/VI + ICA).
- **Prolactin** = random level confirms. **GH (acromegaly)** = fails to suppress on OGTT + ↑IGF-1. **Cortisol (Cushing)** = dexamethasone suppression. **MRI** best (10% healthy have incidental lesions).
- **Macroadenoma >10mm; micro <10mm.**

## Tumour frequency
- Prolactinoma 35–40%, non-functioning 30–35%, GH 10–15%, ACTH 5–10%, TSH <5%.

## Key conditions
- **Hypopituitarism**: post-RT order GH→gonadotrophins→TSH→ACTH. Replace hydrocortisone, levothyroxine, sex steroids, GH.
- **Apoplexy** (EMERGENCY): infarction; macroadenoma, **Sheehan's** (post-partum); sudden headache/visual/CN palsy; **IV hydrocortisone life-saving**.
- **Prolactinoma**: hypogonadism ± galactorrhoea; **>5000 mIU/L = macroprolactinoma**; **dopamine agonist (cabergoline) first-line** (not surgery).
- **Acromegaly**: macroadenoma; prognathism, ↑hands/feet, carpal tunnel, **colon cancer 2–3×**; OGTT non-suppression + IGF-1; **trans-sphenoidal surgery** + somatostatin analogues + pegvisomant.
- **Cushing's disease**: ACTH microadenoma; trans-sphenoidal (70% remission); bilateral adrenalectomy → **Nelson's**.
- **Craniopharyngioma**: Rathke's pouch, calcified, cystic; causes DI + hypothalamic obesity; recurs.
- **NFA**: commonest macroadenoma; serial imaging.
- **Posterior pituitary**: ADH (collecting duct) + oxytocin.
- **Diabetes insipidus**: >3L dilute urine; cranial → **desmopressin**; nephrogenic → thiazide + indomethacin; fluid deprivation test; loss of posterior bright spot on MRI.

# 10. Reproductive System

## Physiology
- **Male**: LH → testosterone (Leydig); FSH → sperm (Sertoli); inhibin suppresses FSH.
- **Female**: FSH → follicle → oestradiol → **LH surge = ovulation** → corpus luteum → progesterone. **Day 21 progesterone >10 = ovulation.** Menopause: ↑LH/FSH (>30).

## Puberty & delayed puberty
- Delayed = no maturation by **14 (boys), 13 (girls)**.
- **Constitutional delay** = commonest, bone age < chronological, normal velocity.
- **Hypogonadotrophic** (low LH/FSH): Kallmann (GnRH deficiency + anosmia).
- **Hypergonadotrophic** (high LH/FSH): Klinefelter (boys), Turner (girls) → karyotype.

## Amenorrhoea
- Primary (never) vs secondary (stops). Exclude pregnancy. **Progestogen challenge**: PCOS bleeds, hypogonadotrophic doesn't. Uterus + oestrogen → must add progestogen.

## Male hypogonadism
- Low fasting 0900 testosterone; LH/FSH localizes. Testosterone replacement → check **PSA + Hb**; inhibits sperm.

## Infertility
- ≥12 months. 1/3 male factor. Semen analysis, day 21 progesterone, HSG (tubal). **Clomifene** (PCOS), FSH+hCG (gonadotrophin deficiency). **OHSS** = enlarged ovaries + capillary leak.

## PCOS
- **2 of 3 (Rotterdam)**: menstrual irregularity, androgen excess, polycystic ovaries. Insulin resistance; ↑androstenedione 80%; LH:FSH >2.5 in 60%. **Weight loss + metformin (cycles) / clomifene (fertility)**.

# 11. Thyroid Gland Diseases

## TFT interpretation
| TSH | T4 | T3 | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| Undetectable | High | High | Primary thyrotoxicosis |
| Undetectable | N | N | Subclinical thyrotoxicosis |
| High | Low | Low | Primary hypothyroidism |
| Normal/high | Low | Low | Secondary hypothyroidism |

## Thyrotoxicosis
- Causes: **Graves' 76%**, MNG 14%, adenoma 5%.
- **Graves'-specific**: ophthalmopathy, pretibial myxoedema, acropachy, **TRAb+**. (Lid lag = all.)
- **Ix**: ↑T3/T4, ↓TSH, TRAb, isotope scan. Low-uptake = thyroiditis. Factitious = ↓uptake + **T4:T3 >70:1** + ↓thyroglobulin.
- **Management**: β-blocker (symptoms). **Antithyroid (carbimazole/PTU)** — agranulocytosis (stop if sore throat/fever), **PTU in pregnancy**; >50% relapse. **Radioiodine** (CI pregnancy + active eye disease). Surgery (prep with potassium iodide).
- **Thyroid storm** (EMERGENCY, 10% mortality): infection-precipitated; propranolol + sodium ipodate + dexamethasone + carbimazole.
- **Ophthalmopathy**: smokers worse; selenium (mild), steroids (severe), decompression if acuity loss.

## Hypothyroidism
- **Hashimoto's + post-treatment = 90%**; women 6×; anti-TPO.
- **Clinical**: weight gain, cold intolerance, fatigue, menorrhagia, **loss lateral 1/3 eyebrows**, bradycardia, **delayed reflex relaxation**, carpal tunnel.
- **Ix**: **TSH >20, T3 unhelpful**.
- **Management**: levothyroxine 50→100–150mcg; recheck after 6 wks; don't oversuppress (osteoporosis/AF).
- **Myxoedema coma** (EMERGENCY, 50% mortality): elderly, depressed consciousness, temp 25°C; **IV T3 + hydrocortisone**, treat before labs.

# 12. Diabetes Mellitus

- DM = vascular disease (micro: eyes/kidney/nerves; macro: stroke/MI/limb).

## Type 1 vs 2
| | Type 1 | Type 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Cause | Autoimmune β-cell (anti-GAD) | Insulin resistance |
| Genetics | HLA DR3/4; twin ~30% | No HLA; **80% twin** |
| Body | Ketosis, weight loss | Obesity |

- **LADA** = slow type 1 adults; **MODY** = autosomal dominant young.

## Diagnosis
- Fasting ≥126, random ≥200, OGTT 2h ≥200, **HbA1c ≥6.5%** (prediabetes 5.7–6.4).
- HbA1c falsely low: cirrhosis, blood loss, transfusion → fructosamine.
- **C-peptide**: low = type 1, high = insulin resistance.

## Metabolic syndrome
- ≥3 of 5: central obesity, BP ≥130/85, TG ≥150, HDL <40M/<50F, fasting glucose ≥100.

## Drugs
| Class | Mechanism | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Metformin** | insulin sensitizer | **first-line**, no hypo; avoid eGFR ≤36 |
| Sulfonylureas | secretagogue | hypoglycaemia, weight gain |
| DPP-4i | ↑incretin | weight neutral |
| Glitazones | sensitizer | CI heart failure |
| **SGLT2i** | glycosuria | empagliflozin ↓CV death; **euglycaemic DKA** |
| GLP-1 | incretin mimetic | BMI >35; continue if HbA1c ↓1% + weight ↓3% |

- Insulin: rapid (lispro/aspart), long (glargine/detemir/degludec). Target **HbA1c <7%**. **Sick days: never stop insulin.**

# 13. Adrenal Gland Diseases

## Cushing's syndrome (cortisol excess)
- **Exclude iatrogenic first.** F:M 4:1; ectopic = M.
- **Symptoms**: moon face, central obesity, buffalo hump, purple striae, proximal myopathy, HTN, DM, osteoporosis.
- **2-step Ix**: confirm (LDDST, midnight cortisol, 24h UFC) → ACTH (**undetectable = adrenal**; high = pituitary/ectopic). HDDST suppresses pituitary not ectopic.
- **Ectopic ACTH**: pigmentation + hypokalaemic alkalosis + ↑↑ACTH.
- **Treatment**: trans-sphenoidal (Cushing's disease, 70%); metyrapone/ketoconazole; **Nelson's** after bilateral adrenalectomy.

## Adrenal insufficiency
- **Commonest = ACTH deficiency (secondary, steroid withdrawal).** Addison's (primary, 21-OH antibodies).
- **Crisis**: shock, hypoNa, **hyperK**, hypoglycaemia → **treat before labs** (IV hydrocortisone + saline).
- **Addison's**: pigmentation, vitiligo, weight loss; **short Synacthen test**; ACTH high.
- **APS-II (Schmidt)** = Addison + thyroid + T1DM.
- **Treatment**: hydrocortisone 15–20mg (all) + **fludrocortisone (primary only)**.

## Primary hyperaldosteronism
- Up to 10% of HTN; most bilateral hyperplasia, minority Conn's. **↑ARR** screening; hypokalaemic alkalosis. Spironolactone (gynaecomastia); adrenalectomy (HTN persists 70%).

## Phaeochromocytoma
- 80% adrenal, 15% malignant, 40% inherited (MEN 2, VHL, NF, SDH).
- **Triad: headache + sweating + palpitations**; episodic HTN.
- **Ix**: plasma/urine metanephrines; CT/MRI; MIBG.
- **Treatment**: **α-blocker (phenoxybenzamine) FIRST, then β-blocker. NEVER β before α.**

## CAH
- **Autosomal recessive; 21-hydroxylase deficiency commonest** → ↑17-OH-progesterone → androgen excess. Severe: ambiguous genitalia + salt-wasting. **17/11β-OH → HTN.**

# 14. Infective Endocarditis

- Microbial infection of valve/chamber/vessel. Case fatality ~20%.
- **Pathophysiology**: vegetation (organisms+fibrin+platelets) at endothelial damage. **S. aureus → normal heart; TV in IDU.**

## Microbiology
| Organism | Notes |
|---|---|
| **S. aureus** | acute, IDU 50%, destructive |
| Strep viridans | subacute, dental, PCN-sensitive |
| Strep **bovis** | **→ colon cancer (colonoscopy)** |
| Enterococci | +gentamicin synergy |
| Culture-negative | Coxiella, Bartonella, fungi |

## Clinical
- Fever, night sweats, **murmur 85%**. **Osler's nodes (painful), Janeway lesions (painless), Roth's spots, splinter haemorrhages.**

## Diagnosis (Modified Duke)
- **Major**: typical blood culture ×2 / persistent; echo vegetation or new regurgitation.
- **Minor**: predisposition, IDU, fever ≥38, embolic, vasculitic, suggestive culture/echo.
- **Diagnosis: 2 major / 1 major + 3 minor / 5 minor.**

## Treatment
- 4–6 wks. Empiric: **vancomycin + ceftriaxone**. MSSA → β-lactam; MRSA → vanc/dapto. Enterococci → ampicillin + gentamicin.
- **Surgery**: HF, abscess, persistent infection, large vegetation + emboli, S. aureus/fungal.
- **Prophylaxis**: only high-risk condition + high-risk procedure; **amoxicillin 2g** before dental.

# 15. Acute Abdomen

- Sudden, non-traumatic, severe abdominal pain <24h. ~40% need surgery. **Young → appendicitis; old → biliary/obstruction/ischaemia/diverticulitis.**

## Pain pathophysiology
- **Visceral**: midline, vague, dull (bilateral autonomic). **Parietal**: sharp, localized, worse on movement (somatic).
- **Embryological**: foregut → epigastric; midgut → periumbilical; hindgut → suprapubic.
- **Migration periumbilical → RIF = appendicitis.**

## History
- Sudden (perforation, ruptured AAA/ectopic, infarction); rapid (colic, inflammation); gradual (neoplasm, LBO).
- Vomit: bilious = obstruction; coffee-ground = gastritis; feculent = late obstruction.

## Examination
- **Inspect → Auscultate → Percuss → Palpate.** Peritonitis → lies still. **Loss of liver dullness = perforation.** Guarding (voluntary) vs rigidity (involuntary = peritonism). **qSOFA = 2 of: altered sensorium, tachypnoea, hypotension.**

## Investigations
- Bloods: leukocytosis, CRP, **lactate (ischaemia)**, lipase, **β-hCG (all women)**. **Erect CXR = free air (perforation)**; US best in pregnancy/gynae; CT best accuracy; CTA for mesenteric ischaemia.

## Management
- **ABC** + IVF + analgesia + NG + Foley + antibiotics. **Urgent surgery**: peritonitis signs, pneumoperitoneum, ischaemia, bleeding. **Don't operate**: appendicular mass, pancreatitis, diverticulitis, cholangitis, medical causes (MI, DKA, pyelonephritis).
- **NSAP** = commonest cause (esp children), self-resolving.

# 16. Fractures — Late Local Complications

## Perkins' timetable (normal)
| | Upper | Lower |
|---|---|---|
| Callus | 2–3 wks | 2–3 wks |
| Union | 4–6 wks | 8–12 wks |
| Consolidation | 6–8 wks | 12–16 wks |

## Complications
| Complication | Key features | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| **Delayed union** | slow; ↓blood supply, infection, intact fellow bone; little callus | conservative; if >6mo → fixation + graft |
| **Non-union** | never unites; **painless movement**; pseudoarthrosis; atrophic vs hypertrophic | hypertrophic → rigid fixation; atrophic → fixation + graft |
| **Malunion** | bad position; angulation >15°, shortening >3cm | osteotomy + IF; raised boot |
| **Joint stiffness** | post-immobilization; adhesions | prevent (exercise); MUA + CPM |
| **Myositis ossificans** | heterotopic ossification; elbow dislocation; **fluffy calcification** | rest → excise when stable |
| **AVN** | scaphoid/talus/femoral head/lunate; sickle/steroids/alcohol | avoid weight-bearing, graft, prosthesis |
| **Algodystrophy (CRPS)** | Sudeck's; burning pain; **patchy osteoporosis** | physio, sympathetic block (guanethidine) |
| **Post-traumatic OA** | months (direct) or years (secondary) | analgesia, osteotomy (young), arthroplasty (>60) |

- Also: Volkmann's contracture, tendon lesions, nerve entrapment, growth disturbance, bed sores.

# 17. Quick-Reference Topics

## PTH vs Glucagon
| | PTH | Glucagon |
|---|---|---|
| Made by | parathyroid glands | pancreas α-cells |
| Controls | **calcium** | **glucose** |
| Action | ↑Ca (bone, kidney, vit D) | ↑glucose (glycogenolysis, gluconeogenesis) |
| Opposed by | calcitonin | insulin |

## Calcitonin
- Secreted by **parafollicular C cells of thyroid**. Triggered by high calcium → **lowers Ca** (↓osteoclasts). Medullary thyroid carcinoma = C-cell tumour (MEN 2).

## Bone fracture healing — 5 stages
1. **Haematoma** (0–48h)
2. **Inflammation/granulation** (day 1–7)
3. **Soft callus (fibrocartilage)** (~2–3 wks)
4. **Hard callus (woven bone)** (3 wks–months)
5. **Remodelling** (lamellar bone, months–years)
- Secondary healing (callus) = most fractures; primary (direct) = rigid fixation.

## Crush vs Compartment syndrome
| | Compartment | Crush |
|---|---|---|
| Problem | **LOCAL** ↑pressure in fascial compartment | **SYSTEMIC** rhabdomyolysis |
| Danger | limb (muscle/nerve death) | life (hyperK, myoglobin AKI, shock) |
| Signs | **6 P's** (pain on passive stretch earliest) | tea-coloured urine, ↑CK, ↑K | 
| Treatment | **fasciotomy** | **IV fluids (before release), treat hyperK, dialysis** |
- Often coexist. Compartment = save limb; crush = save life.
