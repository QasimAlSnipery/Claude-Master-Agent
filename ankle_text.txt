Malleolar Fractures
Injuries about the ankle joint causes affection of not
only the bony architecture but also often of the
ligamentous and soft tissue components.

Mechanism of injury
• There may be fracture of one or both malleoli or one
malleolus and the opposite ligaments.
• If the malleolus pushed the fracture is oblique; if its
pulled the fracture is transverse.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 7
=====PAGE 8 [T]=====
Danis -Weber Classification
Based on the location and appearance of the fibular fracture:
• Type A: caused by internal rotation and adduction
that produce a transverse fracture of the lateral
malleolus at or below the plafond, with or without
an oblique fracture of the medial malleolus.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 8
=====PAGE 9 [T]=====
• Type B:
Caused by external rotation that results in an
oblique fracture of the lateral malleolus at the level of
syndesmosis.
The injury may include rupture or avulsion of the anteroinferior
tibiofibular ligament, transverse fracture of the medial
malleolus, or rupture of the deltoid ligament.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 9
=====PAGE 10 [T]=====
• Type C:
caused by abduction injuries with oblique fracture
of the fibula proximal to the disrupted tibiofibular ligaments
;medial malleolar fracture or a deltoid ligament rupture.
Fracture of the posterior malleolus may accompany type C
fractures.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 10
=====PAGE 11 [T]=====
Clinical Features
Skiers, footballers, climbers and RTA.
Pain, inability to stand, swelling, deformity,
ecchymosis and skin blistering.

Treatment
Principles:
1. Don’t delay.
2. Treat the bony and ligament injuries.
3. Accurate reduction and maintaining it.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 13
=====PAGE 14 [T]=====
Conservative Treatments:
• Isolated non displaced medial malleolar fracture.
• Isolated non displaced lateral malleolar fractures.
• Posterior malleolar fracture <25% of articular
surface, <2mm step off.
Below knee posterior
slab for 5 days with elevation,
then check X-ray if still non
displaced full POP cast for
6-8 weeks.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 14
=====PAGE 15 [T]=====
• Operative treatments:
For displaced type A& B and all type C Injuries.
Open reduction and internal fixation in steps:
1. Lateral malleolus:
Perfect reduction and fixation by plate and screws (most important
step).
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 15
=====PAGE 16 [T]=====
2. Medial malleolus:
Reduction after removing the periostium and fixation by two
malleolar screws, k wires and tension band.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 16
=====PAGE 17 [T]=====
3. Syndesmodic screw if the syndesmoses is unstable.
4. Posterior malleolus if large fix by plate and screw..
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 17
=====PAGE 18 [T]=====
Postoperative treatment:
Below knee posterior slab for 5 days with elevation, followed by non weight bearing
for 3-4 weeks then partial wt bearing for 6-8 weeks.
Complications:
Early
1. Vascular injury: if fracture subluxation of the ankle
joint.
2. Wound breakdown and infection.
Late:
1. Malunion.
2. Non union (medial malleolus).
3. Stiffness.
4. Osteoarthritis.
