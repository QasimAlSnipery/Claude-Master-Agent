# Orthopaedic Conditions of the Hip

## The Big Picture
Three big topics: **AVN (osteonecrosis)** of the femoral head, **hip osteoarthritis (OA)**, and **total hip replacement (THR)** — the "operation of the century." Common thread: dying or worn-out femoral head → pain + lost function → staged treatment from drugs to joint replacement.

---

## Avascular Necrosis (Hip Osteonecrosis)
- **What** — reduced blood flow to femoral head → bone death → collapse.
- **Who** — male > female, **age 35–50**; **bilateral 80%** of the time. ~10% of all hip arthroplasties.
- **Causes** — direct: trauma (injures **medial femoral circumflex**), irradiation, leukaemia/lymphoma, **Caisson/decompression (the bends)**, Gaucher's, **sickle cell**. Indirect: **alcohol, steroids, SLE**, hypercoagulable, transplant, HIV/protease inhibitors, idiopathic.
- **Pathophysiology** — intravascular coagulation → venous thrombosis → ↑ intraosseous pressure → ↓ flow → AVN → **chondral fracture & collapse**.
- **Presentation** — insidious **anterior hip pain**, worse on stairs/impact; exam normal early, later like OA (↓ internal rotation).
- **Imaging** — X-ray (AP + frog-lateral); **MRI = 99% sens/spec**, "double density," T1 dark band, T2 bright (marrow oedema = predicts progression).

### Steinberg Classification (modified Ficat)
- 0 normal | I normal X-ray, abnormal MRI | II cyst/sclerosis | **III crescent sign (subchondral collapse)** | IV head flattening | V joint narrowing | VI advanced degeneration.

### AVN Treatment (stage-driven)
- **Bisphosphonates** — pre-collapse (Ficat 0–II).
- **Core decompression** ± bone graft — early, pre-collapse, reversible cause; relieves pressure + triggers angiogenesis.
- **Rotational osteotomy** — small lesions (<15%), rotate off weight-bearing zone.
- **Curettage + graft** — lightbulb (through neck-head junction) or trapdoor (through articular surface).
- **Vascularised free-fibula transfer** — young patient, pre/post collapse; donor-site morbidity.
- **Total hip replacement** — crescent sign/collapse, irreversible cause (chronic steroids), >40 with large lesions. Young AVN patients get **more liner wear/osteolysis** than older OA patients.

## Hip Osteoarthritis
- **What** — degenerative loss of femoral head + acetabular cartilage.
- **Risk** — modifiable (trauma, muscle weakness, heavy work, high-impact sport); non-modifiable (**female > male**, age, genetics, dysplasia, SCFE, Perthes).
- **Pathology** — ↑ cartilage water, ↓ proteoglycans; **MMP/TIMP imbalance**; cytokines IL-1, IL-6, TNF-α. Late: subchondral cysts + sclerosis.
- **Presentation** — function-limiting **groin pain**, ↓ walking distance, night/rest pain, stiffness, **limited internal rotation**, flexion contracture.
- **Imaging** — standing AP pelvis: **joint space narrowing, osteophytes, subchondral sclerosis + cysts**.

### Tonnis Classification
- **0 normal** | 1 sclerosis + slight narrowing + lipping | 2 large cysts + severe narrowing + ↓ sphericity | 3 severe head deformity.

### OA Treatment
- **Non-op (first line)** — NSAIDs/tramadol, **walking stick in opposite hand**, weight loss/exercise/PT, steroid injection (Dx + Rx).
- **Operative** — arthroscopic debridement (labral tears), **periacetabular osteotomy** (young dysplasia, delays THR), hip resurfacing (young active males), **THA** (end-stage, preferred >50).

## Total Hip Replacement (THR)
- **The 4 demands** — implant durable, **low-friction** articulation, firmly fixed to bone, inert (no tissue reaction).
- **History** — Gluck ivory (1891), Austin Moore metal hemiarthroplasty (1940), **Sir John Charnley low-friction arthroplasty (1960s)** = small metal head + polyethylene cup + acrylic cement. **~80% survival at 25 years**.
- **Indications** — pain, lost movement, disability + radiographic joint destruction.
- **Contraindication** — **sepsis (overt or latent)** is the chief one — infected arthroplasty = disaster. <60yo only if no alternative.
- **Biomechanics depend on** — design, bearing surface/lubrication, fixation.
- **Fixation** — press-fit (biologic, hoop stresses) vs cemented (grout; preferred for irradiated bone; cobalt-chrome commonest).
- **Bearing surfaces** — metal-on-polyethylene, metal-on-metal, ceramic-on-ceramic, ceramic-on-poly, titanium-on-poly.
- **Complications** — **Acute:** infection, dislocation, sciatic nerve palsy, vascular injury, periprosthetic fracture, leg-length discrepancy. **Chronic:** **aseptic loosening**, iliopsoas impingement, heterotopic ossification, infection.

## Walk-Away Points
- AVN: bilateral 80%, alcohol/steroid/sickle cell; MRI 99%; **crescent sign = collapse → THR**.
- Stage AVN treatment: pre-collapse = core decompression; collapsed/old = THR.
- Hip OA: groin pain + lost internal rotation; **Tonnis** grading; non-op first, THA >50.
- THR: **Charnley low-friction**, ~80% at 25yr; **sepsis = chief contraindication**; aseptic loosening = main long-term failure.
