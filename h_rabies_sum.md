# Rabies

## The Big Picture
Rabies = an **almost-always-fatal viral encephalitis** from a **bullet-shaped RNA Lyssavirus (Rhabdoviridae)**, transmitted by **animal bites/licks/scratches** (dogs, bats). Once symptoms appear, mortality is ~**100%** — so everything rides on **post-exposure prophylaxis (wound care + vaccine + immunoglobulin)** during the long incubation period.

## Virus & Pathogenesis
- **Lyssavirus, Rhabdoviridae**, bullet-shaped RNA. Zoonotic; all mammals susceptible, few are reservoirs (dogs, bats).
- Virus multiplies in muscle → travels up **peripheral nerves to CNS** (encephalitis) → spreads out to **salivary glands** (+ pancreas, kidney, retina, heart). **Not found in blood.**
- **Negri bodies** — eosinophilic cytoplasmic inclusions in neurons (cerebellum/hippocampus), **pathognomonic** (absent in 20%).

## Incubation
- **Average 20–90 days** (1 week–19 years). **Shorter if:** bite near head/neck/upper limb, children, severe lacerations, high inoculum, virulent strain, low immunity. (Distance from bite to CNS determines it.)

## Clinical (3 phases)
1. **Prodrome (2–10 d):** fever, malaise, nausea, **paraesthesia/pain/itch at wound site**.
2. **Acute neurologic:**
   - **Encephalitic / "furious" (80%):** hyperexcitability, agitation, hallucinations, **lucid intervals**, autonomic dysfunction (salivation/foaming, sweating), **hydrophobia + aerophobia** (painful pharyngeal spasms on swallowing).
   - **Paralytic / "dumb" (20%):** flaccid ascending paralysis from bitten limb; no hydrophobia; longer survival (bat rabies, partially vaccinated).
3. **Coma → death** (within 14 days; paralytic up to 30 days). Recovery extremely rare.

## Diagnosis
- **DFA (direct fluorescent antibody)** for nucleoprotein antigen = **gold standard** (skin/nape hair follicle, corneal smear).
- **CSF antibodies** appear early + only in infection (not vaccination); serum antibodies appear late/post-vaccine.
- **RT-PCR** (saliva, skin, brain) = most sensitive/specific.
- **Negri bodies** — postmortem, pathognomonic.

## Management & Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP)
- **No effective treatment after symptoms** — supportive only.
- **PEP (during incubation) is life-saving:**
  1. **Local wound care** — wash thoroughly with **soap + water 15 min**, then povidone-iodine/alcohol. **Do NOT suture** (spreads virus); no bare-hand contact.
  2. **Rabies vaccine** (HDCV/RAV) — IM deltoid.
  3. **Rabies immunoglobulin (RIG)** — **HRIG 20 IU/kg**, infiltrate around wound (passive neutralisation); within 24 h (up to day 7).
  4. Tetanus prophylaxis + antibiotics.
- **Pre-exposure prophylaxis** — for high-risk people: vaccine days 0, 7, 21/28.

## Walk-Away Points
- **Lyssavirus, bite/lick transmission, travels up nerves to CNS**; mortality ~100% once symptomatic.
- **Negri bodies pathognomonic**; **DFA gold standard**, RT-PCR most sensitive.
- **Furious (80%) = hydrophobia/aerophobia**; dumb (20%) = paralysis.
- **PEP = wash 15 min + don't suture + vaccine + RIG**; preventable if given early.
