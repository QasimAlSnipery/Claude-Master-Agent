# Brucellosis

## The Big Picture
Brucellosis = systemic bacterial **zoonosis** from *Brucella*, caught from **infected animals or unpasteurised dairy**. It's the classic **"undulant fever"** — a flu-like illness with rising/falling fever, sweats, back pain, and **spinal/joint involvement**. An **occupational** disease (farmers, vets, abattoir, lab staff). Diagnosis is hard (slow culture + serology); treatment needs **dual antibiotics for weeks**.

## Organism & Species
- ***Brucella*** — Gram-negative **coccobacilli**, **facultative intracellular** (multiply in monocyte-macrophages); resist phagocytosis (key virulence). Slow, scanty growth (colonies 14 days–4 weeks); need enriched media (blood/chocolate/MacConkey).
- **Species → animal:** **B. melitensis** (sheep/goats — most virulent), **B. abortus** (cattle), B. suis (pigs), B. canis (dogs).

## Transmission
- **Contact** with infected animal fluids/blood/placenta (via cuts), **unpasteurised dairy** (milk, cheese, butter, ice cream), undercooked meat, **inhalation** of aerosols.
- **Occupational:** vets, farmers, abattoir/lab workers, hunters, butchers.
- Person-to-person rare (mother-child, sexual documented).

## Clinical
- **Incubation 5–30 days** (up to 6 months); may persist months without symptoms.
- **Non-specific flu-like:** malaise, fever, **profuse sweats**, anorexia, headache, **back pain**, joint pain, weakness, depression.
- **Undulant fever** — temperature repeatedly rises (peaks evening) then falls.
- **Organ involvement:** **spondylitis (spinal arthritis — confused with disc prolapse), sacroiliitis, arthritis, endocarditis**, hepatosplenomegaly, neurologic.
- **Case fatality <2%**; recovery usual but disability/relapse common.

## Diagnosis
- **History crucial** (occupation, animal exposure, unpasteurised dairy, travel).
- **Culture** (blood/bone marrow/tissue) = most definitive but slow/low sensitivity; take **2–3 peripheral blood cultures**; bone marrow more sensitive.
- **Serology:** **Rose Bengal test, ELISA** (rapid screen); IgM (week 1) → IgG (week 2). **PCR** = faster DNA detection.
- Imaging (echo, bone scan, MRI/X-ray) for organ involvement.

## Treatment
- **Dual antibiotics** (combination beats monotherapy — fewer relapses):
  - **Doxycycline 100 mg BD × 6 weeks** + **either streptomycin** (IM × 2–3 wk) **or rifampicin** (600–900 mg/day × 6 wk).

## Prevention
- **Pasteurise milk**; avoid unpasteurised dairy.
- **Animal control:** vaccinate cattle, test-and-slaughter infected herds (B. abortus eradicated in many developed countries).
- **PPE** (gloves/apron) handling animals/tissue; biosafety in labs/abattoirs; hand hygiene; cook meat thoroughly.

## Walk-Away Points
- **Zoonosis from animals + unpasteurised dairy**; occupational; **B. melitensis** most virulent.
- **Undulant fever** + sweats + **back pain/spondylitis**; intracellular → relapses.
- Diagnose: **culture (slow) + serology (Rose Bengal/ELISA) + PCR**; take multiple blood cultures.
- Treat: **dual antibiotics weeks (doxycycline + rifampicin/streptomycin)**; prevent by pasteurisation.
