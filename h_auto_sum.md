# Autoimmune Diseases

## The Big Picture
Autoimmunity = the immune system attacks **"self"** after a **breakdown of self-tolerance**. Some autoreactivity is normal, but disease occurs when **autoreactive T and B lymphocytes get activated**. Driven by failure of **tolerance checkpoints** + genetic (HLA) + environmental factors. Tissue damage is via **chronic inflammation** or **autoantibodies**. Over 80 diseases are classed autoimmune.

## Tolerance Checkpoints (and their failures)
1. **Thymic deletion** of high-self-affinity TCRs — needs thymic self-antigen expression. Failure: **AIRE gene defect → autoimmune polyglandular syndrome type 1**.
2. **Regulatory T cells (Tregs)** — **FoxP3 defect → IPEX syndrome**; subtle Treg defects in T1DM, MS, RA.
3. **Need for a danger signal** to activate dendritic cells/CD4 T cells.
- (Also **ALPS** — Fas/FasL defect → can't switch off activated T cells → cytopenias + lymphoproliferation; e.g. Evans syndrome.)

## How Self-Reactive CD4 T Cells Get Activated
- **Bystander activation** — infection releases hidden self-antigens + danger signals.
- **Molecular mimicry** — pathogen resembles self-antigen.
- **Post-translational modification** — self-protein altered to look foreign (e.g. **citrullination in rheumatoid arthritis**).
- Usually multiple subtle defects combine.

## Mechanisms of Tissue Damage
### 1. Chronic inflammation (T-cell driven)
- Th1/CTL/Th17 cells → cytokines → recruit macrophages / direct toxicity. E.g. **T1DM** (β cells killed by IL-1β, IFN-γ, IL-17), **multiple sclerosis**.
- **IgG4-related disease** — Th2-driven fibro-inflammatory swellings (autoimmune pancreatitis etc.).

### 2. Autoantibody-mediated
- **Direct binding/destruction:** **myasthenia gravis** (anti-acetylcholine receptor), **autoimmune haemolytic anaemia** (anti-RBC), **Goodpasture's** (anti-GBM).
- **Immune complexes:** deposit in tissues (kidney) → **SLE** (autoantigen = DNA).
- **Receptor stimulation:** **Graves' disease** (anti-TSH-receptor antibody stimulates thyroid → hyperthyroidism).

## Diagnostic Criteria
- **Major:** autoreactivity (autoantibodies/T cells), response to immunosuppression, disease transferable by the immune effector (hardest).
- **Minor:** animal model, transfer in model, **HLA association** (frequent clue).

## Diagnosis (Lab)
Autoantibody detection (ANA, anti-dsDNA, RF, anti-CCP, etc.), HLA typing, inflammatory markers — matched to the suspected disease.

## Walk-Away Points
- Autoimmunity = **loss of self-tolerance**; checkpoints = thymic deletion (**AIRE**), **Tregs (FoxP3/IPEX)**, danger signal.
- Triggers: **molecular mimicry, bystander activation, post-translational (citrullination → RA)**.
- Damage: **T-cell chronic inflammation (T1DM, MS)** vs **autoantibody** (myasthenia = anti-AChR, Graves = anti-TSHR, SLE = anti-DNA immune complexes, Goodpasture = anti-GBM).
- **HLA association** is a hallmark; treat with immunosuppression.
