# PEDIATRIC GASTROENTEROLOGY

## Abdominal pain — pathophysiology (3 categories)
- **Visceral (splanchnic) pain**: visceral fibres **bilateral + unmyelinated**, enter spinal cord at multiple levels → **dull, poorly localized, midline**. Triggered by ischaemia, inflammation, stretch of peritoneum.
- **Parietal (somatic) pain**: stimulation of **parietal peritoneum** → **sharp, intense, discrete, localized**; worse on coughing/movement. Transmitted via myelinated fibres to specific dorsal root ganglia (same side + dermatome).
- **Referred pain**: felt at site distant from organ (shared neuronal pathways). Classic = **pneumonia presents with abdominal pain** (T9 dermatome shared by lung + abdomen).

## Acute abdominal pain in children
- **Most common medical cause = gastroenteritis** (viruses: rotavirus, norovirus, adenovirus, enterovirus; bacteria: E. coli, Yersinia, Campylobacter, Salmonella, Shigella).
- **Causes by age/type**:
  - **Acute, child <2 yrs**: surgical (**malrotation, volvulus, intussusception, appendicitis, necrotising enterocolitis, incarcerated inguinal hernia**); medical (GE, UTI, hepatitis, pneumonia, bacterial peritonitis).
  - **Chronic, child >2 yrs**: surgical (appendicitis, Meckel's diverticulum, peritonitis, cholecystitis); medical (GE, UTI, lead poisoning, **non-specific abdominal pain, IBD, mesenteric lymphadenitis**).
- **DDx list**: infantile colic, constipation, mesenteric adenitis, trauma, intestinal obstruction, PID, UTI, Meckel's.
- **"The pain": use analgesia as required — it does NOT affect diagnostic accuracy.**

### Specific causes
- **Infantile colic**: up to **10% of infants**; first 6 months; scream + draw knees up; paroxysmal crying (evening), relieved by flatus/stool; normal feeding/exam, no associated symptoms.
- **Constipation**: over-diagnosed; can be visceral/LIF; acute (organic) or chronic (functional); diagnosis of last resort.
- **Mesenteric adenitis**: preceding **URTI/adenovirus**; mimics appendicitis (more diffuse pain, higher temperature, **shifting tenderness**, other lymphadenopathy).
- **Abdominal trauma**: accidental vs **non-accidental (parents may conceal info)**.
- **Intestinal obstruction**: malrotation, volvulus, **intussusception**, **incarcerated hernia, adhesions (acquired or congenital)**.

## Appendicitis (detailed)
- **Types**: acute (inflammatory/suppurative), perforated/complicated (perforated, gangrenous), or normal. Mass → **appendix mass / appendix abscess**.
- **Pathogenesis**: primary luminal obstruction (filled with mucus, swells) → ↑pressure → vessel compression → ischaemia/necrosis (gangrene) → bacterial invasion → **perforation → peritonitis/abscess → septicaemia**.
- **Symptoms**: **central pain (vague) → moves to RIF (localized, sharp, constant, wants to lie still)**; anorexia, nausea, vomiting, mild pyrexia, change in bowel habit (diarrhoea/constipation), urinary symptoms.
- **Signs**: localized tenderness + guarding, **Rovsing's sign**, **psoas sign** (pain on hip extension), **obturator sign** (pain on hip internal rotation), caecal gurgle, **McBurney's point**, rebound tenderness.
- **Appendix positions**: **retrocaecal (poor localizing signs), retroileal (diarrhoea), pelvic (diarrhoea/bladder symptoms)**.
- **Investigations**: bloods (**neutrophil leucocytosis, lymphopenia, ↑CRP**, sickle test), radiology (AXR — obstruction, faecolith, soft tissue mass, loaded colon; **US** — gynaecological, abscess/mass/thick bowel loops, stones).
- **Treatment**: prep for theatre (analgesia, fluids, antibiotics, consent) → appendicectomy.

## Henoch-Schönlein purpura (HSP)
- **Systemic vasculitis**; **IgA immune complex deposition in skin + kidney**; **young children**. Self-limiting, no treatment (symptom control). 1/3 relapse; **irreversible kidney damage ~1%**. Post-viral/bacterial / drug reaction.

## Vomiting in children
- **By cause (colour of vomit)**:
  - **Obstruction proximal to duodenum** (pyloric stenosis/IHPS, achalasia, web): **non-bilious**.
  - **Obstruction distal to duodenum** (congenital/acquired obstruction, foreign body): **bilious**.
  - **Reflux/GER**: non-bilious.
  - **CNS** (meningitis, encephalitis, head injury, hydrocephalus): non-bilious.
  - Renal (renal failure, calculi), endocrine/metabolic.

## GER & GERD
- **GER** = physiological reflux. **GERD** = pathologic.
- **Why GER becomes GERD**: aberrance in normal physiology (insufficient clearance/buffering, ↓gastric emptying, abnormal epithelial repair, ↓neural reflexes) → **erosive oesophagitis → oesophageal shortening → hiatal herniation**.
- **Diagnostic approach**: symptoms/signs, history/exam, **upper GI series, oesophageal pH monitoring** (detects acid reflux + temporal symptom association; can't detect non-acid reflux or complications), endoscopy + biopsy, empirical medical therapy.
- **Conservative therapy**:
  - **Infants**: normalize feeding volume/frequency, **thickened formula**, non-prone positioning during sleep, hypoallergenic formula trial.
  - **Older children**: avoid large meals, don't lie down after eating, lose weight, avoid caffeine/chocolate/spicy, **avoid tobacco smoke**.

## Constipation
- **Definition**: hard stool passed with difficulty **every 3 days, persisting ≥2 weeks**.
- **Organic causes**: structural (anal stenosis, fissure), neuromuscular (**Hirschsprung disease, spina bifida**), metabolic (hypokalaemia, hypercalcaemia, **hypothyroidism**, DM), drugs (narcotics, anticholinergic, lead), intestinal (IBD, coeliac, CF, tumours).
- **Functional**: healthy child; painful defecation episode → withholding; toilet training; school toilet avoidance. **Stool-withholding** (stiffen legs, squat, push, cry — misinterpreted as straining). + abdominal pain, distension, flatulence.
- **Clinical**: rectal bleeding, poor appetite, enuresis, UTI; **soiling (overflow incontinence)** in long-standing. Exam: distension, **palpable faecal masses**, DRE (sphincter, ampulla).
- **Functional diagnosis**: favourable response to treatment supports it; refractory → **barium enema + anorectal manometry** (rule out organic, e.g. Hirschsprung).
- **Bristol stool chart**: Type 1 (separate hard lumps — very constipated) → Type 7 (liquid — inflammation). Types 3–4 normal.
- **Treatment (functional)**:
  1. **Patient education + regular bowel training** (toilet sitting 5–10 min after meals).
  2. **Disimpaction**: **enema or polyethylene glycol (PEG)**.
  3. **Stool softening**: lactulose, high fibre. **Maintenance: osmotic laxatives (lactulose, PEG/Movicol), stool softeners (docusate), stimulant laxatives, lubricants (mineral oil)** until regular pattern established (several months).
- **Squatting = only natural defecation posture** (anorectal angle).

## High-yield one-liners
- **Abdominal pain: visceral = midline/dull; parietal = localized/sharp; referred (pneumonia → abdomen).**
- **Acute abdominal pain commonest medical cause = gastroenteritis; analgesia doesn't affect diagnosis.**
- **Appendicitis: central → RIF migration; Rovsing's/psoas/obturator; retrocaecal = poor signs.**
- **Mesenteric adenitis: post-URTI, shifting tenderness, mimics appendicitis. HSP = IgA vasculitis, skin + kidney.**
- **Vomiting: bilious = distal obstruction; non-bilious = pyloric/GER/CNS.**
- **GER vs GERD; pH monitoring; infants → thickened formula + positioning.**
- **Constipation = hard stool every 3 days ≥2 weeks; functional commonest; PEG/enema disimpaction; Bristol chart; Hirschsprung if organic suspected.**
