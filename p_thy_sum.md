# Thyroid Diseases (Dr Sarmad)

## The Big Picture
Butterfly gland, front of neck, **10–20 g**, makes **T4 + T3** under **TSH** (anterior pituitary) control via negative feedback; **TRH** from hypothalamus drives TSH. **Calcitonin** (parafollicular C-cells) lowers calcium. Master the **hyper- vs hypothyroid** picture, the TFT pattern, the **thyroid storm** emergency, and **thyroidectomy complications**.

## Axis & Hormones
- Thyroid → T4 + T3; controlled by **TSH** (negative feedback).
- **Hyperthyroid → TSH suppressed; hypothyroid → TSH stimulated.**
- Calcitonin (C-cells) — stimulated by hypercalcaemia, lowers serum Ca.

## Hyperthyroidism
- **Symptoms:** weight loss, ↑ appetite, anxiety, irritability, heat intolerance, **tachycardia, tremor**; **Graves = eye signs**.
- **Causes (primary):** **Graves disease**, toxic multinodular goitre, toxic adenoma.
- **Graves ophthalmopathy:** eyelid retraction, **proptosis**, periorbital oedema, chemosis (orbital fat/muscle inflammation).
- **Secondary:** pituitary adenoma (TSH-secreting).
- **Without hyperfunction:** subacute thyroiditis (leak), iatrogenic overdose.

## Hypothyroidism
- **Symptoms:** fatigue, ↓ appetite, **weight gain, constipation, cold intolerance, dry skin**.
- **Primary atrophic:** **Hashimoto** (autoimmune), end-stage Graves, iatrogenic (**I-131, thyroidectomy, radiation**), biosynthesis defects, iodine deficiency/excess, agenesis.
- **Secondary:** pituitary (panhypopituitarism, isolated TSH deficiency), hypothalamic.
- **Transient:** silent/subacute thyroiditis, thyroxine withdrawal.
- Early detection in childhood prevents **mental retardation** + oral/skeletal complications.

## Thyroiditis (causes)
Acute suppurative, subacute painful, subacute painless, **Hashimoto**, chronic fibrosing (**Riedel**).

## Investigations
- **TFT:** T3, T4, TSH.
  - **Hyperthyroid:** ↑T3, ↑T4, **↓TSH**.
  - **Hypothyroid:** ↓T3, ↓T4, **↑TSH**.
- Neck **ultrasound**; **FNAC** (cytology of nodules); **thyroid scan** (Tc-99m — toxic adenoma = hot nodule).

## Thyroid Storm (Thyrotoxic Crisis) — emergency
- Life-threatening sudden hormone surge complicating hyperthyroidism.
- **Triggers:** untreated/poorly controlled hyperthyroid, stress, infection, trauma, certain drugs.
- **Features:** **high fever, tachycardia, agitation/confusion, vomiting/diarrhoea, muscle weakness, coma**.
- **Treatment:** admit; **β-blockers, antithyroid drugs, iodine, glucocorticoids**.

## Thyroidectomy Complications
- **Hypocalcaemia = most common** (parathyroid injury) → calcium + vitamin D.
- **Recurrent laryngeal nerve** injury → hoarseness (often recovers).
- **Bleeding/haematoma** → airway pressure → **emergency re-operation**.
- Less common: **superior laryngeal nerve** (voice quality/high notes), infection, oesophageal/tracheal injury, thyroid storm, hypothyroidism (if total + no replacement), seroma, surgical emphysema (transoral).

## Walk-Away Points
- **Hyper:** ↑T3/T4, **↓TSH**, Graves + eye signs. **Hypo:** ↓T3/T4, **↑TSH**, Hashimoto.
- **Thyroid storm** = fever + tachycardia + confusion → β-blocker + antithyroid + iodine + steroid.
- Thyroidectomy: **hypocalcaemia commonest**, RLN → hoarseness, **haematoma → airway emergency**.
