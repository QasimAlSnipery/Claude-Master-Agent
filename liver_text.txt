Acute parenchymal liver disease
• There is sudden widespread liver damage in which variable
numbers of hepatocytes undergo necrosis.

• 1- viral hepatitis, A, B, C, D, E, CMV, EBV, yellow fever.

Drug, Toxins & the liver
The liver is the main organ in which drugs are metabolized &
consequently is important in determining the effects
of drugs in the body.
Hepatic drug metabolism involves the conversion of fat-soluble
(non-polar) drugs into H2O- soluble (polar) metabolite
Then can be excreted in the bile if they are relatively large
molecule
Or
in the urine, if they are relatively small.
.

Two-types reaction takes place during hepatic drug
metabolism:
Type 1 reaction: mainly involve oxidation or reduction to
increase drug polarity & provide the drug for subsequent type
2 reactions.
Type 2: involve conjugation ( eg. Glucuronation,
Methylation, Sulfation….
, which lead to the production of highly polar metabolite,
which are excreted in bile or urine
=====PAGE 7 [TEXT]=====
The rate of drug metabolism vary depending on:
1. Genetic factor
2. Nutritional factors.
3. Induction or inhibition of the liver enzymes.
4. Numbers of drugs are given simultaneously.
5. Hepatic blood flow.
Drugs which are hepatic enzyme inducer:
Alcohol, Barbaturate, Carbamazepin, Phenytion,
Rifampicin, Primidone.
Drugs which are hepatic enzyme inhibitor: Cimetidine,
INH, Ketoconazole.

HEPATOTOXIC DRUG REACTIONS
 Possibility of Drug toxicity should be high in the differential
diagnosis of acute liver failure, jaundice and abnormal liver
biochemistry.
 the most common picture is a mixed cholestatic hepatitis. The
presence of jaundice indicates more severe liver damage.
Although acute liver failure can occur,
 most drug reactions are acute and self-limiting; chronic liver
damage is rare

Acute hepatic damage:
Is the best-recognized form of drug induced liver injury,
such damage may be:
1- dose- related & predictable; hepatotoxicity is mediated
biochemically .e.g./paracetamol poisoning.
2- unrelated to dose & unpredictable or idiosyncratic;
(infrequent, occur at any time, associated with fever, rash,
arthralgia & eosinophilia
They are attributed to immunological injury , may be associated
with Antibody- formation.eg. halothane hepatitis.
Jundice is usually present& is usually indistinguishable from viral
hepatitis.

DRUGS TO AVOID IN CIRRHOSIS
• Most analgesics can precipitate complications
and need to be used cautiously in cirrhosis .
• NSAIDs are hepatotoxic and can potentiate
Hepatorenal failure.
• Paracetamol
up to a dose of 3 g/day can be used
safely in chronic liver disease, but higher doses may
result in acute hepatic necrosis, particularly in patients
with alcoholic liver disease
