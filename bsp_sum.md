# BILIARY SYSTEM, SPLEEN & PANCREAS — Imaging (Radiology)

## Overview
- **CT = mainstay** for inflammatory, neoplastic, traumatic conditions. **US = best for gallstones.** **MRI = no ionizing radiation.**

## BILIARY SYSTEM — modalities
- **US = primary + best all-purpose** imaging.
- **Radionuclide (HIDA)**: key role in **biliary atresia + cystic duct obstruction**.
- **CT**: shows stones, biliary dilatation, wall thickening — but expensive + radiation.
- **MRCP**: non-invasive, **has largely replaced diagnostic PTC/ERCP**.

### US of GB & biliary system
- Stones **>1–2 mm** seen. CBD/CHD almost always seen (except distal end). **Normal intrahepatic ducts NOT seen** (only proximal few mm).
- **Normal CBD ≤7 mm. Normal wall <3 mm. Fasting needed.**

### Radionuclide (Tc-IDA)
- Fast 4h → IV Tc-labelled IDA. Normally **GB + CBD + duodenum seen within 1 hour**. **Cystic duct obstruction = CBD + duodenum seen but GB NOT seen.**

### ERCP
- Endoscope + catheter; **mainly therapeutic** now. **Complications: pancreatitis, perforation, bleeding, sepsis.**

### PTC
- Alternative to ERCP; **better with dilated ducts**. **Complications: bleeding, septicaemia, biliary peritonitis.**

## Gallstones & GB pathology
- **Gallstones**: 10–20% radio-opaque (plain film); **60–80% seen on CT**. **US: strongly echogenic foci + acoustic shadowing**; US **not reliable for bile duct stones**.
- **GB polyp**: mucosal echogenic focus, **NO posterior shadowing** (vs stone); mostly few mm; **>10 mm → indication for cholecystectomy**.
- **Acute cholecystitis (US)**: **thick wall >3 mm, distended >40 mm, sludge/stones, wall oedema, positive sonographic Murphy's sign**. **Wall oedema differentiates acute from chronic.**

## Jaundice — role of imaging
- Determine: obstructive or not (US/CT/MRCP), **site**, **cause** — commonly **CBD stone, carcinoma head of pancreas, ampullary**.
- **Obstructive jaundice → biliary dilatation**; may be **no intrahepatic dilatation in first 48h**; obstruction without dilatation in liver transplants.
- **US more sensitive than CT for biliary dilatation.** **ERCP/PTC = sometimes diagnostic, mainly therapeutic.** ("Double channel sign" = dilated CBD + pancreatic duct.)

## SPLEEN
- **US: iso-echoic to liver.** CT/MRI good evaluation.
- **Common masses**: cyst (**hydatid**), abscess, **tumour — lymphoma > metastases**.
- **Splenomegaly >13 cm** (texture may be normal): **lymphoma, portal hypertension, chronic infection, haemolytic anaemia, leukaemia**.
- **Splenic trauma**: **most commonly injured organ in blunt trauma**; contusion/laceration/haematoma. US useful; **CT = standard** (also bleeding + other organ injury, esp. liver + left kidney).

## PANCREAS
- **CT = main imaging (not affected by air).** US limited (absorbed by air). MRI/MRCP, angiography, ERCP occasional. **Normal duct 2 mm.**
- Oblique orientation; variable shape/size (measurements not useful); **feathery outline (CT)**, medium/high echogenicity (US); atrophy common with age.

### Pancreatic masses
- Carcinoma, lymph node, abscess/pseudocyst, focal pancreatitis.
- **Adenocarcinoma = commonest**; **2/3 in head**. **Head tumour → early diagnosis (pancreatic + biliary duct dilatation)**; **tail → late diagnosis (large + advanced)**.
- **CA pancreas (CT)**: focal mass deforming outline, **irregular obliteration of peripancreatic fat**; low-density tumour, lymph nodes, duct dilatation, liver involvement, **vascular encasement/thrombosis**.
- **Endocrine tumours**: small, don't deform outline → hard to detect; **hypervascular → selective angiography, dynamic CT/MRI, intraoperative US** show small round mass.

### Pancreatitis
- **Acute**: variable (necrosis/haemorrhage/suppuration). **Pancreas enlarged (often diffuse), low-density/echogenicity areas (oedema + necrosis)**. Imaging role: **stage (CT severity index/CTSI)**, exclude complications (abscess, pseudocyst), exclude underlying carcinoma.
- **Pseudocyst**: thin/thick-walled cystic structure within/adjacent to pancreas, fluid-filled; **many resolve in 4–6 weeks**; US/CT follow-up.
- **Chronic (CT)**: **main pancreatic duct dilatation + beading, intraductal calcifications, pancreatic calcification, atrophy, pseudocysts**.

## High-yield one-liners
- **US best for gallstones (echogenic + acoustic shadowing); CT mainstay for inflammation/tumour/trauma; MRCP replaced diagnostic ERCP/PTC.**
- **Normal CBD ≤7mm, wall <3mm. GB polyp >10mm → cholecystectomy.**
- **Acute cholecystitis: wall >3mm + distended + sludge + Murphy's; wall oedema = acute vs chronic.**
- **Cystic duct obstruction (HIDA) = GB not seen but CBD + duodenum seen.**
- **Obstructive jaundice → biliary dilatation (US more sensitive than CT); may lag 48h.**
- **Spleen = most injured in blunt trauma (CT standard); splenomegaly >13cm; lymphoma > mets.**
- **Pancreatic adenocarcinoma = 2/3 head, early dx (duct dilatation); tail = late. Vascular encasement = unresectable.**
- **Chronic pancreatitis = duct dilatation/beading + calcification + atrophy. Pseudocyst resolves 4–6 wks.**
