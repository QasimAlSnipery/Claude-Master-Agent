# Cancer Control & Prevention Strategy

## The Big Picture
Cancer causes **~1 in 6 deaths globally**, and **>70% of cancer deaths are in low/middle-income countries**. WHO's answer = a **National Cancer Control Programme (NCCP)** — a planned, evidence-based, resource-appropriate approach across **5 components: prevention, early detection, diagnosis, treatment, palliative care**. ~**30–50% of cancers are preventable**.

## Facts
- Cancer = leading cause of death; **1 in 6 deaths**; **>70% of deaths in LMICs** (limited resources).
- "Cancer no longer needs to be a death sentence" — burden can be reduced.

## National Cancer Control Programme (NCCP)
- Public-health programme to **reduce cancer cases + deaths and improve quality of life** using systematic, equitable, evidence-based strategies within available resources.
- Needs accurate data — **reliable cancer registries** + monitoring/evaluation.
- **Goal-oriented, realistic, funded, participatory.**

## The 5 Core Components
1. **Prevention** — **30–50% of cancers preventable**: avoid tobacco, reduce alcohol, healthy weight, exercise, address **infection-related** risks (HBV vaccine, HPV vaccine, H. pylori). ~40% preventable now; most cost-effective. Risk factors: tobacco, alcohol, low fruit/veg, inactivity, obesity + physical (UV, radiation), chemical (asbestos, aflatoxins), biological (viruses/bacteria/parasites) carcinogens.
2. **Early detection** — detects disease early when curable (~⅓ of cases):
   - **Early diagnosis** (symptomatic patients — awareness of early signs → prompt referral).
   - **Screening** (asymptomatic people — e.g. **cervical Pap, breast**) → detect pre-cancer/early cancer.
3. **Diagnosis** — confirm with appropriate procedures.
4. **Treatment** — cure, prolong life, improve quality (linked to early detection, evidence-based; includes rehabilitation).
5. **Palliative care** — symptom relief + psychosocial support for patients/families, especially advanced/terminal; from time of diagnosis.

## Prevention Planning (3 steps)
1. **Where are we now?** — assess risk-factor burden.
2. **Where do we want to be?** — what interventions reduce exposure.
3. **How do we get there?** — translate knowledge to practice, select by resources.

## Basic Principles
Leadership, stakeholder involvement, partnerships, respond to people's needs, **systemic + stepwise approach**, evidence-based decisions, continuous improvement.

## Cancer Control in Iraq (challenges)
Infrastructure deficits (few centres, **drug/equipment shortages — chemo, PET, radiotherapy**), workforce (specialist shortage, brain drain), **delayed diagnosis** (low awareness, stigma, rural access), financial constraints, environmental/war factors (pollution, depleted uranium), instability, **poor cancer registry/data**, low prevention focus, sanctions limiting access.

## Walk-Away Points
- Cancer = **1 in 6 deaths**, >70% in LMICs; WHO answer = **NCCP**.
- 5 components: **prevention → early detection → diagnosis → treatment → palliative care**.
- **30–50% preventable** (tobacco, alcohol, diet, weight, infections/vaccines).
- Early detection = **early diagnosis (symptomatic) + screening (asymptomatic, e.g. Pap/breast)**; needs registries + data.
