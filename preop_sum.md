# PREOPERATIVE ASSESSMENT & GENERAL ANAESTHESIA

## Preoperative evaluation
- Aims: ensure safety, optimize condition, minimize risk. Begins when anaesthetist reviews record + visits patient day before elective surgery.
- **Includes**: history, current drugs, exam, labs, **consent**, anaesthesia plan, postop pain relief, fasting, **ASA classification**, premedication.

## ASA Fasting guidelines
| Material | Minimum fast |
|---|---|
| **Clear liquids** | **2 h** |
| Breast milk | 4 h |
| Infant formula | 5–6 h |
| Non-human milk | 6 h |
| **Light meal** | **6 h** |
| **Heavy meal (fat/meat)** | **8 h** |

## ASA Physical Status Classification
- **I**: normal healthy (non-smoker, minimal alcohol).
- **II**: mild systemic disease, no functional limitation (smoker, social alcohol, **pregnancy, obesity 30–40, well-controlled DM/HTN, mild lung disease**).
- **III**: severe systemic disease with functional limitation (poorly controlled DM/HTN, COPD, **BMI ≥40**, ESRD on dialysis, **MI/CVA/stent >3 months ago**).
- **IV**: severe disease = constant threat to life (**MI/CVA/stent <3 months**, ongoing ischaemia, severe valve dysfunction, shock, sepsis, DIC).
- **V**: moribund, not expected to survive without operation (ruptured AAA, massive trauma, intracranial bleed).
- **VI**: brain-dead, organ donor.
- **E** suffix = emergency.

## General anaesthesia
- **Definition**: drug-induced loss of consciousness, not arousable even by pain.
- **History**: nitrous oxide (Priestley 1772, Davy dental analgesia); **Morton — ether, Massachusetts General 1846 (successful)**; Wells (nitrous, 1845, discredited).
- **Components**: **immobility, amnesia, analgesia, loss of reflexes, lack of patient harm**.
- **Drugs**: hypnotics, neuromuscular blockers, analgesics.
- **Depth of sedation**: minimal (anxiolysis) → moderate (conscious) → deep → GA (no response to pain, airway intervention often needed).
- **Indications for GA**: neuromuscular blockade required, secure airway needed, not appropriate for MAC/regional, uncooperative/refusal, surgical pain not amenable to local.

## Steps of GA
1. **Induction** (awake → anaesthetized): inhaled or IV.
   - **Preoxygenation (denitrogenation)**: 100% O2 for 3 min → safety margin during apnoea.
   - **Inhalational**: paediatric (no IV); **sevoflurane** (low pungency, high potency, rapid).
   - **IV** (commonest in adults): **propofol, thiopental, etomidate, ketamine, benzodiazepine-opioid**.
   - **Rapid Sequence Induction (RSI)**: for **aspiration risk** (GERD, delayed emptying, unknown fasting, full stomach). Sequence: preoxygenate → hypnotic (propofol) → **rapid-onset NMB (succinylcholine/rocuronium)** → **cricoid pressure** → no mask ventilation → intubate → release cricoid after confirmation.
2. **Airway management**: mask ventilation → NMB → laryngoscopy/intubation or supraglottic.
3. **Maintenance**: **potent inhaled (halogenated) agents = mainstay** (titratable, ↓autonomic response, muscle relaxation); **nitrous oxide** (hypnosis + analgesia, but can't be sole — low potency); **propofol** (↓PONV, less laryngospasm); **TIVA (propofol + ketamine + opioid)** for evoked potential monitoring (e.g. scoliosis — inhaled agents affect signals).
4. **Recovery**: inhaled cleared via lungs; IV redistributed/metabolised/antidotes; **muscle relaxants reversed**.
5. **PACU**: recovery + direction for ongoing care.

## High-yield one-liners
- **Fasting: 2h clear liquids, 6h light meal, 8h heavy meal.**
- **ASA: I healthy → VI brain-dead; III/IV split by MI/CVA >3mo vs <3mo; E = emergency.**
- **GA components: immobility, amnesia, analgesia, loss of reflexes, no harm.**
- **Preoxygenation = 100% O2 ×3min; sevoflurane for paediatric inhaled induction.**
- **RSI for aspiration risk: propofol + succinylcholine/rocuronium + cricoid pressure, no mask ventilation.**
- **Maintenance: halogenated agents mainstay; TIVA (propofol) for evoked potential monitoring; Morton 1846 ether.**
