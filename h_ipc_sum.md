# Infection Prevention & Control (IPC)

## The Big Picture
IPC aims to **prevent infection transmission between patients, staff and visitors**. Built on understanding the **chain of infection**, preventing **healthcare-acquired infections (HAIs)**, applying **standard precautions** (hand hygiene + PPE) for everyone, and adding **transmission-based (isolation) precautions** for specific organisms. **Hand hygiene is the single most important measure.**

## Chain of Infection
Infectious agent → reservoir → portal of exit → mode of transmission → portal of entry → susceptible host. Break any link to stop spread.

## Healthcare-Acquired Infection (HAI / Nosocomial)
- Infection acquired during hospitalisation, **not present/incubating at admission** — occurs **>48–72 h after admission**.
- Often Staph aureus, Gram-negatives (Klebsiella), etc.

## Care Bundles
- **Bundles** = small sets of **evidence-based practices** that, done together, improve reliability and **prevent HAIs**.

## Standard Precautions (for ALL patients)
### 1. Hand Hygiene (most important)
- **5 moments**; soap + water **15 seconds** (visible soiling) or **alcohol rub** (no visible soiling); **no artificial nails**.
### 2. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- Treat **all blood/body fluids as infectious** — "if it's wet and not yours, use a barrier."
- **Bloodborne "Big 3": HIV, Hepatitis B, Hepatitis C.** High-risk: needle sharing, unprotected sex, unclean tattoo, needlestick.
- **Needlestick risk:** Hep B **6–30%** (if unvaccinated), Hep C **~3%**, **HIV 0.3%** (0.1% mucosal splash). Hep B vaccine + report all injuries.
### 3. Respiratory Etiquette
- Cover cough/sneeze with tissue or sleeve (not hand); mask coughing patients; wear mask/eye protection within 3 ft of coughing patient.

## Transmission-Based (Isolation) Precautions
- **Droplet:** influenza, rubella, bacterial meningitis.
- **Airborne:** **TB, smallpox, chickenpox** → **N-95 mask, closed door, negative-pressure room**; surgical mask on patient during transport.
- **Contact:** C. difficile diarrhoea, skin infections, **MDROs (MRSA, VRE)** → gloves/gown, clean shared equipment.
- Isolation signs always show a STOP + required PPE; communicate isolation status; clean shared equipment between patients.

## Employee Health
- Hep B vaccine, annual TB assessment, **report all needlestick/blood exposures immediately** to supervisor + infection control.

## Walk-Away Points
- IPC = break the **chain of infection**; **hand hygiene = #1 measure**.
- **HAI = infection >48 h after admission**; bundles prevent them.
- **Standard precautions for everyone** (hand hygiene + PPE + respiratory etiquette); treat all fluids as infectious.
- **Airborne (TB/chickenpox) → N-95 + negative pressure**; contact (MDRO) → gloves/gown.
- Needlestick risk: **HBV 6–30% > HCV 3% > HIV 0.3%**.
