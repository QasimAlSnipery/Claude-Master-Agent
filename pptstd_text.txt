Anatomy
• Extent: Cecum to rectum (~1.5 m)
• Features: Teniae coli, haustra, appendices
epiploicae
• Peritoneal relations: Cecum, transverse &
sigmoid are intraperitoneal; ascending &
descending are retroperitoneal

=====PAGE 6 [T]=====
Investigations of Large Bowel Disease
• Laboratory: CBC, CRP, LFTs, CEA
• Endoscopy: Colonoscopy + biopsy (gold
standard)
• Radiology:
- CT abdomen/pelvis (diverticulitis, cancer
staging)
- Contrast enema (apple-core lesion)
- MRI pelvis (rectal cancer)
- PET-CT (metastasis)
11/16/2025 KARZAN SEERWAN 6
=====PAGE 7 [T]=====
Diverticulosis and Diverticulitis
• Diverticulosis: mucosal outpouchings,
usually sigmoid
• Risk factors: age, low-fiber diet,
constipation
• Usually asymptomatic until inflammation
(diverticulitis)

=====PAGE 9 [T]=====
Acute Diverticulitis
• Pathophysiology:
inflammation/microperforation of
diverticula
• Clinical features: LLQ pain, fever,
leukocytosis, bowel changes
• Complications: abscess, fistula,
perforation, peritonitis, stricture
11/16/2025 KARZAN SEERWAN 9
=====PAGE 10 [T]=====
Management of Diverticular Disease
• Uncomplicated: bowel rest, IV fluids,
antibiotics (ceftriaxone + metronidazole)
• Complicated:
- Abscess → drainage
- Perforation → Hartmann’s procedure
- Fistula/stricture → elective sigmoid
colectomy

=====PAGE 12 [T]=====
Colorectal Carcinoma
• 95% adenocarcinoma
• Risk factors: age, adenomas, family
history, diet, IBD
• Common sites: rectosigmoid, ascending
colon
11/16/2025 KARZAN SEERWAN 12
=====PAGE 13 [T]=====
Types of Tumors
• Adenocarcinoma (most common)
• Carcinoid (neuroendocrine)
• Lymphoma
• GIST
• Benign adenomas
11/16/2025 KARZAN SEERWAN 13
=====PAGE 14 [T]=====
Clinical Presentation of CRC
• Right-sided: occult bleeding, anemia
• Left-sided: change in habit, obstruction
• Rectal: bleeding, tenesmus, incomplete
evacuation
11/16/2025 KARZAN SEERWAN 14
=====PAGE 15 [T]=====
Diagnosis and Radiology in CRC
• Colonoscopy with biopsy → definitive
• CT chest/abdomen/pelvis → staging
• MRI rectum for local staging
• CEA for baseline & follow-up

=====PAGE 17 [T]=====
Surgical Management of CRC
• Principles: oncologic resection with
lymphadenectomy
• Procedures:
- Right/Left hemicolectomy
- Sigmoid colectomy
- Anterior resection / APR
• Laparoscopic preferred if feasible
• Adjuvant chemo for Stage III
11/16/2025 KARZAN SEERWAN 17
=====PAGE 18 [T]=====
Right Hemicolectomy
• Used for tumors of cecum, ascending colon,
hepatic flexure.
• Includes ileocolic and right colic vessels.
11/16/2025 KARZAN SEERWAN 18
=====PAGE 19 [T]=====
Left Hemicolectomy
• Used for tumors of descending colon
• Includes left colic artery ligation.
11/16/2025 KARZAN SEERWAN 19
=====PAGE 20 [T]=====
Sigmoid Hemicolectomy
For sigmoid lesions; ligate sigmoid branches and
mobilize sigmoid colon.

Postoperative Outcomes & Follow-Up
• Prognosis:
- Stage I: >90% 5-year survival
- Stage III: ~50%
• Surveillance:
- Colonoscopy, CEA, CT every 6–12
months
- Lifestyle modification
