# Reproductive Tract Infections & STIs

## The Big Picture
RTIs = infections of the genital tract — not all are sexually transmitted. Three causes: **endogenous** (overgrowth of normal flora — candidiasis, bacterial vaginosis), **iatrogenic** (procedures — IUD, abortion, catheter), and **sexually transmitted (STIs)**. The most common STI symptom is **no symptom**. Untreated → **PID, infertility, cancer, congenital infection**. Know each STI's organism, sign, diagnosis (mostly **NAAT**), and treatment.

## Causes & Agents
- **Endogenous (commonest worldwide):** candidiasis, bacterial vaginosis.
- **Iatrogenic:** IUD insertion, abortion, catheterisation.
- **STIs:** bacterial (chlamydia, gonorrhoea, syphilis), viral (**4 H's: HIV, HPV, Herpes, Hepatitis** — antibiotics don't work), parasitic (trichomoniasis), fungal (candidiasis).

## PID (Pelvic Inflammatory Disease)
- Ascending infection (vagina → uterus/tubes/ovaries), usually **gonorrhoea/chlamydia**.
- **Complications: infertility, ectopic pregnancy, chronic pelvic pain, salpingitis.**
- Risk: young (<25), multiple partners, no condom, prior STI/PID.

## Trichomoniasis
- **Trichomonas vaginalis** (flagellated protozoan) — **most common non-viral STI**.
- **Frothy yellow-green discharge, fishy odour, itching; "strawberry cervix" (pathognomonic).** Men usually asymptomatic.
- Diagnosis: wet-mount motile protozoa, **NAAT** (more sensitive), culture (only validated test in men).
- **Treatment: metronidazole** (500 mg BD × 7 d or 2 g single) / tinidazole.

## Chlamydia
- **Chlamydia trachomatis** — most numerous STI; leading infectious blindness; often **asymptomatic (70% women, 50% men)**.
- → PID/infertility (women), epididymitis (men); neonatal **pneumonia / ophthalmia neonatorum**.
- Diagnosis: **NAAT** (vaginal swab/urine).
- **Treatment: doxycycline 100 mg BD × 7 d** or azithromycin (erythromycin in pregnancy).

## Gonorrhoea
- **Neisseria gonorrhoeae** — Gram-negative diplococci. Infects cervix/urethra/rectum/pharynx/eye; vertical transmission.
- Diagnosis: **NAAT**; **Gram stain shows intracellular Gram-negative diplococci**; culture for sensitivity.
- **Treatment: ceftriaxone 500 mg IM single dose** (rising **multidrug resistance — "super gonorrhoea"**). Treat partners (60 days), retest at 3 months, avoid sex 1 week.

## Syphilis
- **Treponema pallidum** (spirochete) — the **"great imitator."** 4 stages:
  - **Primary:** painless **chancre**.
  - **Secondary:** rash (palms/soles), fever, lymphadenopathy.
  - **Latent:** asymptomatic.
  - **Tertiary (10–30 yr):** brain, heart, aorta, bones (gummas).
- Congenital syphilis → stillbirth.
- Diagnosis: **non-treponemal (VDRL/RPR — screen + monitor)** + **treponemal (TPHA/FTA-ABS — confirm, stay positive for life)**.
- **Treatment: benzathine penicillin IM** (single dose early; 3 weekly for late); doxycycline if allergic (penicillin in pregnancy).

## HPV
- 200+ viruses; **types 16 & 18 → cervical dysplasia/cancer**; 6 & 11 → genital warts. Often asymptomatic.
- **Prevention: vaccine (before sexual activity) + Pap screening** + condoms. Treat warts (cryotherapy/topical); no drug clears virus.

## Genital Herpes
- **HSV-2** (skin-to-skin, even without sores). Painful **vesicles → ulcers**, recurrent (latent in sensory ganglia); neonatal herpes risk.
- **Treatable not curable.** Diagnosis: **NAAT** of vesicle fluid.
- **Treatment: aciclovir / valaciclovir** (first episode 5 d; recurrent shorter).

## Prevention (general)
Barrier methods, fewer partners/monogamy, **vaccination (HPV, hepatitis B)**, regular testing/premarital screening, prompt full treatment, partner notification, abstain during treatment.

## Walk-Away Points
- RTI = endogenous / iatrogenic / STI; **viral 4 H's** can't be cured with antibiotics.
- **NAAT** is the workhorse diagnostic for chlamydia/gonorrhoea/trichomonas/herpes.
- Key pairs: **chlamydia → doxycycline, gonorrhoea → ceftriaxone IM, syphilis → benzathine penicillin, trichomonas → metronidazole, herpes → aciclovir**.
- Signs: **strawberry cervix (trich), painless chancre (syphilis), warts (HPV 6/11), cancer (HPV 16/18)**.
- PID → infertility/ectopic; prevent STIs with condoms, vaccines, screening.
