# Carcinoma of the Pancreas

## The Big Picture
Pancreatic cancer = a quiet killer. 6th leading cancer death (UK), no screening test, and **>85% are unresectable at diagnosis**. Most are **ductal adenocarcinoma of the head**. The exam themes: **painless obstructive jaundice + Courvoisier**, CT to stage, **Whipple/PPPD** for the lucky resectable few, and palliation (stent the jaundice) for the rest.

## Risk Factors
- Demographic: age **65–75**, male, black ethnicity.
- Lifestyle: **smoking** (the key modifiable one).
- Genetic/medical: family history (2 first-degree relatives → 18–57× risk), **BRCA2**, **hereditary pancreatitis (50–70×)**, chronic pancreatitis (5–15×), Lynch, Peutz-Jeghers, FAP, **diabetes mellitus**.

## Pathology
- **>85% ductal adenocarcinoma**, mostly **head of gland**; infiltrates along nerve sheaths, lymphatics, vessels. Liver + peritoneal mets common.
- Cystic tumours: **serous cystadenoma** (older women, benign) vs **mucinous (MCN, IPMN)** = malignant potential.
- **Periampullary cancer** (ampulla/distal CBD) = ~⅓ of head-region tumours, **better prognosis**.

## Clinical Features
- **Painless obstructive jaundice** = classic (head/ampullary tumour). + pruritus, dark urine, pale stool, steatorrhoea, nausea, epigastric discomfort.
- No jaundice → vague: anorexia, weight loss (often dismissed).
- **New diabetic >50** with upper-abdominal symptoms, or **unexplained pancreatitis** = red flag.
- **Body/tail tumours** grow silently → present advanced/unresectable.
- **Back pain** = retroperitoneal infiltration (worrying).
- **Courvoisier's law** — palpable gallbladder + jaundice = unlikely stones, suspect malignancy.

## Investigation
- Bloods, **US** (is bile duct dilated?), then **contrast-enhanced CT** = preferred staging test.
- **Contraindications to resection:** hepatic/peritoneal mets, distant nodes, **encasement of SMA/hepatic/coeliac artery**. (Tumour size, duodenal/gastric/colon invasion, local nodes are NOT contraindications.)
- MRI/MRCP — "**double duct sign**." ERCP + stenting if cholangitis, diagnostic doubt, or delay to surgery (not mandatory if resectable).
- **EUS + FNA** for tissue (avoids peritoneal spillage); avoid percutaneous biopsy of resectable tumours. **CA19-9** baseline (recurrence marker). Diagnostic laparoscopy spares unnecessary laparotomy.

## Management
- **>85% unresectable** at presentation.
- **Resectable head/ampulla → PPPD** (pylorus-preserving pancreatoduodenectomy): removes duodenum + pancreatic head + distal bile duct + local lymphadenectomy. **Whipple** (adds gastric antrum) reserved for FAP/duodenal encroachment.
- **Total pancreatectomy** — multifocal (main-duct IPMN) or unsafe anastomosis.
- **Body/tail → distal pancreatectomy + splenectomy** (vaccinate against pneumococcus/meningococcus/Hib).
- 3 phases (explore → resect → reconstruct), 3–6 h. **Mortality 3–5%, morbidity 30–40%**; pancreatic **anastomotic leak ≥10%** (octreotide reduces it).
- **Adjuvant** — 6 months gemcitabine ± 5-FU; chemoradiotherapy if R1 margin. Ampullary 5-yr survival 40%.

## Palliation (for the 85%)
- Median survival: locally advanced 6–10 mo, metastatic 2–6 mo.
- **Jaundice** → ERCP stent (plastic cheaper/occludes; metal for longer life expectancy) or percutaneous transhepatic; surgical biliary bypass if found at laparotomy.
- **Duodenal obstruction (~15%)** → gastrojejunostomy (early) or duodenal stent (late).
- **Pain** → analgesia ladder + **coeliac plexus block**. Steatorrhoea → enzymes; treat diabetes.

## Walk-Away Points
- **Painless obstructive jaundice + Courvoisier** = pancreatic head cancer until proven otherwise.
- **CT stages it; arterial encasement / mets = unresectable.**
- **PPPD** is the standard curative op (mortality 3–5%, leak the feared complication).
- New diabetes >50 or unexplained pancreatitis → image the pancreas.
- Most present too late → palliate (stent jaundice, relieve duodenal obstruction, block pain).
