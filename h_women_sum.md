# Women's Health Education

## The Big Picture
Women's health education = equipping women to make informed decisions across life's transitions (**puberty → menopause**). It spans four pillars: **healthcare (SRH programs), screening, vaccines, treatment**, plus attention to **gender-specific concerns** (CVD, osteoporosis, autoimmune disease, gender-based violence).

## Key Areas
- **Sexual & reproductive health** — puberty, menstrual cycle, contraception, STIs, fertility, pregnancy, menopause.
- **Screening & preventive care** — breast cancer, Pap/HPV, vaccinations, bone density.
- **Comprehensive wellbeing** — nutrition, physical activity, mental health, hormonal changes.
- **Gender-specific concerns** — CVD, osteoporosis, autoimmune, Alzheimer's (hormonal + longer lifespan); **gender-based violence** (intimate partner violence, sexual assault).

## 1. SRH Programs (by timing)
- **Premarital** — screen infectious diseases (HIV, HBV, STDs), blood type/Rh, **genetic blood disorders (thalassaemia)**, anaemia.
- **Preconception** — plan ahead: anaemia correction, treat PID/partner, dental care + vaccination, weight correction, chronic disease control (DM, HTN, thyroid, epilepsy), health habits (smoking, salt), **folic acid 3 months prior**.
- **Perinatal** — monitor mother + fetus: scheduled checks (weight, fundal height), bloods (anaemia, Rh, virology), urine (UTI, glucose, protein), ultrasound + fetal heart.

## 2. Screening (key check-ups)
- **Breast:** breast self-exam from **age 20**, **mammography from age 40**.
- **Cervical:** **Pap smear + colposcopy ages 25–65**.
- **Colon:** colonoscopy from **age 45** (or earlier).
- **Others:** HTN, hyperlipidaemia, bone density (postmenopausal).

## 3. Vaccines
- **Hepatitis B** (check anti-HBs if vaccinated at birth).
- **HPV** — recommended **11–12 (from age 9)**; prevents up to **90% of HPV cancers (types 16, 18)**; **contraindicated in pregnancy**.
- **Td (tetanus/diphtheria)** — replaces TT for women of childbearing age.
- **Annual influenza** — safe in pregnancy, from 6 months of age.

## 4. Treatment
- **Physical** — diagnose/treat CVD, UTIs, osteoporosis, breast conditions.
- **Psychological** — anxiety, depression, **postpartum blues**, intimate partner violence, social pressure.

## Walk-Away Points
- Four pillars: **SRH programs, screening, vaccines, treatment**.
- SRH by timing: **premarital (thalassaemia/STD screen), preconception (folic acid 3 mo), perinatal**.
- Screening: **BSE 20, mammography 40, Pap 25–65, colonoscopy 45**.
- Vaccines: **HPV (11–12, prevents 16/18 cancers, not in pregnancy), HepB, Td, flu**.
- Address gender-specific disease + **gender-based violence**.
