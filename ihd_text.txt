pathophysiology
∗ results from progressive blockage of the coronary
arteries by atherothrombotic disease.
∗ Progressive compromise in luminal diameter producing
supply/demand imbalance usually produces a pattern
of chronic stable angina.
∗ Plaque rupture with superimposed thrombosis is
responsible for most acute coronary syndromes
(ACS), which include classic “unstable angina”, non-ST-
elevation myocardial infarctions
(NSTEMI), and ST-elevation infarctions (STEMI).
