# Consultation & Counselling in Family Medicine

## The Big Picture
Family medicine provides **continuous, comprehensive care** for individuals + families of all ages, integrating **biological, psychological and social** factors. Good **consultation + counselling** = the core skill: it improves diagnosis accuracy, satisfaction, and adherence. Without a **shared understanding**, the patient won't follow advice and the whole assessment is wasted.

## Family Medicine
- Medical specialty; continuing + comprehensive care for individual + family; integrates biologic/clinical/behavioural sciences; all ages, sexes, organ systems, diseases. (= general practice in UK/Australia/NZ.)
- **Principles:** **doctor–patient–family relationship**, high-quality primary care for all ages, community/population-based.
- Relationship built on **active listening, patient-centred medicine, empathy, "caring more than curing," continuity** (repeated contact builds trust).

## What Is Counselling?
- Skilled, principled use of relationship to help the patient develop **self-knowledge, emotional acceptance and coping**.
- Effective counsellors show **warmth, genuineness, empathy** (not authoritarianism).
- **Types:** **directive** (lead/interpret/direct) vs **non-directive/reflective** (elicit, reflect, support).

## Aims of Counselling
Help patients accept difficulties + cope more effectively; counsellor listens/questions until both understand the patient's view; client clarifies thoughts/feelings.

## Stages of Counselling
1. **Exploration** — let patient explore the problem, then focus.
2. **New understanding** — see self/situation in new perspective.
3. **Goal setting.**
4. **Action** — plan, implement, evaluate (problem-solving, decision-making).
- Key: reach a **joint understanding + management plan** the patient accepts, or they won't adhere.

## Communication Deficiencies
Doctors: don't get enough of the patient's perspective, give info inflexibly, don't check understanding; <½ of psychological morbidity recognised.

## Why Patients Don't Disclose
Belief nothing can be done, reluctance to burden doctor, fear of appearing pathetic, feeling it's not legitimate, **doctor's blocking behaviour**, fear of bad news, **lack of confidentiality/trust**.

## Blocking Behaviours (doctor) — avoid
Premature advice/reassurance, explaining away distress as normal, attending only to physical aspects, switching topics, "jollying along."

## Failed Consultation
No rapport, **medical jargon**, not exploring patient's agenda, not eliciting the real problem, **no contingency plan**, no summarisation, failing to clarify, ignoring socio-cultural/economic context.

## Barriers
Shortage of time, **language barrier**, misconceptions/myths, low awareness, patient not taking responsibility, socio-cultural/economic barriers, **fatalistic attitude ("God's will")**; organisational (workload, interruptions).

## Benefits of Good Skills
- **Counselling well** → more accurate problems, more satisfaction, better adherence, less anxiety/depression.
- **Consultation well** → better doctor + patient wellbeing, fewer errors, **fewer complaints/lawsuits**.
- **Longer consultation → fewer drug prescriptions + more satisfaction.**

## Walk-Away Points
- Family medicine = **continuous comprehensive care** integrating bio-psycho-social; built on **doctor–patient–family relationship**.
- Counselling = warmth/genuineness/empathy; **directive vs non-directive**; stages = **exploration → new understanding → goal setting → action**.
- **No shared understanding → no adherence**; avoid **blocking behaviours + jargon**.
- Good skills → accuracy, satisfaction, adherence, fewer lawsuits; **longer consults = less prescribing + more satisfaction**.
