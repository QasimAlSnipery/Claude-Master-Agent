# Hypercalcaemia & Thyroid/Parathyroid Review (Dr Sarmad)

## The Big Picture
Hypercalcaemia = serum Ca above normal (**8.5–10.2 mg/dL**). Most are mild and asymptomatic; the danger is **hypercalcaemic crisis (Ca >14 mg/dL)** — a medical emergency. Treatment rests on three pillars: **rehydrate, force renal excretion, block bone release**. This lecture also reviews high-yield thyroid/parathyroid surgical facts via MCQs.

## Hypercalcaemia Basics
- Normal serum Ca **8.5–10.2 mg/dL**; found in up to **5% of hospitalised patients**.
- Most asymptomatic, mild-moderate (**12–14 mg/dL**) — treat underlying cause + diet.

## Hypercalcaemic Crisis
- **Ca >14 mg/dL** with symptoms → needs aggressive management.
- **Presentation:** acute confusion, abdominal pain, vomiting, **dehydration, anuria**.
- **ECG:** prolonged PR + **shortened QT** → risk of lethal arrhythmia. **Ca >18 mg/dL → coma, cardiac arrest.**

## Treatment (3 principles)
1. **↑ renal excretion** — **aggressive rehydration** (normal saline 200–500 mL/h to keep urine >100 mL/h); then **loop diuretic (furosemide)** once volume restored. Most normalise with this alone.
2. **↓ skeletal release** (if malignancy + Ca >12):
   - **Bisphosphonates** — inhibit osteoclasts (IV, poor gut absorption).
   - **Calcitonin** — fast but short-acting; **bridge** until bisphosphonate works.
   - **Glucocorticoids (prednisolone)** — enhance calcitonin, ↑ calciuresis, ↓ gut absorption; useful in **vitamin D excess**.
3. **Treat the underlying cause.**

## Thyroid/Parathyroid High-Yield Review (from MCQs)
- **Thyroglossal duct cyst** — midline congenital remnant of thyroid descent; remove with **Sistrunk operation** (+ central hyoid).
- **Thyroid blood supply** — superior thyroid (from **external carotid**), inferior thyroid (from **thyrocervical trunk**), thyroid ima (from aorta in 1–4%).
- **Recurrent laryngeal nerve** injury → hoarseness (unilateral) / airway obstruction (bilateral). **Superior laryngeal nerve (external branch)** → can't **"hit high notes"** (cricothyroid).
- **TSH** directly triggers T3/T4 release (TRH → TSH); thyroglobulin tyrosines iodinated → T3/T4.
- **Total/near-total thyroidectomy** preferred for Graves when: RAI contraindicated, **goitre >80 g/airway risk**, poor compliance.
- **FNAB "suspicious for malignancy"** → **lobectomy** (60–75% turn out malignant).
- **Papillary thyroid cancer** — 80% of thyroid cancers; predominant in children + **radiation-exposed (Chernobyl)**.
- **Follicular cancer (2 cm)** → **total thyroidectomy**; recurrence treated with **radioiodine**.
- **Medullary thyroid cancer** → screen for **MEN2** (pheochromocytoma + hyperparathyroidism in 2A; ganglioneuromas/Marfanoid in 2B); spontaneous 75%, familial 25%.
- **Post-thyroidectomy emergency needing re-op = wound haematoma** (compresses trachea → dyspnoea; open at bedside). Hypocalcaemia → IV calcium; bilateral vocal cord palsy → reintubate.

## Walk-Away Points
- Normal Ca **8.5–10.2**; crisis **>14**, coma **>18**; ECG = **short QT**.
- Treat crisis: **saline rehydration → furosemide → bisphosphonate** (calcitonin as bridge).
- **Thyroglossal cyst → Sistrunk**; superior thyroid artery from external carotid.
- **Haematoma = the thyroidectomy emergency**; medullary cancer → think MEN2.
