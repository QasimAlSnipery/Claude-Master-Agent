# COMMON SHOULDER (& UPPER LIMB) PATHOLOGY

## Shoulder impingement syndrome
- **Mechanical compression of rotator cuff (mainly supraspinatus)** + subacromial bursa + long head biceps. Pain on overhead movements.
- **Clinical**: **painful arc (60–120°)**, night pain, weak abduction.
- **Special tests**: **Neer** (pain on forced flexion), **Hawkins-Kennedy** (pain on internal rotation), **empty can** (supraspinatus weakness).
- **Management**: conservative (activity modification, NSAIDs, physio = cuff strengthening, subacromial steroid injection) → surgical (**arthroscopic subacromial decompression**, remove spur, repair cuff tear).

## Frozen shoulder (adhesive capsulitis)
- **Self-limiting painful stiffness** — chronic inflammation + fibrosis of joint capsule. **Loss of active AND passive ROM (mainly external rotation).**
- Age 40–50; **females**; **strong DM association** (2–30%). Idiopathic or secondary (trauma, surgery, immobilization).
- **Pathophysiology**: synovial inflammation → fibroblast proliferation → **excess type III collagen → capsular thickening** (rotator interval, coracohumeral ligament, axillary pouch).
- **Clinical**: gradual painful stiffness, night pain; **loss of passive external rotation = hallmark**.
- **3 phases**: **Freezing (painful, 2–9mo) → Frozen (stiff, 4–12mo) → Thawing (6–24mo)**.
- **Management**: clinical diagnosis (X-ray rules out arthritis); NSAIDs, physio (stretching), **intra-articular steroid (early)**, MUA / arthroscopic release if refractory.

## Calcific tendinitis
- **Calcium hydroxyapatite deposits in rotator cuff (mainly supraspinatus)**.
- **Etiology**: tendon hypoxia → fibrocartilaginous metaplasia → calcium deposition. Assoc DM + thyroid disease.
- **4 phases**: formative → resting → **resorptive (severe pain)** → post-calcific.
- **Clinical**: sudden severe pain, night pain, painful arc. **Imaging**: X-ray (dense circumscribed calcification), US, MRI.
- **Management**: NSAIDs, physio, subacromial steroid, **US-guided barbotage, shock-wave therapy**; surgical (arthroscopic removal).

## Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis)
- **Overuse of ECRB tendon at lateral epicondyle** (repetitive wrist extension + gripping).
- **Clinical**: lateral elbow pain, worse with gripping/lifting, tender over lateral epicondyle, pain on resisted wrist extension.
- **Special tests**: **Cozen's** (resisted wrist extension → pain), **Mill's** (passive wrist flexion → pain), **Maudsley** (resisted middle finger extension → pain).
- **Management**: rest, NSAIDs, physio (eccentric strengthening), counterforce brace, ESWT, PRP injection; **surgery only if >6–12 months conservative failure**.

## Congenital upper limb deformities
- **Radial longitudinal deficiency**: failed radial development → radial deviation. Assoc **VACTERL, Holt-Oram, Fanconi anaemia**. Bayne I–IV. Tx: splinting, centralization/radialization, **pollicization**.
- **Syndactyly**: failed digital separation. Assoc **Apert, Poland**. Surgical release 6–12 months.
- **Polydactyly**: extra digits. Assoc Ellis-van Creveld, Patau. Preaxial/postaxial/central. Excision (Wassel for thumb).
- **Thumb hypoplasia**: underdeveloped thumb. Assoc Holt-Oram, VACTERL. Blauth I–V. Opponensplasty/pollicization.
- **Macrodactyly**: digit enlargement. Assoc Proteus, NF1. Debulking, epiphysiodesis.

## High-yield one-liners
- **Impingement: painful arc 60–120°; Neer + Hawkins-Kennedy + empty can tests; subacromial decompression.**
- **Frozen shoulder: loss of PASSIVE external rotation = hallmark; DM association; freezing/frozen/thawing phases.**
- **Calcific tendinitis: hydroxyapatite in supraspinatus; resorptive phase = severe pain; barbotage.**
- **Tennis elbow = ECRB; Cozen's/Mill's/Maudsley; eccentric strengthening.**
- **Radial deficiency = VACTERL/Fanconi → pollicization; syndactyly = Apert/Poland.**
