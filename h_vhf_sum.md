# Viral Haemorrhagic Fever (focus: Crimean-Congo HF)

## The Big Picture
Viral haemorrhagic fevers (VHFs) damage **small blood vessels → leak + bleeding** and impair clotting, affecting many organ systems. In the Eastern Mediterranean/Iraq the main one is **Crimean-Congo haemorrhagic fever (CCHF)** — a **tick-borne** virus with **10–40% case fatality**, spread by livestock contact. Treatment is supportive ± **ribavirin**; prevention = tick/animal control + protective barriers.

## VHF Overview
- Caused by several virus families; damage vessel walls → leak, impair clotting → bleeding.
- Mostly tropical. EMR examples: **CCHF, yellow fever, dengue, Ebola, Rift Valley fever.**

## Crimean-Congo Haemorrhagic Fever (CCHF)
- **Virus:** Nairovirus, **Bunyaviridae family**; tick-borne. First described Crimea 1944, Congo 1969.
- **Case fatality 10–40%.** Endemic in Africa, Balkans, Middle East, Asia (south of 50°N — limit of tick vector).
- **Vector:** **Hyalomma tick** (principal). Hosts: cattle, sheep, goats (ostriches susceptible; most birds resistant). Tick–animal–tick cycle.

### Transmission
- **Tick bite** OR **contact with infected animal blood/tissue** (during/after slaughter) — most cases in **livestock/abattoir workers, vets**.
- **Human-to-human** via blood/secretions/body fluids; **hospital-acquired** (unsterile equipment, needle reuse).

### Clinical
- **Incubation:** tick bite 1–3 days (max 9); blood/tissue contact 5–6 days (max 13).
- **Sudden onset:** fever, myalgia, dizziness, neck pain/stiffness, backache, headache, sore eyes, photophobia; nausea/vomiting/diarrhoea; mood swings, confusion.
- Day 2–4: RUQ pain + **hepatomegaly**, tachycardia, lymphadenopathy, **petechial rash** (mucosa + skin) → ecchymoses + haemorrhage.
- Severe (after day 5): hepatitis, **rapid kidney/liver/pulmonary failure**, epistaxis, haematemesis.

### Diagnosis
**ELISA, antigen detection, serum neutralisation, RT-PCR, viral culture.**

### Treatment
**Supportive care** + **ribavirin** (oral or IV, apparent benefit).

### Prevention & Control
- **Animals/ticks:** acaricides, tick-free 14-day quarantine before slaughter; **no animal vaccine**.
- **People (no human vaccine):** awareness + reduce exposure.
  - **Tick→human:** protective + **light-coloured clothing** (spot ticks), repellent/acaricide, check skin, avoid tick areas (spring/summer).
  - **Animal→human:** gloves/PPE when handling animals/tissue, quarantine + treat animals before slaughter.
  - **Human→human:** avoid close contact, gloves/PPE caring for ill, hand hygiene.
  - **Healthcare:** standard precautions, hand hygiene, PPE, safe injection + burial; trained lab handling.

## Walk-Away Points
- **CCHF = tick-borne (Hyalomma) Bunyavirus**, CFR 10–40%, the main VHF in Iraq.
- Spread by **tick bite + livestock blood/slaughter** + human-to-human/nosocomial.
- Clinical: sudden fever → **petechiae/ecchymoses + hepatitis + multi-organ failure**.
- Treat: **supportive + ribavirin**; prevent via tick/animal control + PPE (no vaccine).
