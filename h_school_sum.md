# School Health

## The Big Picture
Schools are a **strategic setting for health** — they reach ~1/6 of the population, a captive, vulnerable, growing group, for ~9 years. Good health ↔ better attendance and attainment. WHO's **Global School Health Initiative (1995)** promotes health in schools. The program rests on **education + services + healthy environment + nutrition + screening**, plus family/community involvement.

## Why Schools Matter
- Build knowledge, social-emotional + critical-thinking skills.
- Health ↔ reduced drop-out, better performance, employment.
- **WHO Global School Health Initiative (1995).**

## Why School-Age Get Special Consideration
- **~1/6 of the population**; easily reached (compulsory attendance, half the day, 9 years).
- **Vulnerable group** — physiological/mental/emotional stress, active/curious (accidents), health affects achievement, best window for **health education**, reach parents through children.

## Objectives (10)
1. Promote physical health (growth, prevent disease, hygiene).
2. **Prevent disease + early detection** (screening, referral).
3. Mental/emotional/social wellbeing (reduce stress, bullying).
4. Safe healthy environment (clean facilities, water, sanitation).
5. Nutrition + healthy eating.
6. Health knowledge + life skills.
7. Prevent risky behaviours (tobacco, alcohol, drugs; age-appropriate sexual health).
8. First aid + emergency care.
9. School–family–community linkage.
10. Support academic performance (less absenteeism).

## Targets
Not just students — also **teachers/staff, school environment, parents/families, community, education sector**.

## Components of School Health Program
1. **Health education/instruction** (hygiene, first aid, sanitation).
2. **School health services** (exams, communicable disease control, treat minor ailments, referral, counselling).
3. **Healthy + safe environment** (building safety, water/sewage, playgrounds, class size, **mid-day meals**).
4. **Nutrition + feeding** (inspect vendors, **subsidised mid-day meal = ≥⅓ daily requirements**).
5. **Physical education** (motor/cognitive skills; improves academics).
6. **Parent + community involvement.**
7. **Mental health services** (psychologists, counsellors, social workers).
8. **Staff health promotion + employee wellness.**
9. **Social/emotional climate.**

## Screening Program
Systematic early detection: **general physical, vision, hearing, dental, nutritional, communicable disease, mental/behavioural, postural/musculoskeletal, age-appropriate (speech, puberty)** → timely referral + follow-up.

## Comprehensive School Health Policy (8 components)
Safe environment, sequential **health education curriculum** (daily, every grade), sequential **PE curriculum**, **nutrition services**, **health services** program, **counselling/psychological/social services**, **family/community involvement**, **staff health promotion**.

## Walk-Away Points
- Schools reach **~1/6 of population**, a vulnerable captive group — best setting for **health education**.
- WHO **Global School Health Initiative (1995)**.
- Components: **education + services + healthy environment + nutrition + PE + screening + mental health + family/community**.
- Mid-day meal = **≥⅓ daily nutrient requirements**; screening = vision/hearing/dental/nutrition/mental.
