=====PAGE 4 [T]=====
Indications
• Amputation can be regarded as a treatment
and not tragedy
• indications :-
1. Dead ( or dying ) limb
2. Dangerous limb
3. Damn nuisance limb
10/28/2025 4
=====PAGE 5 [T]=====
Dead or dying limb
1. Peripheral vascular disease
2. Sever traumatized limb
3. Burns
4. Frostbite
10/28/2025 5
=====PAGE 6 [T]=====
Dangerous limb
1. Malignant tumors
2. Lethal sepsis
3. Crush injury leading to crush syndrome
10/28/2025 6
=====PAGE 7 [T]=====
Damn nuisance
Remaining the limb is more worse than having no
limb at all …. Because of :-
1. Pain
2. Gross malformation
3. Recurrent sepsis
4. Sever loss of function

Varieties of amputations
Provisional amputation
• When primary healing is unlikely
• The limb amputate as distal as the causal
factor will allow
• Skin flap suture loosely over a pack
• Re-amputation perform when stump
condition is favorable
10/28/2025 8
=====PAGE 9 [T]=====
Definitive amputation
Definitive amputation is that when the stump is
closed which may be end bearing or non- end
bearing.
• When weight is taken through the end of the
stump
• The scar must not be terminal
• Bone end must be solid (cut near the joint)
10/28/2025 9
=====PAGE 10 [T]=====
Definitive non-end-bearing
• Examples of definitive non-end bearing
amputation are through knee amputation , Syme’s
amputation which is above malleoli amputation
• Commonest variety
• All upper limb & most lower limb amputations
are come under this type
• The scar can be terminal

=====PAGE 13 [T]=====
Principles of technique
Tourniquet is used unless there is vascular
insufficiency
Skin flaps are cut so that there combined length
equals one & half the width of the limb at the
site of the amputation
Equal flaps are for upper limb & trans-femoral
amputations , long posterior flap for bellow –
knee
10/28/2025 13
=====PAGE 14 [T]=====
• Muscle cut at distal level from the level of the
bone proposed
• Myoplasty
…. When the muscles
sutured together over the bone
• A raw nerve end should not bear weight
• Divide the nerve proximal to the bone cut
10/28/2025 14
=====PAGE 15 [T]=====
• Saw is used to make the bone ends smooth , and
for beveling the tibia anteriorly
• Fibula is cut 3 cm shorter than the tibia
• Remove the tourniquet and stop bleeding
• Suture the skin without tension
• Apply suction drain
• Bandage the stump tightly
10/28/2025 15
=====PAGE 16 [T]=====
Aftercare
• Evacuate the hematoma as soon
• Elastic bandaging the stump for shrinkage
• Exercise the muscles
• Keep joints mobile
• Advice using the prosthesis

Complications of the stump:
Early
Late
10/28/2025 20
=====PAGE 21 [T]=====
• Stump is the terminal segment of the
limb remaining after the amputation
10/28/2025 21
=====PAGE 22 [T]=====
Early complications
1. Secondary hemorrhage
2. Breakdown of the skin flaps (may
be due to ischemia or excessive
suture tension )
3. Gas gangrene
10/28/2025 22
=====PAGE 23 [T]=====
Late complications
Skin
• eczema
• purulent lumps
• fissuring & ulceration
• infected epidermoid cyst
• Squamouse cell carcinoma
• Verrucous hyperplasia
10/28/2025 23
=====PAGE 24 [T]=====
• Muscle .. If excessive muscle left , it will
produce unstable , loose cushion.
• Artery: poor vascularity gives cold sensation,
blue color stump which is liable to ulcerate
• Nerve : Painful neuromas attached to the scar
• Neuroma is tender nodule as a result of trauma
to the peripheral nerve, local percussion may
induce Parastehsia indicating the level of the
lesion ( Tinsel's sign ) . Microscopically consists
of overgrowth of fibrous tissue & haphazard
sprouting of nerve fibrils .It is not tumor .
10/28/2025 24
=====PAGE 25 [T]=====
Phantom limb
• A feeling that the amputated limb
still present
• Its difficult to be treated
10/28/2025 25
=====PAGE 26 [T]=====
Joint
• the joint above the site of amputation may
become stiff or deformed
• Deformities are fixed flexion & fixed
abduction deformity of the stump in above
knee ….. Fixed flexion deformity of the stump
in bellow knee amputations .
10/28/2025 26
=====PAGE 27 [T]=====
Bones
• terminal bone spur may cause pain &
infection.
• Fracture of the bone of the stump may resulted
from sever osteoporosis .
