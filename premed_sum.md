# PREMEDICATION IN ANAESTHESIA

## Definition & goals
- **Premedication** = drugs given before anaesthesia to optimize comfort, reduce complications, improve outcomes.
- **Goals**: anxiolysis/sedation, haemodynamic stability, **reduce gastric volume/acidity**, decrease anaesthetic needs, facilitate induction + analgesia, **prevent PONV**.

## Drug classes
Benzodiazepines, opioids, anticholinergics, antiemetics, H2 blockers/PPIs/antacids, alpha-2 agonists/beta-blockers, NSAIDs.

## Anxiety management
- High incidence; anxiety → ↓satisfaction + ↑PONV + ↑acute pain. **Non-pharmacological**: rapport, explanation, empathy, reassurance, relaxation.

## Benzodiazepines
- **Indications**: anxiolysis, sedation, **amnesia**, anticonvulsant, muscle relaxation (dose-dependent).
- **Mechanism**: enhance **GABA at GABA-A receptors** → ↑Cl⁻ influx → hyperpolarization.
- **Dosing**: lower in elderly, adjust for weight/liver/renal.
- **Midazolam**: 1–2mg IV / 7.5–15mg PO; onset 3–5min IV; short-acting (high lipid solubility); **very strong amnesia**.
- **Diazepam** (5–10mg, 2–4h, moderate amnesia), **Lorazepam** (1–2mg, long 6–8h, strong amnesia).
- **Side effects**: **respiratory depression (esp. with opioids)**, hypotension (↓SVR), paradoxical reactions, prolonged sedation, amnesia (problematic for instructions).
- **CI**: severe respiratory insufficiency, allergy. Caution: elderly, hepatic/renal, substance abuse. **Pregnancy: avoid.**
- **Paediatric: midazolam = gold standard** (PO 0.25–0.5mg/kg, intranasal 0.2–0.3mg/kg; multiple routes, rapid onset).

## Analgesia (multimodal)
- Pre-emptive → prevent central sensitization, ↓opioid use, ↓postop pain.
- **Classes**: opioids, NSAIDs, paracetamol, gabapentinoids, alpha-2 agonists, NMDA antagonists, steroids, local anaesthetics.
- **Opioids**: fentanyl 1–2 mcg/kg IV (30min pre); morphine 0.1–0.2 mg/kg (severe, longer). **NSAIDs** (ketorolac, ibuprofen, diclofenac) — mild-moderate.
- **Paracetamol** 1g IV/PO. **Gabapentin** 300–600mg / **pregabalin** 75–150mg (block Ca channels → ↓neuronal excitability).
- **Ketamine** (NMDA) 0.1–0.5 mg/kg; **clonidine/dexmedetomidine** (alpha-2) = analgesia + sedation.

## Other agents
- **Beta-blockers**: continue perioperatively if already on.
- **Anticholinergics** (atropine, glycopyrrolate, scopolamine): ↓secretions, prevent bradycardia.
- **Antiemetics (PONV)**: **5-HT3 antagonists = gold standard** (ondansetron 4–8mg, granisetron 1mg); dopamine antagonist (**metoclopramide** — ↑gastric motility, ↓aspiration); steroids (dexamethasone).
- **Acid suppression**: H2 blockers/PPIs (ranitidine, omeprazole) ↓acid + volume; **antacid (sodium citrate)** ↑pH but NOT volume (GERD, trauma, obstetrics).
- **Local anaesthetics**: block Na⁺ channels. Lidocaine 1–2% (blunts propofol pain), bupivacaine 0.25–0.5% (4–8h). **Paediatric: EMLA cream 30–60 min before cannulation.**

## High-yield one-liners
- **Goals: anxiolysis + ↓gastric volume/acid + ↓anaesthetic needs + prevent PONV.**
- **Benzodiazepines = GABA-A; midazolam = strong amnesia + paediatric gold standard; SE = respiratory depression with opioids.**
- **5-HT3 (ondansetron) = gold standard antiemetic; metoclopramide ↓aspiration risk.**
- **Sodium citrate (antacid) ↑pH not volume — obstetrics/trauma; EMLA for paediatric cannulation.**
