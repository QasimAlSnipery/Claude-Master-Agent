The pituitary–thyroid axis
• Synthesis and release of thyroid hormones from the thyroid is
controlled by thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) from the
anterior pituitary.
• Secretion of TSH depends upon the level of circulating
thyroid hormones and is modified in a negative feedback
manner.
10
===PAGE 11===
• In hyperthyroidism, TSH production is suppressed, whereas
in hypothyroidism, it is stimulated.
• Regulation of TSH secretion also results from the action of
thyrotrophin-releasing hormone (TRH) produced in the
hypothalamus.

Hyperthyroidism
• It’s an abnormally high thyroid hormone.
• Symptoms of hyperthyroidism can include weight loss,
increased appetite, anxiety, irritability, heat intolerance, rapid
heart rate, and tremors.
• Graves disease is associated with eye signs also.

Hypothyroidism
• This condition occurs when the thyroid gland does not produce
enough thyroid hormone.
• Symptoms include
1. Fatigue.
2. Decrease appetite.
3. Weight gain.
4. Constipation.
5. Sensitivity to cold.
6. Dry skin.

Thyroid toxic crisis (thyroid
storm)
It’s a life-threatening complication of hyperthyroidism.
It occurs when the thyroid gland suddenly releases a large
amount of thyroid hormone into the bloodstream.
47
===PAGE 48===
Causes
• Untreated or poorly controlled hyperthyroidism
• Stress
• Infection.
• Trauma
• Certain medications
48
===PAGE 49===
• Symptoms of thyroid toxic crisis include:
• High fever
• Rapid heartbeat
• Agitation and confusion
• Diarrhea and vomiting
• Muscle weakness
• Coma
49
===PAGE 50===
Treatment
• Admission to hospital
• Beta-blockers
• Antithyroid medications
• Iodine preparations
• Glucocorticoids

Hypothyroidism
• Patients with hypothyroidism should be identified when
possible because medical treatment can greatly improve their
quality of life.
51
===PAGE 52===
• With detection early in childhood, permanent mental
retardation can be avoided with appropriate medical
management.
• In addition, oral complications of delayed eruption of teeth,
malocclusion, enlarged tongue, and skeletal growth
impairment can be prevented through early detection and
medical treatment.

Common thyroidectomy
complications
• Hypocalcemia (low calcium): This is the most common
complication. managed with calcium and vitamin D
supplements.
• Hoarseness: Damage to the recurrent laryngeal nerve can lead
to hoarseness or voice changes.
• This often improves within a few weeks to months

===PAGE 54===

pressure on the airway.
