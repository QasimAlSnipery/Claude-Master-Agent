Supracondylar fractures of the femur
•
Bimodal Distribution:
1. Young healthy males from high energy trauma with
significant displacement.
2. Elderly osteoporotic persons from low energy trauma with
less displacement.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 3
=====PAGE 4 [T]=====
Mechanism of injury
Direct violence, the fracture line could be:
• Supra condylar: above the condyles.
• Inter condylar: extend between the condyles(T or Y).
• Severely comminuted.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 4

DISPLACEMENT
•The distal fragment displaced into extension (Apex
posterior) by the gastrocnemius endangering the Popliteal
artery.
•Hamstring and quadriceps femoris cause shortening.
•Adductor Magnus cause distal femur varus
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 5

Clinical Features
The knee is deformed and swollen, movements are impossible, don’t forget
to examine the distal pulses.
• Plain radiography:
the line above the condyles and may extend
between them with comminution; displacement and tilting are
posterior.
• CT Scan: intraarticular extension, Hoffa fracture
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 6
=====PAGE 7 [T]=====
Treatment
1. Non operative(rare):
Nondisplaced and non ambulatory patient.
Hinged knee brace with early range of motion and non
weightbearing for 6 weeks.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 7
=====PAGE 8 [T]=====
2. Operative: provide early mobilization and regain of knee
flexion:
• Retrograde intramedullary nail: (if no intraarticular extension, good for
osteoporotic fractures).
• Locking Anatomical plate :(intercondylar fracture with comminution).
• Dynamic condylar screw :(Intraarticular Extension without
comminution).
.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 8
=====PAGE 9 [T]=====
Complications
1. Arterial damage: Popliteal artery in danger even with
minimal displacement, if happen need exploration and
repair.
2. Joint stiffness: If intraarticular and exercise is not
performed well.
3. Non union: rare if associated with knee stiffness
treatment is difficult( revision of Fixation and bone grafting).
4. Malunion.
5. Implant failure.

Dislocations Of The Knee Joint
Usually caused by considerable violence, causing rupture of
cruciate and collateral ligaments.
Clinical features: severe bruises, swelling, gross deformity,
exclude popliteal artery and common peroneal nerve injury.
Plain radiography: shows the dislocation and possible
fracture of the tibial spine (cruciate ligament avulsion).

Complications
1. Popliteal artery damage (40-50%):
need immediate repair and fasciotomy.
2. Common peroneal Nerve injury (25%)
usually neuropraxia.
3. Joint instability: due to ligament injury, need early
ligament reconstruction.
4. Stiffness: due to prolonged immobilization.

Fracture Patella
• The largest sesamoid bone; function to increase the
efficacy of the quadriceps muscles, protection of the knee.
• Its part of extensor mechanism (quadriceps tendon, patella,
patellar ligament, extensor retinaculum).
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 16
=====PAGE 17 [T]=====
Mechanism of injury
1. Direct impact: Fall onto the knee, dashboard injury
causing undisplaced crack or comminuted fracture with
minimal injury to the extensor expansion.
2. Indirect Force: Strong passive knee flexion while the
quadriceps is contracting, causing transverse fracture with
displacement.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 17
=====PAGE 18 [T]=====
Clinical features
• Pain, swelling, abrasions in front of the knee, a gap may
be felt (transverse fracture).
• Test active knee extension, if present extensor
mechanism is intact.
• Aspiration of the knee shows hemarthroses with fat
droplets.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 18

Plain radiography: (AP and lateral view)
1. Transverse fracture with displacement.
2. Comminuted fracture (stellate).

Treatment
(cid:0) Undisplaced and minimally displaced.
(cid:0) Intact extensor mechanism:
Aspirate the hemarthroses and immobilization in a POP cylinder in

(cid:0) Comminuted displaced fractures, non salvagable.
Patellectomy otherwise it ends with Osteoarthritis.
