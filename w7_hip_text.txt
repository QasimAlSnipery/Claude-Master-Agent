Orthopaedic conditions of the Hip joint
Avascular necrosis of the hip (Hip osteonecrosis)
Hip Osteonecrosis, also known as avascular necrosis of the hip, represents a condition caused by
reduced blood flow to the femoral head secondary to a variety of risk factors such as a traumatic
event, sickle cell disease, steroid use, alcoholism, autoimmune disorders, and hypercoagulable
states.

Hip Osteoarthritis
Hip Osteoarthritis is degenerative disease of
the hip joint that causes progressive loss of
articular cartilage of the femoral head and
acetabulum.

Etiology
◦ Pathophysiology
◦ pathoanatomy
◦ articular cartilage
◦ increased water content
◦ decrease in amount of proteoglycans
◦ Collagen organization and binding of proteoglycans to hyaluronic acid are lost
◦ synovium and capsule
◦ early phase of OA
◦ mild inflammatory changes in synovium
◦ middle phase of OA
◦ moderate inflammatory changes of synovium
◦ late phases of OA
◦ synovium becomes increasingly thick and vascular
◦ subchondral bone attempts to remodel
◦ forming lytic lesion with sclerotic edges (different than bone cysts in RA)
◦ bone cysts form in late stages

TOTAL HIP REPLACEMENT
Total replacement of the articular surfaces seems
the ideal way of treating any disorder causing joint
destruction. However, there are several problems
to be overcome:
(1) the prosthetic implants must be durable
(2) they must permit extraordinary low friction
movement at the articulation;
(3) they must be firmly fixed to the skeleton.
(4) they must be inert and not provoke any
unwanted reaction in the tissues.
11/28/2024 DR. MUSTAFA NAWZAD MIRZA 35

INDICATIONS
The indications for hip replacement include pain, loss of movement and associated disability in
the presence of radiographic evidence of joint destruction. Formerly, the procedure was largely
restricted to the elderly and the infirm. The success of the early implants and vastly improved
access to information have persuaded patients that an unacceptable compromise in the quality
of life represents a valid indication for joint replacement. These patients expect to return to a full
profile of professional and recreational activities. Given their increased expectations it is
important that the risks and benefits of total hip replacement be fully discussed with them.
11/28/2024 DR. MUSTAFA NAWZAD MIRZA 39
=====PAGE 40 [T]=====
Contraindications
Overt or latent sepsis is the chief contraindication to joint replacement. An infected arthroplasty
spells disaster. Patients under 60 years of age are considered if other operations are unsuitable.
