# Breastfeeding

## The Big Picture
Breast milk is the ideal infant food — **exclusive breastfeeding for the first 6 months** (no other food/drink), then continued **to 2 years** with complementary foods. It cuts infant mortality/morbidity, protects via antibodies, and benefits the mother too. Know the **hormones (prolactin/oxytocin)**, the **3 phases of milk**, fore- vs hind-milk, and correct attachment.

## Key Recommendations (WHO/AAP)
- **Exclusive breastfeeding 0–6 months**; continue to **2 years** + complementary foods from 6 mo.
- **Initiate within first hour** after birth.
- Only ~44% of infants exclusively breastfed worldwide (WHO 2024); target 70% by 2030.

## Hormones
- **Prolactin (anterior pituitary)** — makes milk; **more suckling → more prolactin → more milk**.
- **Oxytocin (posterior pituitary)** — milk ejection ("let-down").
  - **Stimulated by:** suckling, seeing/hearing/thinking of baby, confidence, relaxation.
  - **Inhibited by:** stress, anxiety, pain, fatigue, embarrassment, smoking/alcohol.

## Composition
- Mature milk ≈ **87% water, 7% lactose, 4% fat, 1% protein** (lots of water → no extra water needed).
- Varies by: stage of lactation, time of day (**fat highest midday**), maternal nutrition, climate (hot → more water, cold → more fat), fore/hind milk, individual.

## Phases of Lactation
- **Colostrum (week 1)** — yellowish, thick, small quantity; **high protein/minerals/fat-soluble vitamins + immunoglobulins (passive immunity = "1st vaccine")**; aids gut flora + meconium passage.
- **Transitional milk (7–14 days)** — bluish, larger volume, more fat/lactose, less protein.
- **Mature milk (after 2 weeks)** — ~65–75 kcal/100 mL.
  - **Fore-milk** (start) — water, protein, vitamins.
  - **Hind-milk** (later) — **high fat → growth + weight gain**.

## Technique
- **Positioning:** mother comfortable/supported; baby's neck-back-shoulders supported, facing mother, nose to nipple.
- **Attachment:** mouth wide open, **chin touches breast, lower lip turned out, more areola visible above than below**.
- **Reflexes:** rooting (finds nipple), sucking, swallowing.

## Benefits
- **Baby:** optimal nutrition, antibodies/immunity, easy digestion, gut health, cognition, fewer allergies/asthma.
- **Mother:** uterine contraction (less postpartum bleeding), weight loss, bonding, **↓ breast/ovarian cancer + type 2 diabetes**, **natural contraception** (delays menses), ↓ postpartum depression.

## ABCs
**A**wareness (on-demand feeding), **B**e patient, **C**omfort (relaxed → let-down). Support: lactation consultants, peer groups, workplace policies.

## Walk-Away Points
- **Exclusive 6 months, continue to 2 years, initiate within 1 hour**.
- **Prolactin = production, oxytocin = ejection**; suckling drives both.
- **Colostrum = "first vaccine"** (immunoglobulins); **hind-milk = fat for growth**.
- Maternal benefits: ↓ breast/ovarian cancer, contraception, bonding.
