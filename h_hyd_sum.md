# Hydatid Disease (Echinococcosis)

## The Big Picture
Hydatid disease = larval infection by the tapeworm ***Echinococcus granulosus***. **Dogs are the definitive host**; humans are accidental intermediate hosts who swallow eggs → slow-growing **hydatid cysts**, mostly in **liver (>65%) and lungs (25%)**. Often silent for years; danger = **rupture → anaphylaxis** + organ damage. Diagnose by imaging + serology; treat with surgery/PAIR + **albendazole**.

## Parasite & Life Cycle
- **E. granulosus** (cestode). **Definitive host = dogs/carnivores** (carry adult worm, shed eggs in faeces). **Intermediate hosts = sheep/cattle/humans.**
- Human swallows eggs → oncosphere penetrates intestine → blood/lymph → organs → **hydatid cyst (metacestode)** containing thousands of protoscolices.
- **E. multilocularis** = more dangerous (fine invasive strands, behaves like cancer).

## Histopathology
Cyst = **outer laminated (acellular) layer + inner germinal layer + brood capsules/protoscolices**, surrounded by host fibrous capsule; **daughter cysts** bud inward; host granulomatous reaction.

## Clinical
- **Most asymptomatic** (incidental on imaging); symptoms when cyst reaches **8–10 cm** or compresses/erodes structures.
- **Liver (>65%):** abdominal pain, hepatomegaly, palpable mass, decreased appetite.
- **Lung (25%):** chronic cough, chest pain, dyspnoea.
- Other: brain (early symptoms in childhood), bone (fractures), eye (exophthalmos), heart, kidney.
- **Rupture → anaphylaxis** (fever, hypotension), superinfection (abscess), seeding. 20–40% multi-organ.

## Diagnosis
- **Imaging:** **ultrasound = preferred** for liver/abdominal (+ screening/monitoring); CT/X-ray (lung). Can't determine species.
- **Eosinophilia** on CBC (sometimes); **serology (ELISA) + confirmatory immunoblot**. LFTs unreliable (ALP may rise; AST/ALT/bilirubin usually normal).

## Treatment
- **Surgery** = choice for: large/complicated cysts, superficial (rupture-prone), infected, biliary communication, pressure on organs. (E. multilocularis surgery very hard — risk of seeding.)
- **PAIR** (Puncture, Aspiration, Injection, Re-aspiration) — US-guided, minimally invasive; for inoperable, relapse, drug failure.
- **Benzimidazoles — albendazole (12–15 mg/kg/day, drug of choice)** or mebendazole; for cysts <5 cm, multi-organ, inoperable, and **post-surgery to prevent recurrence (≥2 years)**. **Contraindicated in rupture-prone cysts + early pregnancy.**

## Complications
- Liver cyst rupture into biliary tree → obstruction, superinfection, peritonitis.
- Lung cyst rupture into bronchus → pneumonitis, pneumothorax, pleural effusion.

## Epidemiology & Prevention
- More common where **dogs guard/herd livestock**; Mediterranean, Middle East, Russia, China, Africa, S America, Australia.
- **Prevention:** deworm dogs/cats regularly, avoid stray animals, hygiene (gloves/boots for hunters handling animal fur).

## Walk-Away Points
- ***E. granulosus*; dog = definitive host**, human accidental; cysts in **liver (>65%) > lung**.
- Often silent; **rupture → anaphylaxis**; **US = preferred imaging** + ELISA serology.
- Treat: **surgery / PAIR + albendazole** (≥2 yr post-op); avoid aspiration of rupture-prone cysts.
- Prevent by deworming dogs.
