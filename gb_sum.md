# GALL BLADDER & BILIARY TREE

## Anatomy & physiology
- **GB**: 7–12 cm, 30–50 mL (distends to 300 mL). Parts: fundus, body, neck → **Hartmann's pouch** (infundibulum). **No submucosa/muscularis mucosa** (differs from GIT). Mucosal crypts = **crypts of Luschka**.
- **Cystic duct**: spiral mucosal folds = **valves of Heister**; sphincter of Lütkens.
- **Cystic artery**: 90% from right hepatic artery, in **Calot's triangle** (cystic duct, CHD, liver edge). **Moynihan's hump** = tortuous hepatic artery in front of cystic duct (danger in surgery).
- **CBD**: 7–11 cm; 4 parts (supraduodenal, retroduodenal, infraduodenal/pancreatic, intraduodenal); opens at **ampulla of Vater** via **sphincter of Oddi**.
- **Sphincter of Oddi**: regulates bile/pancreatic flow, prevents reflux, diverts bile to GB. Relaxes when **CCK** rises.
- Nerve: **vagus = stimulant**, sympathetic (celiac) = inhibitory.
- **Bile**: 500–1000 mL/day; 97% water + bile salts + pigment. CCK contracts GB + relaxes Oddi; **somatostatin inhibits GB contraction (↑gallstone risk)**.
- **Enterohepatic circulation**: primary bile salts (cholate, deoxycholate) → 80% reabsorbed in terminal ileum; gut bacteria → secondary bile acids.

## Diagnostic studies
- **Bloods**: LFT (↑bilirubin + alk phos in cholangitis/cholestasis); **normal in biliary colic**.
- **Plain X-ray**: 10% radio-opaque stones, porcelain GB, limey bile.
- **Ultrasound = standard** for gallstones + jaundice.
- **HIDA scan**: acute cholecystitis, ampullary obstruction, post-op leaks.
- **MRCP = gold standard for biliary tree anatomy + CBD stones.**
- **ERCP**: diagnostic + therapeutic (papillotomy, stone removal, stenting). **PTC** if ERCP unavailable. EUS for periampullary/depth.
- **CT**: inferior to US for stones; used for malignancy.

## Congenital anomalies
- GB anatomy classic in only 1/3. Absent GB (rare), **Phrygian cap** (2–6%), duplicated GB, floating GB (torsion risk), ducts of Luschka.
- Cystic/hepatic artery anomalies in **50%**.
- **Extrahepatic biliary atresia**: 1/4000; BD destroyed by inflammation → cirrhosis; jaundice from birth, pale stool, dark urine; **untreated dies before age 3**. Type 1 (CBD) → Roux-en-Y; Types 2,3 → **Kasai procedure**.
- **Caroli's disease**: congenital intrahepatic duct dilatation → stones, cholangitis.
- **Choledochal cyst**: congenital dilatation of biliary tree; **female 6:1**, eastern countries; >90% anomalous pancreaticobiliary junction; **premalignant** → excision + Roux-en-Y hepaticojejunostomy.

## Gallstones (cholelithiasis)
- Commonest GIT surgery in west; rare Africa/India. **F:M 3:1** (equal in old age). **Saint's triad** = gallstones + hiatus hernia + colonic diverticulosis.
- Asymptomatic → 3%/yr become symptomatic; complicated 3–5%/yr.
- **Risk (5 F's)**: Fat, Female, Forty, Fertile, Family. Also: obesity, pregnancy, ileal disease/resection, haemolytic disease (sickle, thalassemia, spherocytosis).
- **Mechanisms**: metabolic (cholesterol supersaturation = lithogenic bile), infection (E. coli, pigment stones), bile stasis.
- **Types**: **Cholesterol** (west, 80%, solitary/large, mostly radiolucent), **pigment** (<20% cholesterol — **black** = haemolysis/cirrhosis, in GB; **brown** = infection/parasites, in ducts, Asia).
- **Effects**: GB (silent, acute/chronic cholecystitis, mucocele, empyema, gangrene, perforation, carcinoma); ducts (obstructive jaundice, cholangitis, biliary pancreatitis); intestine (**gallstone ileus**).

## Acute cholecystitis
- 90–95% from gallstones obstructing cystic duct → inflammation (lysolecithin, bile salts).
- **Clinical**: biliary colic NOT subsiding for days, RUQ/epigastric/back pain, fever, N/V; **Murphy's sign +ve**, guarding, ± mass.
- **Investigations**: ↑WBC (>20,000 = complication), mild ↑LFT; **US 95%** sonographic features; HIDA if atypical.
- **Treatment**: **conservative** (bed rest, IVF, analgesia, antibiotics, NGT) — **90% respond → interval cholecystectomy** at 6–10 wks. 10% no response or gangrene/perforation → **cholecystectomy** (laparoscopic/open); unfit → percutaneous cholecystostomy.

## Chronic cholecystitis (biliary colic)
- Cystic duct obstruction → distension. Aschoff-Rokitansky sinuses. Pain 1–5h, after fatty/heavy meal, ± N/V, bloating/belching.
- Investigation: WBC/LFT normal; **US (stones, sludge)**.
- Treatment: avoid heavy/fatty meals; **cholecystectomy** if diabetic, atypical recurrent, or symptomatic. **Mucocele** (stone impacted, bile absorbed, mucus secreted → palpable non-tender GB) → cholecystectomy.

## Other benign conditions
- **Cholecystoses**: cholesterosis (**strawberry GB** — yellow specks), cholesterol polyps, adenomyomatosis. Cholecystectomy if symptomatic/enlarging.
- **Empyema**: GB full of pus (sequel of cholecystitis/mucocele) → drainage + cholecystectomy.
- **Acalculous cholecystitis**: no stone; **after major surgery/trauma/burns/ICU**; missed → 20% mortality. → cholecystostomy.
- **Cholangitis**: ascending bacterial infection + biliary obstruction (E. coli, Klebsiella). **Charcot's triad = pain + fever + jaundice** (2/3); **Reynolds pentad** = + shock + confusion. Treat IVF + antibiotics; 15% need decompression (ERCP/PTC/surgery).
- **Choledocholithiasis**: 6–12% of gallstone patients. **Primary** (Asian, pigment, stasis) vs **secondary** (western, cholesterol, from GB). → obstructive jaundice, cholangitis, pancreatitis. **Courvoisier's law**: palpable GB + jaundice → NOT stone (think cancer). **MRCP gold standard**; treat **ERCP + papillotomy**.
- **Biliary pancreatitis**: stone at ampulla → ERCP + stone removal, then cholecystectomy.
- **Recurrent pyogenic cholangitis (cholangiohepatitis)**: Orient; bacteria + parasites → brown pigment stones, strictures, abscess. MRCP/PTC; treat stones ± hepaticojejunostomy.
- **Sclerosing cholangitis**: inflammatory stricture intra/extrahepatic. Primary (unknown) or secondary; **2/3 associated with UC**; **cholangiocarcinoma risk 10–20%**. ERC = "beading"; treat stenting/transplant.
- **Parasites**: Ascaris (via ampulla), hydatid cyst, **Clonorchis sinensis** (BD carcinoma risk), **Fasciola hepatica** (water cress; eosinophilia; triclabendazole).
- **Typhoid GB** (Typhoid Mary = carrier), **torsion of GB** (elderly, long mesentery), **post-cholecystectomy syndrome** (15%; exclude retained CBD/stump stone, stricture).

## Bile duct injury & stricture
- BD injury: mostly **iatrogenic** (cholecystectomy). Pain, fever, ↑LFT, bile leak/peritonitis, jaundice. Treat by drainage/stent → **Roux-en-Y hepaticojejunostomy**.
- BD stricture: 80% post-operative; unrecognized → recurrent cholangitis + secondary biliary cirrhosis.

## Biliary tumours
- **Cholangiocarcinoma**: M=F, 50–70 yrs; risk = **PSC, UC, choledochal cyst, Clonorchis, hepatolithiasis, typhoid carrier, thorotrast**. Common at **hilar bifurcation = Klatskin tumour** (Bismuth-Corlette classification). **Painless jaundice + pruritus + weight loss**. Markers CA19-9, CEA. Resectable only 10–20%; median survival 18 mo.
- **Gallbladder carcinoma**: rare; **F:M 5:1**; **>90% have gallstones**; mostly adenocarcinoma. Risk: stones >3cm (10×), polyp >10mm, **porcelain GB**, choledochal cyst, anomalous junction. Spreads to liver early; 40% metastatic at diagnosis. Treat by cholecystectomy → extended cholecystectomy + hepatectomy depending on depth.

## High-yield one-liners
- **Calot's triangle (cystic duct, CHD, liver) → cystic artery.** Hartmann's pouch, valves of Heister.
- **5 F's; cholesterol stones (west, radiolucent); black = haemolysis; brown = infection.**
- **Murphy's sign = acute cholecystitis; US 95%; 90% respond to conservative → interval cholecystectomy.**
- **Charcot's triad (pain+fever+jaundice) = cholangitis; Reynolds pentad +shock+confusion.**
- **Courvoisier's law: palpable GB + jaundice ≠ stone (think cancer).**
- **MRCP = gold standard biliary tree/CBD stones; ERCP = therapeutic.**
- **Choledochal cyst + PSC = cholangiocarcinoma risk; Klatskin = hilar.**
- **GB cancer: F 5:1, &gt;90% gallstones, porcelain GB risk, poor prognosis.**
- **Biliary atresia: Kasai procedure; untreated dies &lt;3yr.**
