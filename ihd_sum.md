# SURGERY FOR ISCHAEMIC HEART DISEASE (IHD)

## Pathophysiology
- Progressive coronary blockage by **atherothrombotic disease**. Luminal compromise → supply/demand imbalance → **chronic stable angina**.
- **Plaque rupture + thrombosis → acute coronary syndromes (ACS)**: unstable angina, **NSTEMI, STEMI**.

## Diagnosis
- Clinical, blood tests (troponin), **ECG**, treadmill, echo, **CT angiography, coronary angiography**.

## Medical management
- **Aspirin, beta-blockers, nitrates, CCB** (if BB contraindicated), **statins** (plaque stabilization), **ACE inhibitors**, clopidogrel.

## Acute management
- **STEMI: PCI (90-min door-to-balloon)** or thrombolytic (alternative). **"Time is myocardium."**
- **ACS**: aspirin + heparin; **clopidogrel** (better outcome if held 30 days... post-CABG); **prasugrel stopped 7 days pre-op**; **GP IIb/IIIa inhibitors (tirofiban, eptifibatide)** if continuing ischaemia, rest pain, HD instability, HF, ↑troponin, ECG changes.
- **PCI**: bare-metal stent (BMS) vs drug-eluting stent (DES).

## Surgery (CABG)
- Surgery effective: relieves angina, delays infarction, **improves survival vs medical/PCI** in selected.
- **Indications — clinical**: class III/IV angina, ischaemic pulmonary oedema, failed PCI, patient undergoing major vascular surgery.
- **Indications — anatomic**: **left main >50%**, **3-vessel disease (3VD) with EF <50%**, 3VD EF >50% with inducible ischaemia, lesions not amenable to PCI.
- **Other**: >50% stenosis in branch arteries + valve surgery, MI complications, coronary anomalies with sudden death risk.
- **Conduits**: venous vs **arterial** (arterial superior — e.g. LIMA).
- **Procedures**: on-pump CABG, **OPCAB (off-pump)**, MIDCAB, TECAB, robotic, laser transmyocardial revascularization.
- **Post-op**: beta-blocker, aspirin, statin, ACE inhibitor.

## MI complications (surgical)
- **Septal wall rupture, free wall rupture, ischaemic mitral regurgitation, LV aneurysm.**

## High-yield one-liners
- **ACS = plaque rupture + thrombosis (unstable angina/NSTEMI/STEMI).**
- **STEMI → PCI 90-min door-to-balloon; "time is myocardium".**
- **CABG anatomic indications: left main >50%, 3VD + EF <50%, lesions not PCI-amenable.**
- **Arterial conduit (LIMA) superior to venous; prasugrel stop 7 days pre-op.**
- **MI surgical complications: septal/free wall rupture, ischaemic MR, LV aneurysm.**
- **Post-op + medical IHD: aspirin + beta-blocker + statin + ACE inhibitor.**
