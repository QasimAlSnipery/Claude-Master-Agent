Avascular necrosis of the hip (Hip
osteonecrosis)
Hip Osteonecrosis, also known as
avascular necrosis of the
hip, represents a condition caused by
reduced blood flow to the femoral
head secondary to a variety of risk
factors such as a traumatic event,
sickle cell disease, steroid use,
alcoholism, autoimmune disorders,
and hypercoagulable states.

TOTAL HIP REPLACEMENT
Total replacement of the articular surfaces seems
the ideal way of treating any disorder causing
joint destruction. However, there are several
problems to be overcome:
(1) the prosthetic implants must be durable
(2) they must permit extraordinary low friction
movement at the articulation;
(3) they must be firmly fixed to the skeleton.
(4) they must be inert and not provoke any
unwanted reaction in the tissues.
