I have been practicing pain medicine for 28 years. And my patients paid for that gap in my knowledge with years of unnecessary pain.

What I missed — what most of my colleagues are still missing — is not complicated. It is one molecule. One that the body produces naturally, that every major tissue and nerve structure depends on, and whose decline after 50 is now one of the most well-documented findings in pain research. I did not learn about it in medical school. I found it going through the research literature on my own, years into my career, and what I found made me genuinely angry — at myself, and at a system that had pointed me and my patients in the wrong direction for decades.

Before I explain what it is and why it matters, let me tell you about Carol.

The Patient Who Changed How I Practice

Carol had started holding the stair rail with both hands and going up one step at a time. She was 59.

None of it had changed the underlying picture. The ibuprofen gave her a few hours of relief, but her regular doctor had flagged concerns about long-term kidney damage. The CBD relaxed her in the evenings but did nothing for the physical pain. She came to me looking for a prescription solution. I almost gave her one.

Instead, I went back through recent research before our next appointment — something a busy practice rarely leaves time for. What I found changed the conversation I had with Carol, and every patient like her since.

Why Everything You’ve Tried Has Only Ever Worked Temporarily

I have recommended versions of everything on this counter. I understand now why none of them address the underlying problem.

Let me be direct about something most doctors do not say clearly enough. Ibuprofen, Aleve, and the other anti-inflammatories are not pain treatments. They are pain suppressants. They temporarily block the pain signal while you are taking them. The moment you stop, the underlying cause is in exactly the same state it was. Nothing has been corrected. Nothing has been restored. You borrowed the relief — and your body takes it back the day you stop making payments. That is why every time you try to cut back, everything comes back within 48 hours. That is the design. Not a flaw. The design.

On CBD: I am not dismissive of CBD as a compound. It has real effects on the part of the nervous system that handles stress, sleep, and mood. But for the specific problem driving chronic joint and nerve pain in adults over 50 — which I will explain in a moment — that system is not the primary driver. CBD is aimed at the wrong target. That is why its effects, when they appear at all, tend to be mild and inconsistent for this type of pain. It is the right tool for a different job.
On turmeric and glucosamine: Turmeric has real anti-inflammatory properties in the lab. The problem is that most standard capsules do not absorb properly — the active part passes through your body before it reaches your joints. And glucosamine supports cartilage structure over time, which is a legitimate benefit — but it does nothing to address the nerve sensitivity that produces the daily aching my patients describe. You can have perfectly healthy cartilage and still be in constant pain. Both of these are aimed at the wrong part of the problem.
These are not bad products. They are products aimed at the wrong problem. And no amount of the wrong solution fixes the right one.

What I Found in the Research That Changed Everything

In 1957, researchers identified a compound that the body produces naturally — in immune cells, nerve tissue, and connective tissue — as a direct response to stress and sustained pain. They called it Palmitoylethanolamide, or PEA. It is not a drug. It is not an herbal extract. It is something your own body makes, specifically designed to act as a natural pain dampener. When tissue comes under sustained stress, PEA is released to tell the system to stand down.

Here is the simple version of how it works. Your body has a built-in volume knob for pain. When something hurts — a joint, a nerve, a muscle — PEA turns that knob down. In a healthy system, this is seamless: pain flares up, the body responds, things quiet back down within a day or two. After 50, PEA production drops. The knob stops working properly. Pain signals that should last a couple of days stay turned up for weeks, then months. The daily aching becomes the new normal — not because structural damage is getting worse, but because the body has lost the molecule it uses to regulate itself.
