Thyrotoxicosis
It describes a constellation of clinical
features arising from elevated circulating
levels of thyroid hormone. The most
common causes are Grave’s disease,
MNG and autonomously functioning
thyroid nodule (Toxic Adenoma).
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 7
=====PAGE 8 [T]=====
Causes & their relative frequencies
1) Grave’s disease 76%
2) Multinodular goitre 14%
3) Solitary thyroid adenoma 5%
4) Thyroiditis (Subacute 3%, Post-partum 0.5%)
5) Iodide induced( drugs amiodarone1%, radiographic
contrast media, iodine prophylaxis program)
6) Extrathyroidal source of TH (factitious thyrotoxicosis
0.2%, strauma ovali)
7) TSH induced
8) TSH secreting pituitary adenoma 0.2%, choriocarcinoma
& hydatidiform mole
9) Follicular Ca +- metastasis 0.1%

=====PAGE 14 [T]=====
All causes of thyrotoxicosis can cause lid lag & lid
retraction, due to potentiation of sympathetic
innervations of the levator palpebrae muscles, but
only Grave’s disease causes other features of
ophtalmopathy, including periorbital oedema,
conjunctival irritation, exophthalmos & diplopia.
Peritibial myxoedema & the rare thyroid acropachy (a
periostial hypertrophy, indistinguishable from finger
clubbing) are also specific to Grave’s disease.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 14
=====PAGE 15 [T]=====
Differential diagnosis
If the patient is clinically thyrotoxic, we
look for features of Grave’s disease
(diffuse goitre with bruit,
ophthalmopathy, peritibial myxoedema,
positive TSH receptor AB -TRAb). Also
look for any features of Non-Grave’s
disease ( recent, <6 months pregnancy,
neck pain, flu like illness, palpable MNG
or solitary nodule). In either situation we
need to perform thyroid Scintigraphy.

Investigations
The first line are serum T3,T4 & TSH. In most
patients, serum T3 & T4 are both elevated, but T4
is in the upper part of the reference range & T3
raised (T3 Toxicosis) in about 5%. Serum TSH is
undetectable in in primary thyrotoxicosis, but
values can be raised in the very rare syndrome of
secondary thyrotoxicosis caused by TSH-producing
pituitary adenoma. When biochemical
thyrotoxicosis has been confirmed, further
investigations should be undertaken to determine
the underlying cause, including measurement of
TSH receptor AB (TRAb elevated in Grave’s
disease) & isotop scanning.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 17
=====PAGE 18 [T]=====
Radio-iodine uptake test measures the
proportion of isotope that is trapped in the
whole gland, but have been largely
superseded by 99m technetium scintigraphy
scan, which also indicate trapping are quicker
to perform with a lower dose of radioactivity
& provide a higher resolution image. In low-
uptake thyrotoxicosis the cause is usually
transient thyroiditis.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 18
=====PAGE 19 [T]=====
In Factitious Thyrotoxicosis, the exogenous
thyroxine suppresses pituitary TSH secretion &
hence iodine uptake, serum thyroglobulin &
release of endogenous thyroid hormones. The
T4:T3 ratio (typically 30:1 in conventional
thyrotoxicosis, is increased to above 70:1 because
T3 is derived exclusively from peripheral
Monoiodination of T4 and not from thyroid
secretion. The combination of negligible iodine
uptake, high T4:T3 ratio & low or undetectable
thyroglobulin is diagnostic.

Management
Definitive treatment of thyrotoxicosis depends
on the underlying cause and may include anti-
thyroid drugs, radioactive iodine or surgery. A
non-selective beta blocker such as propranolol or
nadalol will alleviate but not abolish symptoms
in most patients within 24-48 hours. It should not
be used for long term treatment of
thyrotoxicosis, but extremely useful in short
term.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 21
=====PAGE 22 [T]=====
Thyrotoxic Crisis (Thyroid storm)
It’s a rare but life-threatening complication of
thyrotoxicosis. It’s a medical emergency with
10% mortality rate despite early recognition &
treatment. It’s commonly precipitated by
infection in a patient with previously
unrecognized or inadequately treated
thyrotoxicosis. It may also develop shortly
after subtotal thyroidectomy in an ill prepared
patient or within a few days of iodine therapy.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 22
=====PAGE 23 [T]=====
The most important signs are; fever,
agitation, confusion, tachycardia or AF
& cardiac failure in older patients.
Patient should be rehydrated and given
propranolol either orally 80mg 4 times
daily or IV 1-5 mg 4 times daily. Sodium
Ipodate 500mg per day will restore T3
levels to normal in 48-72 hours.
Dexamethasone 2mg 4 times daily &
Amiodarone have similar effects.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 23
=====PAGE 24 [T]=====
Oral Carbimazole 40-60mg daily
should be given to inhibit the
synthesis of new hormone. If the
patient is unconscious or
uncooperative, Carbimazole can
be given rectally but no
preparation available for
parenteral use. After
10-14 days the patient can be
maintained on Carbimazole alone.

Autoimmune Thyroid Disease
Thyroid diseases are amongst the most prevalent
antibody-mediated autoimmune diseases & are
associated with other organ Specific
Autoimmunity. Autoantibodies may produce
inflammation & destruction of thyroid tissue
resulting in:
1) Hypothyroidism
2) Goitre (in Hashimoto’s thyroiditis)
3) Transient thyrotoxicosis (Hashitoxicosis)
4) Thyrotoxicosis (Grave’s disease)
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 25
=====PAGE 26 [T]=====
Grave’s disease
It can occurs at any age but is unusual before
puberty & commonly affects women aged
30-50 years. The most common
manifestation is thyrotoxicosis with or
without a diffuse goitre. Grave’s disease also
causes Ophthalmopathy and rarely Pretibial
Myxoedema. These extrathyroidal features
can occur in the absence of thyroid
dysfunction.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 26
=====PAGE 27 [T]=====
Grave’s thyrotoxicosis
Pathophysiology
Thyroxicosis results from production of
IgG AB directed against the TSH
receptors on thyroid follicular cells which
stimulate thyroid hormone production &
proliferation of follicular cells leading to
goitre in the majority of patients. The
concentration of TRAb in the serum is
presumed to fluctuate to account for
natural history of Grave’s thyrotoxicois.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 27
=====PAGE 28 [T]=====
The thyroid failure seen in some patients
may result from the presence of blocking
Ab against TSH receptors.
Grave’s disease has a strong genetic
component. There is 50% concordance
for thyrotoxicosis between monozygotic
twins but only 5% concordance between
dizygotic twins.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 28
=====PAGE 29 [T]=====
A suggested trigger for the development
of thyrotoxicosis in genetically
susceptible individuals may be infection
with virus or bacteria. Iodine
supplementation can precipitate
thyrotoxicosis in subclinical Grave’s
disease. Smoking is strongly associated
with development of ophthalmopathy.

=====PAGE 31 [T]=====
Antithyroid drugs
The most commonly used is Carbimazole & its
active metabolite, Methimazole.
Propylthiouracil is equally active. These drugs
reduce synthesis of new thyroid hormones by
inhibiting iodination of tyrosine. Carbimazole has
got an immunosuppressive effect that can reduce
TRAB concentration but not enough to influence
the natural history of the disease.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 31
=====PAGE 32 [T]=====
Antithyroid drugs should be introduced at
high doses (carbimazole 40-60mg/day or
PTU 400-600mg/day). Usually this results in
subjective improvement within10-14 days,
renders patient clinically & biochemically
euthyroid at 3-4 weeks. In most of the cases
carbimazole can be continued at 5-20mg daily
for 12-18 months. Patients with thyrotoxicosis
relapse in at least 50% of cases usually within
2 years of stopping treatment
.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 32
=====PAGE 33 [T]=====
Rarely, T4 & TSH levels fluctuate between
those of thyrotoxicosis & hypothyroidism
despite good drug compliance, presumably
due to rapidly changing concentration of
TRAB. In these patients control can be
achieved by blocking thyroid hormone
synthesis with Carbimazole 30-40mg daily
and adding levothyroxine 100-150mcg daily
as replacement therapy, this is so called Block
& Replace regime.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 33
=====PAGE 34 [T]=====
Agranulocytosis is rare but potentially a
serious complication that cannot be
predicted by the routine measurement of
WBC count & is reversible on stopping
the medication. Patient should stop the
medication and seek medical help when a
severe sore throat or fever develops while
on the treatment.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 34
=====PAGE 35 [T]=====
PTU is associated with a small but definite
risk of Heptotoxicity which some time can
result in liver failure requiring liver
transplantation and even in death. For this
reason it should be considered as a second line
therapy to Carbimazole & only be used during
breast feeding & pregnancy.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 35
=====PAGE 36 [T]=====
Thyroid surgery
Patients should be rendered Euthyroid
with antithyroid drugs before operation.
Potassium Iodide, 60mg 3 times daily
orally is often added for 2 weeks before
surgery to inhibit thyroid hormone release
& reduce the size & vascularity of the
gland. Traditionally a subtotal
thyroidectomy is performed. After
operation 80% of patients are euthyroid,
15% are permanently hypothyroid & 5%
remain thyrotoxic.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 36
=====PAGE 37 [T]=====
As a consequence many surgeons nowadays
perform near total thyroidectomy, leaving
behind a very small portion of the gland
adjacent to recurrent laryngeal nerve. This
result in permanent hypothyroidism and
probably associated with higher risk of
hypoparathyroidism but maximize the
potential cure of thyrotoxicois.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 37
=====PAGE 38 [T]=====
Radioactive iodine
It is administered orally as a single dose
and it’s trapped and organified in the
thyroid. During the lag period, symptoms
can be controlled by beta blockers or in
more severe cases by Carbimazole.
However, Carbimazole reduces the
efficacy of iodine 131 because it prevents
organification of iodine 131 in the gland,
so it should be avoided until 48 hours
after radio-iodine administration.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 38
=====PAGE 39 [T]=====
In women of reproductive age, pregnancy
must be excluded before administration of
iodine 131 & avoided for 6 months
thereafter. Men are also advised against
fathering children for 6 months after
receiving Iodine 131.

Grave’s Ophthalmopathy
It’s immunologically mediated but the
autoantigen has not been identified. Within
the orbit there is cytokine-mediated
proliferation of fibroblasts which secrete
Hydrophilic Glycosaminoglycans. The
resulting increase in interstitial fluid content,
combined with a chronic inflammatory cell
infiltrate, causes marked swelling & ultimately
fibrosis of extraocular muscles & rise in
retrobulbar pressure.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 40
=====PAGE 41 [T]=====
There will be proptosis, exophtlamos, & in
severe cases there is optic nerve compression.
Eye disease is detectable in up to 50% of
thyrotoxic patients at presentation, but active
ocular inflammation may occur before or after
thyrotoxic episodes. It’s more common in
cigarettes smokers & is exacerbated by poor
control of thyroid function.

The most frequent presenting symptoms are
related to increased exposure of the cornea.
There may be excessive lacrimation, made
worse by wind & bright light. There may be
reduction in visual acuity & visual fields as a
result of corneal oedema or optic nerve
compression.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 45
=====PAGE 46 [T]=====
The majority of patients require no treatment
other than reassurance. Smoking cessation
should be actively encouraged. Artificial tear
drops or gel counter the gritty discomfort of
dry eyes. In mild cases of grave’s
ophthalmopathy oral selenium 100 mcg twice
daily for 6 months, improves the quality of life
, reduces ocular involvement and slows the
progression of the disease.

More severe inflammatory episodes are treated
with glucocorticoids, like daily oral
prednisolone or pulsed IV methylprednisolone
& some time orbital radiotherapy.
Loss of visual acuity is an indication for
urgent surgical decompression of the orbit
.

Hypothyroidism
It’s a common condition with various causes, but
Hashimoto’s thyroiditis & thyroid failure following
Iodine 131 or surgical treatment of thyrotoxicosis
account for > 90% of cases, except in areas where
iodine deficiency is endemic. Women are affected
approximately 6 times more frequently than men.

Clinical assessment
The clinical presentation depends on the duration &
severity of the hypothyroidism.
A consequence of prolonged hypothyroidism is the
infiltration of many body tissues by the
mucopolysaccharides, hyaluronic acid &
chondrotin sulphate resulting in a low pitch voice,
poor hearing, slurred speech due to a large tongue
& compression of median nerve (carpal tunnel
syndrome).
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 51
=====PAGE 52 [T]=====
Infiltration of dermis gives rise to non- pitting
oedema (Myxoedema), which is most marked
in the skin of the hands, feet & eyelids. The
resultant peri-orbital puffiness is often striking
& may be combined with facial pallor due to
vasoconstriction & anaemia or a lemon-
yellow tint to the skin caused by
carotenaemia, along with purplish lips &
malar flush.

Investigations of Hypothyroidism
In the vast majority of cases, hypothyroidism results
from intrinsic disorder of the thyroid gland
(Primary Hypothyroidism). In this situation serum
T4 is low & TSH is elevated usually in excess of 20
mU/L. Measurements of T3 are unhelpful since they
do not discriminate reliably between Euthyroidism &
Hypothyroidism.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 59
=====PAGE 60 [T]=====
In severe prolonged hypothyroidism the ECG
classically demonstrates Sinus Bradycardia
with low voltage complexes & ST- segment
& T- wave abnormalities
.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 60
=====PAGE 61 [T]=====
Management
Treatment is with Levothyroxine replacement.
Usually we start with a low dose of 50 mcg daily
for 3 weeks, increasing thereafter to 100mcg daily
for a further 3 weeks & finally to a maintenance
dose of 100-150 mcg daily. In young patients, it’s
safe to start at a higher dose e.g. 100 mcg daily to
allow a more rapid normalization of thyroid
hormone.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 61
=====PAGE 62 [T]=====
Levothyroxine has a half-life of 7 days, so it
should always be taken as a single daily dose
& at least 6 weeks should pass before
repeating TFT & adjusting the dose, usually
by 25 mcg. Patients feel better within 2-3
weeks. Reduction in weight & per-iorbital
puffiness occurs quickly, but the restoration of
skin & hair texture & resolution of any
effusions may take 3-6 months.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 62
=====PAGE 63 [T]=====
The dose of levothyroxine should be adjusted
to maintain serum TSH within reference
range. To achieve this, serum T4 often needs
to be in the upper part of the reference range
or even slightly raised, because T3 is required
for receptor activation is derived exclusively
from conversion of T4 within target tissues
.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 63
=====PAGE 64 [T]=====
Some patients remain symptomatic despite
normalization of TSH & may wish to take
extra levothyroxine, which suppresses TSH.
However, suppressed TSH is a risk factor for
osteoporosis & AF, so this approach cannot
be recommended. It’s important to measure
thyroid function every 1-2 years once the dose
of levothyroxine is stabilized.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 64
=====PAGE 65 [T]=====
Levothyroxine absorption is maximal when
the medication is taken before bed & may be
further optimized by vitamin C supplement.
Coronary angioplasty or CABG may be
required if angina is exacerbated by
levothyroxine replacement.

=====PAGE 66 [T]=====
Myxoedema Coma
This is a very rare presentation of hypothyroidism in
which there is a depressed level of consciousness,
usually in an elderly patient who appears
myxoedematous. Body temperature may be as low
as 25 Celsius degree, convulsion is not uncommon
& CSF pressure & protein content are raised.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 66
=====PAGE 67 [T]=====
The mortality rate is 50% & survival depends
on early recognition & treatment of
hypothyroidism & other factors contributing
to the altered consciousness level such as
medication, heart failure, pneumonia,
dilutional hyponatraemia & respiratory
failure. It’s a medical emergency & treatment
must begin before biochemical confirmation
of the diagnosis.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 67
=====PAGE 68 [T]=====
Suspected cases should be treated with an IV
injection of 20 mcg of tri-iodothyronine (T3),
followed by further injections of 20mcg 3
times daily until there is a sustained clinical
improvement. Other measures include slow
rewarming, cautious use of IV fluids, broad-
spectrum AB & high-flow oxygen,
occasionally assisted ventilation may be
necessary.
12/2/2024 ZAGROS G RASHID 68
=====PAGE 69 [T]=====
Unless it is apparent that the patient has
primary hypothyroidism, the thyroid failure
should also be assumed to be secondary to
hypothalamic or pituitary disease & treatment
given with Hydrocortisone 100mg IM 3 times
daily, pending the result of T4, TSH &
cortisol measurement
.
