# Primary Health Care (PHC)

## The Big Picture
PHC = **essential health care, universally accessible, at affordable cost, with full community participation** — the route to **"Health for All."** Launched at the **Alma-Ata Declaration (1978)**, it shifts from a narrow medical/cure model to **prevention + promotion + community + intersectoral** action. Reaffirmed by the **Astana Declaration (2018)** and central to **Universal Health Coverage + SDGs**.

## Defining Health
- **WHO (1948):** "a state of complete **physical, mental and social well-being**, not merely the absence of disease."
- Highest attainable standard of health = a **fundamental right** of every human.

## Alma-Ata Declaration (1978)
- Adopted at the International Conference on PHC, **Alma-Ata, Kazakhstan, 6–12 Sept 1978**.
- **First international declaration** on the importance of PHC; urged urgent action by all governments for "Health for All."

## Definition of PHC (Alma-Ata)
"Essential health care based on **practical, scientifically sound and socially acceptable methods and technology**, made **universally accessible** to individuals and families through their **full participation** and at a **cost the community and country can afford**, in the spirit of **self-reliance and self-determination**."

## Medical Model vs PHC (the shift)
| Medical model | PHC |
|---|---|
| Treatment | **Health promotion** |
| Cure | **Prevention + care + cure** |
| Episodic care | **Continuous care** |
| Specific problems | **Comprehensive care** |
| Professional dominance | **Community participation** |
| Health sector alone | **Intersectoral collaboration** |

## Principles of PHC (4)
1. **Equitable distribution** (accessible to all).
2. **Community participation.**
3. **Intersectoral coordination.**
4. **Appropriate technology** (affordable, acceptable).

## Strategies (Alma-Ata)
1. Develop a basic **health infrastructure**.
2. **Two-way referral system** between service levels.
3. Develop **human resources** for health.
4. Incorporate **traditional medicine.**
5. Ensure **intersectoral cooperation.**

## Elements of PHC (the classic list)
Health **education**, **nutrition** (food supply), **safe water + sanitation**, **maternal & child health + family planning**, **immunization**, **prevention/control of endemic diseases**, **treatment of common diseases/injuries**, **essential drugs** (+ mental health, dental in expanded lists).

## Levels of Prevention (role of PHC)
- **Primary** — prevent disease occurring (immunisation, health education, sanitation).
- **Secondary** — early detection + treatment (**screening**).
- **Tertiary** — limit disability + **rehabilitation**.
- Key economic argument: **$1 in prevention saves much in treatment**.

## Obstacles to PHC Progress
Lack of political commitment/funding, inadequate infrastructure + workforce, poor community participation, weak intersectoral coordination, poverty, conflict.

## Astana Declaration (2018)
Reaffirmed Alma-Ata; PHC as the cornerstone of **Universal Health Coverage (UHC)** and the **Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)**; emphasises empowered people + communities, and integrated services.

## Walk-Away Points
- PHC = **essential, universally accessible, affordable, community-participatory** care = route to **Health for All**.
- **Alma-Ata 1978** birthed it; **Astana 2018** reaffirmed it (→ UHC + SDGs).
- **4 principles:** equity, community participation, intersectoral coordination, appropriate technology.
- Elements = **education, nutrition, water/sanitation, MCH/family planning, immunisation, endemic disease control, treatment, essential drugs**.
- Prevention levels: **primary (prevent), secondary (screen), tertiary (rehab)**.
