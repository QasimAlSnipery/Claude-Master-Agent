# Allergic Diseases (Immediate Hypersensitivity)

## The Big Picture
Allergy = an exaggerated **IgE-mediated (type I) hypersensitivity** to harmless environmental **allergens**. Allergen binds IgE on **mast cells → degranulation → histamine + mediators** → immediate reaction; a **late-phase response (Th2/eosinophil)** causes chronic inflammation. Spectrum runs from hay fever to fatal **anaphylaxis**. Treatment: avoid, antihistamines, steroids, immunotherapy; **anaphylaxis = adrenaline**.

## Mechanism
- **Early phase:** allergen → IgE on mast-cell high-affinity receptor → **rapid degranulation** (histamine) → bronchoconstriction, vascular leak. **Responds to antihistamines.**
- **Late phase (LPR):** hours later, **Th2 cells + eosinophils** (IL-4, IL-5, eotaxin) → chronic inflammation, prolonged wheeze. **Not responsive to antihistamines — needs corticosteroids.**

## Mediators
- **Pre-formed:** **histamine** + serotonin (bronchoconstriction, vascular permeability), chemotactic factors.
- **Newly formed:** **leukotrienes (LTC4/D4/E4 = SRS-A)** sustained bronchoconstriction/oedema, prostaglandins, **platelet-activating factor (PAF)**.

## Atopy (the allergic tendency)
- Strongly **heritable**: 2 atopic parents → 75% risk, 1 → 50%, none → 15%. ~20–30% of population atopic.
- **Hygiene hypothesis** — reduced early microbial exposure → less Treg/Th1 → more allergy (allergy rose in East Germany after 1990).
- **Th2 cells recognising allergens = the pathological hallmark.**
- Allergens often **proteolytic enzymes** in small aerodynamic particles (pollen).

## Allergic Disorders
Allergic rhinitis (hay fever), allergic eczema, bee/wasp venom allergy, food allergy, urticaria, angioedema, asthma.

## Diagnosis
- History + **skin-prick test** (weal size; needs histamine positive + saline negative controls) and/or **serum allergen-specific IgE**.
- **Mast cell tryptase** peaks 1–2 h after an event (confirms anaphylaxis), stays high 24 h.

## Treatment
1. **Avoidance** (first line).
2. **Antihistamines** — immediate reactions (no role in asthma).
3. **Corticosteroids** — suppress mediators/inflammation (the LPR).
4. **Leukotriene receptor antagonists** (montelukast) — asthma, esp. aspirin-induced.
5. **Omalizumab** (anti-IgE) — severe asthma; **Dupilumab** (anti-IL-4/IL-13) — severe eczema/asthma.
6. **Desensitisation (allergen immunotherapy)** — for IgE-mediated insect-sting, drug, rhinitis allergy; SC increasing doses then monthly maintenance 2–3 yr; sublingual for hay fever.

## Anaphylaxis
- **Serious, rapid-onset, IgE-mediated** systemic reaction; can kill in minutes. Needs prior **sensitisation then re-exposure**; allergen must be **systemically absorbed** (ingestion/injection).
- **Features:** tingling/warmth/itch → flush, urticaria, angioedema → **hypotension, bronchospasm, laryngeal oedema, arrhythmia** → death. **PAF level correlates with severity.**
- Triggers: **nuts, shellfish, penicillin, bee/wasp stings**.
- **Management: adrenaline (epinephrine) IM** — first and most important; + ABC, O₂, antihistamine, steroid. Patients carry **EpiPen (0.3 mg adult)**; education + avoidance; desensitisation for unavoidable triggers (e.g. stings). Early peanut introduction modulates risk.

## Walk-Away Points
- Allergy = **type I, IgE + mast cells → histamine**; **early phase (antihistamine) vs late phase (steroids)**.
- **Atopy heritable**; hygiene hypothesis; Th2 hallmark.
- Diagnose: **skin-prick + specific IgE**; tryptase confirms anaphylaxis.
- **Anaphylaxis → IM adrenaline** first; carry EpiPen; immunotherapy for IgE-mediated disease.
