Anesthesia Safety Overview
Anesthesia is among the safest specialties
o
Incidence: Minor (1:5), Major (1:100), Death (1:100,000)
o
Continuous improvement due to better monitoring and training
o
11/5/2025 Dr Gona Ahmed 3
=====PAGE 4 [T]=====
Risk Factors for Complications
Patient-related: ASA status, comorbidities, age
o
Procedure-related: extent and urgency of surgery
o
Environmental: staffing, equipment, emergency preparedness
o

Cardiac Arrest & Mortality Trends
Historical decline in anesthesia-related deaths:
o
• Pre 1970; 3.6:10,000
• 1970-1980; 0.5:10,000
• After 1990; 0.3:10,000
Current rate: ~25 per million in developed countries
o
Comparison with surgical mortality in ASA I–II patients
o

Difficult Airway Management
Pre-op airway assessment is critical
o
Plan for difficult cases: equipment,
o
technique, backup plans
Avoid multiple intubation attempts
o
to prevent CICO (cannot intubate
cannot oxygenate)

Aspiration Prevention Strategies
Fasting guidelines: 6–8 hrs. solids, 2 hrs. liquids
•
NG tube, antacids, prokinetics (metoclopramide )
•
RSI (rapid sequence induction- intubation) , awake extubation,
•
fiberoptic intubation

Includes infection, atelectasis, respiratory failure
o
Risk factors:
o
Age
o
ASA ≥2
o
CHF,
o
Abdominal and thoracic surgery
o
Prevention:

o
Avoid GA
o
Minimal opioids (other analgesia modalities)
o
Early mobilization and physiotherapy
o

Airway Trauma & Fire
Trauma: lips, teeth, pharynx, trachea from devices (ETT)
o
Trauma can be minor with simple bleeding or major such as
o
perforation which carry a high mortality rate.
Pulmonary barotrauma and pneumothorax (High pressure or volume)
o
Fire risk: diathermy/laser ignition of flammable materials
o

Includes ischemia, hypotension,
o

Often multifactorial and
o

=====PAGE 23 [T]=====
Myocardial Infarction (MI)
Cause: imbalance in myocardial oxygen supply/demand (hypoxia,
o
anaemia, hypotension or hypertension and tachycardia)
Prevention: optimize cardiac status, manage drugs,
o
delay elective surgery if unstable
11/5/2025 Dr Gona Ahmed 23
=====PAGE 24 [T]=====
Hypotension During Anesthesia
↓ COP, ↓ PVR or both
o
Causes: hypovolemia, anesthetic drugs, high spinal block
o
Risks: cerebral hypoperfusion, renal injury, cardiac arrest
o
11/5/2025 Dr Gona Ahmed 24
=====PAGE 25 [T]=====
Hypertension & Arrhythmias
Pre-op causes: anxiety, pain
o
Post-op causes: pain, full bladder, hypercapnia
o
Arrhythmias: often benign, treat underlying cause
o
11/5/2025 Dr Gona Ahmed 25
=====PAGE 26 [T]=====
Stroke & Vascular Access Complications
Stroke: emboli(AF), thrombosis, neck rotation, post cardiac bypass.
o
Vascular access(peripheral & central) complications: infection, air
o
embolism, mechanical injury (central venous line) such as nerve or
pneumothorax and hematoma.
11/5/2025 Dr Gona Ahmed 26
=====PAGE 27 [T]=====
Thromboembolism & Gas Embolism
DVT/Pulmonary embolism risk factors:
o
Age, BMI, immobility,
o
surgery type, site & duration,
o
HRT & contraceptive pill.
o
Gas embolism: 0.5-1 mL/kg of significant
o
Cyanosis, hypotension, tachycardia, engorged neck veins and cardiac arrest.
o
CPR, CV line aspiration
o
11/5/2025 Dr Gona Ahmed 27
=====PAGE 28 [T]=====
Neurological Complications
Awareness (accidental & brief awake paralysis):
o
- Unintentional (faulty equipment e.g. empty vaporizer), pharmacodynamic
variability & Intentional( very sick patient).
Delayed recovery: Sedative, muscle relaxant (genetic & non-genetic)
o
opioids, thiopental or propofol, volatile anaesthetic agents or surgical
and metabolic causes.
11/5/2025 Dr Gona Ahmed 28
=====PAGE 29 [T]=====
CNB & PNB Complications
Complication of central neuraxial blockade/peripheral neuraxial
o
blockade (Rare but potentially devastating)
CNB: infection, abscess, headache, nerve injury
o
PNB: LAST(Local anaesthetic systemic toxicity), hematoma, organ
o
injury
Use ultrasound guidance to reduce risk
o
11/5/2025 Dr Gona Ahmed 29
=====PAGE 30 [T]=====
Cognitive Dysfunction & Convulsions
Delirium: common in elderly, multifactorial
o
Convulsions: drug-induced or true seizures (e.g. hypoxia, toxicity)
o
Shivering - common after volatile anaesthetics even when
o
temperature is normal.
11/5/2025 Dr Gona Ahmed 30
=====PAGE 31 [T]=====
Renal & Hepatic Complications
Rare as direct anesthesia effects
o
Common in patients with pre-existing disease
o
Prevention: avoid nephrotoxic & hepatotoxic drugs, maintain
o
perfusion to liver and kidney
11/5/2025 Dr Gona Ahmed 31
=====PAGE 32 [T]=====
Drug Reactions & PONV
Errors: wrong drug, dose, route, timing
o
PONV( postoperative nausea & vomiting) multifactorial – patient,
o
anesthetic, surgical factors
Prevention: TIVA (total intravenous anaesthesia), antiemetics, regional
o
techniques ( Avoid GA)
11/5/2025 Dr Gona Ahmed 32
=====PAGE 33 [T]=====
Summary
Complications are multifactorial and often preventable
o
Vigilance, preparation, and communication are key
o
Tailor anesthesia care to individual patient risks
o
