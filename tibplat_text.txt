Peri articular injuries commonly associated with soft
tissue injuries.
• Affect male in 40s (high energy).
• Female in 70s (falls).
Mechanism of injury:
❖ Varus or valgus force combined with axial loading as in:
1. Car striking a pedestrian (bumper fracture).
2. FFH with varus or valgus bending.
The tibial condyle split by the opposing
femoral condyle.
• Commonly cause meniscal and
ACL injuries

FRACTURE CLASSIFICATION (SCHATZKER)
•
Type I (Pure cleavage) :
A
typical wedge-shaped uncomminuted fragment is split off the lateral
condyle and displaced laterally and downward. This fracture is common in
younger persons.
• Type II (cleavage combined with depression) :
A lateral wedge is split off, but in addition the articular surface is depressed
down into the metaphysis. This tends to occur in older people.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 4
=====PAGE 5 [T]=====
• Type III (pure central depression):
The articular surface is driven into the plateau, the lateral cortex is intact.
These tend to occur in osteoporotic bone.
• Type IV (fractures of medial condyle):
These may be split off as a single wedge or may be comminuted and
depressed. The tibial spines often are involved.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 5
=====PAGE 6 [T]=====
• Type V (bicondylar fractures):
Both tibial plateaus are split off. The distinguishing feature is that the
metaphysis and diaphyses retain
continuity.
• Type VI (plateau fracture with dissociation of
metaphysis and diaphyses):
A transverse or oblique fracture of the proximal tibia is present in addition to
a fracture of one or both tibial condyles and articular surfaces.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 6

Clinical Features
• Swelling, deformity, extensive bruising and doughy feeling of
the joint due to hemarthroses.
• Neurovascular examination is a must, TYPE IV may cause
neuropraxia of common peroneal nerve.
• Always exclude the presence of compartment syndrome.
• Examination under anesthesia may reveal medial or lateral collateral
ligament injuries.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 7

Treatment
• Type I:
Non displaced: aspirate the hemarthroses then plaster
immobilization, partial wt. bearing after 3weeks, plaster
removal after 4weeks, full wt. bearing after 8weeks.
Displaced: can be fixed with two transverse cancellous screws.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 9
=====PAGE 10 [T]=====
• Type II:
1. If the depression is less than 5mm or if instability cannot
be demonstrated on stress, treatment is conservative.
2. If the depression is severe or if instability can be
demonstrated on stress, the articular fragments should be
elevated and bone-grafted, and the lateral cortex is
supported with a buttress plate.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 10
=====PAGE 11 [T]=====
• Type III:
The same as type II.
• Type IV:
These fractures tend to angulate into varus and should be
treated by open reduction and fixation with a medial
buttress plate and cancellous screws.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 11

▪ Type V: (Span, Scan, Fix)
(cid:0) Both condyles can be fixed with buttress plates and
cancellous screws, when swelling subsides.
(cid:0) If severe comminution, compartment, soft tissue
compromise; closed reduction, percutaneous reduction and
circular external fixation.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 12

• Type VI: (Span, Scan, Fix)
Should be treated with buttress plates and cancellous screws.
Circular pin and wire fixators (illizarof), also have been
advocated for fixation of these difficult fractures with high
complication rates if fixed internally.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 13

Complications
• Early: compartment syndrome, neurovascular
injuries, fracture blisters.
• Late: stiffness, deformity, instability, osteoarthritis.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 14

Fracture Tibia and Fibula
Most common long bone fracture, due to its subcutaneous
position commonly sustain compound fracture.
Mechanism of injury:
1.Twisting force:
spiral fracture of both bones at different
level, usually low force injury, minimal
soft tissue injury and the bone may
penetrate the skin from within.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 15
=====PAGE 16 [T]=====
2. Angulatory direct force:
transverse or short
oblique fracture of both at the same level, high energy,
more comminution, more soft tissue injury and more
chance to be compound.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 16

Clinical Features
Pain, swelling, bruises, crushing the skin, open
fracture, circulatory changes, check always for
impending compartment syndrome.

Managements
Depend on certain factors:
• State of the soft tissue.
• Severity of bony injury (spiral or transverse,
comminuted).
• Stability of the fracture (oblique, butterfly,
comminuted, segmental) are unstable.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 19
=====PAGE 20 [T]=====
• Conservative treatments:
Low energy fractures, minimally displaced, closed, most
pediatric fractures, Non ambulatory paralyzed patient.
❖ Reduction if needed (MUA); immobilization in a full POP
cast from midthigh to metatarsal necks.
❖ If skin in doubt open a window for daily observation.
❖ Elevation and observation for72 hours, after 2 weeks check
x-ray, functional brace at 4 weeks, partial wt. bearing for
8-16weeks.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 20
=====PAGE 21 [T]=====
• Operative treatments:
Unstable high energy fractures, comminuted, segmental,
low energy fractures cannot be hold satisfactorily by
conservative way.
1. Closed locked intramedullary nailing: the
standard method for most fractures.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 21
=====PAGE 22 [T]=====
2. Closed reduction and percutaneous
elastic nail fixation:
Open or closed fractures, floating knee in skeletally
immature patients.

staged IM nailing:
compound fractures, infected

4. obese children with open
physis.

Complications
Early:
1. Vascular injury: rare, occur with proximal
fractures.
2. Compartment syndrome:
(young patient, severe injury, delay treatment,
shock, excessive manipulation with long
operation).
3. Infection: Open fractures, after plate and
screw fixation.
10/11/24 Areewan MS Saeed 25
=====PAGE 26 [T]=====
4. Malunion: varus and valgus(poor management).
5. Delay union and non union: common( poor soft
tissue, comminuted, segmental, compound, infected……).
6. Ankle and foot stiffness.
