# Integrated Management of Childhood Illness (IMCI)

## The Big Picture
IMCI = a **WHO/UNICEF strategy** to cut under-5 death/illness by treating the **whole child**, not single diseases (sick children in poor settings often have several conditions at once). It combines prevention + cure at first-level facilities, and is **6× cheaper per child** than usual care.

## Why It Exists
- **5.9 million under-5 deaths (2015)**; **83%** from infectious/neonatal/nutritional causes — mostly **preventable**.
- Top child killers: **pneumonia (#1), diarrhoea (#2)** + undernutrition underlying ~53%.
- Single-disease approaches fail because children present with overlapping conditions → **"look at the child as a whole."**

## The 3 Components
1. **Improve health-worker skills** (case-management guidelines + training + follow-up).
2. **Improve health systems** (drugs, supplies, supervision, referral).
3. **Improve family/community practices** (breastfeeding, complementary feeding, vaccination, growth monitoring, early care-seeking).

## Diseases Covered (the big 5 — >70% of child mortality)
**Diarrhoea, Acute Respiratory Infection (pneumonia), Malaria, Measles, Malnutrition.** Other problems → "**Assess Other Problems**" box.

## Age Groups
- **Young infant: up to 2 months.**
- **Child: 2 months up to 5 years.**

## Case Management Process (6 steps)
1. **Assess** child. 2. **Classify** illness. 3. **Identify treatment**. 4. **Treat** (pre-referral / medical / home). 5. **Counsel** caregiver (feeding, mother's health). 6. **Follow-up**.

## General Danger Signs (check in ALL sick children)
- **Unable to drink/breastfeed**, **vomits everything**, **convulsions** (past or now), **lethargic/unconscious**.
- Then assess **4 main symptoms: cough/difficult breathing, diarrhoea, fever, ear problem** + nutrition, immunisation, vitamin A.

## Classification = Traffic-Light Rows
- **RED** — severe → urgent referral/admission.
- **YELLOW** — specific treatment at health centre (antibiotic, antimalarial) + teach home care.
- **GREEN** — home care, no specific drug.

## Example: Cough / Difficult Breathing
- Danger sign / chest indrawing / stridor → **Severe pneumonia → first-dose antibiotic + refer urgently**.
- **Fast breathing → Pneumonia → oral antibiotic 5 days**, follow-up 2 days.
- No signs → cough/cold → soothe, follow-up 6 days (refer if cough >30 days).

## Walk-Away Points
- IMCI = **WHO/UNICEF, treat the whole child**, cheaper + cuts under-5 mortality.
- Covers **diarrhoea, ARI/pneumonia, malaria, measles, malnutrition** (>70% of deaths).
- Age groups: **<2 months** and **2 months–5 years**.
- General danger signs: **can't drink, vomits everything, convulsions, lethargy**.
- Classify by **red/yellow/green**; pneumonia = **fast breathing → oral antibiotic**.
