=====SLIDE 6=====
Anxiety Management
Surgical patients have a high incidence of anxiety. 
Undesirable psychological effects has been associated with reduced patient satisfaction and an increased risk of postoperative nausea and vomiting (PONV) and acute postoperative pain. 
Dr  Gona Ahmed
=====SLIDE 7=====
Non-Pharmacological Measures
Establishing a rapport at the preoperative visit.
Providing a clear explanation of the process and events. 
Seeking to address specific fears with honest advice.
Empathy. 
Reassurance.
Basic psychotherapeutic and relaxation techniques.

=====SLIDE 9=====
Benzodiazepines – Dosing Considerations
Adjust for age, weight, comorbidities, and surgery type:
Dosing Consideration
Age: Lower dose in elderly
Weight: Adjust on BMI
Comorbidities: In liver or kidney impairment, prolonged drug clearance.
Surgical type: Tailor based on procedure length, and expected stress level.
Dr  Gona Ahmed
=====SLIDE 10=====
Benzodiazepines – Mechanism
 Enhances the effect of gamma aminobutyric acid( GABA) at  GABA-A receotprs in the central nervous system
 Increase Cl⁻ influx → neuron hyperpolarization
Effects: sedation, amnesia, muscle relaxation & anxiolysis.
Dr  Gona Ahmed
=====SLIDE 11=====
Midazolam
Dose ( Adult)1-2 mg IV,  7.5-15 mg PO.
Route: IV, PO, IM.
Onset: 3-5 min (IV), 15-30 min(PO).
Duration: 1-4 hrs.
Is a rapid-acting drug by virtue of its high lipid solubility, short acting .
Very strong amnestic potency.
Dr  Gona Ahmed
=====SLIDE 12=====
Diazepam & Lorazepam
Diazepam: 5–10 mg PO/IV/IM (2-4 hrs), moderate amnesia.
Lorazepam: 1–2 mg PO/IV/IM, long duration(6-8 hrs), strong amnesia.
Dr  Gona Ahmed
=====SLIDE 13=====
Benzodiazepine Side Effects
Dr  Gona Ahmed
Respiratory depression: Especially when combined with opioids or other CNS depressants.
Hypotension: Reduces systemic vascular resistance( SVR). 
Paradoxical reactions: Rarely, agitation or aggression.
Prolonged Sedation: Especially with longer acting agents like diazepam or lorazepam.
Amnesia: While usually desired, this may be problematic postoperatively if the patient has important instructions to remember.
=====SLIDE 14=====
Contraindications & Special Populations
Absolute: severe respiratory insufficiency, allergy
Caution: elderly, hepatic/renal impairment, substance abuse
Pediatrics: Midazolam preferred
Pregnancy: Avoid unless necessary
Dr  Gona Ahmed

=====SLIDE 16=====
Analgesia in Premedication
Adminstering medications before aesthesia induction to manage pain before, during, and after surgery.
Prevent central sensitization, so reducing opioid consumption& postoperative pain.
Improve recovery  and control inflammatory and surgical stress response.
A multimodal approach combining different drug classes offers the best results while minimizing side effects.
Dr  Gona Ahmed

=====SLIDE 19=====
Paracetamol & Gabapentinoids
Paracetamol: 1g IV/PO
Gabapentin: 300–600 mg PO 1-2 hours preoperatively (Block calicium channels in the CNS, reducing neuronal excitability).
Pregabalin: 75–150 mg PO.

=====SLIDE 21=====
Beta-Blockers & Anticholinergics
Continue perioperatively if already on beta blocker.
Anticholinergics: Atropine, Glycopyrrolate, Scopolamine (Reduce salivary and respiratory secretions and prevent bradycardia)
Dr  Gona Ahmed
=====SLIDE 22=====
             Antiemetics
To prevent nausea and vomiting (PONV) in high risk patients.
5- HT3 Receptor Antagonists ( Gold Stanadard), standard medication for PONV.
In Laparoscopic, gynecological, and major abdominal surgeries
Ordansetron:  4-8 mg IV/PO
Granisetron:   1 mg IV/PO
Dopamine Antagonists : Metoclopramide(10-20 mg) IV/PO/IM, promotes gastric motility, decreases gastric volume and the risk of aspiration.
Steroids (Dexamethasone).
Dr  Gona Ahmed
=====SLIDE 23=====
Acid Suppression
H2 blockers and PPIs: Ranitidine, Cimetidine, Omeprazole, Pantoprazole
Reduce Gastric Acid Sedcretion, increase stomach PH to prevent aspiration pneumonitis.
Reduce Gastric Volume: Minimize the amount of acid fluid available for aspiration.
 Antacids: Sodium citrate
Increases PH but does not decrease volume of gastric contents.
Indicated in GERD, trauma (emergency), obstetrics
Dr  Gona Ahmed
=====SLIDE 24=====
Local Anesthetics in Premedication
Mechanism: Block Na⁺ channels → inhibit nerve conduction → analgesia, nerve block, regional/local anesthesia.
Preoperative: IV cannulation, arterial line placement:
Lidocaine: 1–2% (20–50 mg IV/local); blunts propofol pain; lasts 1–2 hrs.
Bupivacaine: 0.25–0.5% (infiltration, epidural, spinal); lasts 4–8 hrs
Pediatric Tip: Use EMLA (Lidocaine–Prilocaine) cream 30–60 min before cannulation.
Dr  Gona Ahmed
