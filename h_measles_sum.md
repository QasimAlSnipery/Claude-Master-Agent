# Measles & Rubella

## The Big Picture
Two RNA-virus exanthems with **maculopapular rashes**, droplet spread, human-only reservoirs, and **MMR vaccine** prevention. Measles = **highly contagious** (90% attack rate) with serious complications (pneumonia, encephalitis, SSPE). Rubella = mild in children but **devastating to the fetus (congenital rubella syndrome)**.

---

## MEASLES (Rubeola)
- **Virus:** Paramyxovirus (Morbillivirus), single-stranded RNA, **one genotype**; killed by heat/light, survives <2 h on surfaces.
- **Reservoir:** human only. **90% attack rate** in susceptibles; spreads to 9–10 contacts (R0 high).
- **Transmission:** droplet (airborne hours). **Winter** peak.
- **Incubation:** 10–12 days. **Infectious: 4 days before to 4 days after rash.**

### Clinical (the 3 C's + Koplik + rash)
- **Prodrome (2–4 d):** high fever + **Cough, Coryza, Conjunctivitis (3 C's)**.
- **Koplik spots** — blue-white on red buccal mucosa, **pathognomonic** (1–2 days before rash).
- **Maculopapular rash** — starts at **hairline/face → spreads down to hands/feet**, lasts 5–6 days, fades head-to-toe.

### Complications (30% have ≥1)
- Diarrhoea (8%), **otitis media** (7%), **pneumonia** (6% — commonest cause of death in children).
- **Encephalitis** 1/1000 (CFR 15%; death cause in adults), seizures.
- **SSPE** (subacute sclerosing panencephalitis) — 5/million, ~7 years later, fatal degenerative CNS disease.
- Death 1–3/1000 (up to 25% in malnourished).

### Diagnosis & Prevention
- Clinical; lab = **measles IgM** or IgG rise (ELISA); isolation from urine/nasopharynx.
- **Live attenuated vaccine** (Edmonston-Enders); MCV/MMR/MMRV. **Nationally notifiable.**
- Schedule: 9 mo (85%), 12 mo MMR1 (95%), 18 mo MMR2 (98%); **2 doses from 12 months** (dose before 12 mo not counted). SC, 0.5 mL.
- AEFI: fever (5–15%), rash, joint symptoms (25%), rare thrombocytopenia.
- **Contraindications:** severe allergy, **pregnancy, immunosuppression**, recent blood product.

## RUBELLA (German measles, "little red")
- **Virus:** Togaviridae (Rubivirus), RNA, one type.
- **Transmission:** droplet/respiratory; **incubation 14 days (12–23)**; infectious 7 d before to 5–7 d after rash; CRS infants shed for a year.

### Clinical
- Mild: low-grade fever prodrome, **posterior lymphadenopathy (2nd week)**, maculopapular rash 14–17 d after exposure.
- **Complications:** **arthralgia/arthritis (adult females up to 70%)**, thrombocytopenic purpura, encephalitis.

### Congenital Rubella Syndrome (CRS) — the key danger
- 1st-trimester infection → **up to 85% of fetuses affected**.
- Classic: **deafness, cataracts, heart defects** (+ microcephaly, mental retardation, hepatosplenomegaly).

### Prevention
- **Live attenuated, in MMR**, SC 0.5 mL, from **12 months**.
- Emphasis: **non-pregnant women of childbearing age**. Contraindicated in pregnancy.

## Walk-Away Points
- Measles: **3 C's + Koplik spots** (pathognomonic) + descending rash; **pneumonia = top killer, SSPE** late.
- Measles infectious **4 days before–4 after rash**; 2 MMR doses from 12 months.
- Rubella mild BUT **CRS = deafness + cataracts + heart defects** (1st trimester).
- Both: live MMR, **contraindicated in pregnancy**.
