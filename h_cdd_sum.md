# Control of Diarrhoeal Disease (CDD)

## The Big Picture
Diarrhoea = **≥3 liquid/watery stools in 24 h** (consistency matters more than frequency). It kills ~**3 million children/year (6/minute)** — but **the killer is dehydration, not the diarrhoea itself**. The whole strategy: assess dehydration (A/B/C), pick the right plan, and rehydrate with **ORS**.

## Definitions
- **Diarrhoea** — ≥3 watery stools/24 h. (Breastfed infants passing soft yellow stool after feeds = **normal**, not diarrhoea.)
- **Acute** ≤14 days; **persistent** ≥14 days.
- **Dysentery** = **blood (+ mucus) in stool** (amoebic or bacillary/Shigella).
- **Causes:** viruses, bacteria, protozoa (NOT fungi).

## Why It Matters
- Leads to malnutrition (~**25 g weight loss per day** of diarrhoea) → ↓ immunity → more episodes.
- **At-risk infants:** artificially-fed; **weaning age (~6 months)** (contaminated food, low resistance).
- Diarrhoea is partly protective (washes out organisms) — self-limiting; **dehydration is the danger**.

## Dehydration Assessment (A / B / C)
| Sign | A (none) | B (some) | C (severe) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Condition | Well, alert | Restless, irritable | Lethargic/unconscious |
| Eyes | Normal | Sunken | Sunken |
| Thirst | Drinks normally | Eager to drink | Unable to drink |
| Skin pinch | Back quickly | Back slowly | Back very slowly |
| Plan | **A (home)** | **B (PHC)** | **C (hospital)** |

## Treatment Plans
- **Plan A (prevent dehydration, home):** extra fluid + **ORS**, continue feeding, teach mother ORS prep + danger signs.
- **Plan B (correct some dehydration, health centre):** **ORS = weight(kg) × 75 mL over 4 h**; reassess hourly; continue breastfeeding; if vomits, wait 10 min then restart slowly; **puffy face/eyes = overhydration → slow down**. Reassess at 4 h, re-classify.
- **Plan C (severe, hospital):** urgent IV **Ringer lactate** (or NS) = weight × 100 mL.
  - **<12 months:** 30 mL/kg first **1 h**, then 70 mL/kg over **5 h**.
  - **≥12 months:** 30 mL/kg first **30 min**, then 70 mL/kg over **2.5 h**.
  - Reassess hourly; if able to drink, ORS 5 mL/kg/h.

## ORS Composition (per 1 L water)
- **Na 3.5 g, NaHCO₃ 2.2 g, KCl 1.5 g, glucose 20 g.** Cheap, effective, easy. **Glucose drives sodium-glucose co-transport** → water absorption. ORS dramatically cut diarrhoea fatality.

## Persistent Diarrhoea
- **<6 months + dehydrated → refer to hospital.**
- >6 months, no dehydration → **dietary**: dilute animal milk with equal water (or yogurt), ↑ energy (6 meals/day), reassess in 5 days; after recovery, extra meal/day for a month.

## Key Don'ts
- **No anti-motility drugs in children <5** (risk of **paralytic ileus**).
- Don't stop feeding; don't give plain water alone.

## Walk-Away Points
- **Dehydration kills, not the diarrhoea**; dysentery = blood + mucus.
- Assess A/B/C → **Plan A (home) / B (75 mL/kg ORS over 4 h) / C (IV 100 mL/kg)**.
- ORS = **glucose + Na co-transport**; puffy eyes = overhydration.
- **<6 months + dehydrated = hospital**; no anti-motility under 5.
