# Lymphedema

## The Big Picture
Lymphedema = **chronic** swelling from too much lymph fluid — either **impaired outflow (obstruction)** or **overproduction exceeding transport capacity**. Protein-rich fluid pools → fibrosis → skin changes → eventually **elephantiasis**. No cure; management is lifelong. Don't confuse with **lipedema** (fat) or **venous edema**.

## Lymph Basics
- Lymph = WBCs, triglycerides, cell debris, water, **protein**.
- Drainage chain: capillaries → precollectors → collectors → trunk → nodes.

## Classification — Primary vs Secondary
**Primary** (1 in 6000–10,000 births; **females 2–20×**, peak 12–16 yrs). Sub-classified by:
- **Time of onset:**
  - **Congenital** — at birth/first year; sporadic or AD (Milroy); linked to **Turner, Klinefelter, Trisomy 21**; bilateral whole-leg.
  - **Praecox (Meige's)** — at puberty/3rd decade; **most common (up to 94%)**; F:M **10:1**; unilateral foot+calf; oestrogen role.
  - **Tarda** — after **35 yrs**; ~10% of primary.
- **Morphology:** aplasia (none), hypoplasia (few — commonest), numerical hyperplasia, hyperplasia (megalymphatics — worst prognosis).
- **Anatomy:** distal obstruction (⅓; bilateral mild, female, good prognosis) vs proximal obstruction (½; unilateral severe, relentless, may need surgery).

**Secondary (acquired) — most common overall.** Causes:
- **Cancer** — breast cancer treatment (**axillary dissection + radiotherapy**) → upper-limb; gynae/urologic → lower-limb/pelvis; melanoma after radical dissection.
- **Filariasis** — *Wuchereria bancrofti* (90%), Brugia; commonest cause in tropics; **90 million infected**; adult worms → fibrosis; **eosinophilia**, microfilariae in **nocturnal blood**; → gross **elephantiasis**.
- **Trauma** — burns, circumferential wounds, infection, pregnancy.

## Clinical Staging
- **Latent** — fibrosis around lymphatics, no visible edema.
- **Grade 1** — **pits**, reduced by elevation, no fibrosis.
- **Grade 2** — **non-pitting**, not reduced by elevation, marked fibrosis.
- **Grade 3** — irreversible edema + sclerosis = **elephantiasis**.

## Clinical Signs
- Soft pitting edema early (protein-rich).
- **Stemmer's sign positive** — can't tent the skin at base of 2nd toe (hallmark).
- **Square toes**, **buffalo hump** (foot dorsum), woody texture over time.
- Skin: pinkish-red early → thick, hyperkeratosis, lichenification, **peau d'orange**; lymphorrhea.
- **Yellow nail syndrome** = primary lymphedema + yellow nails + pleural effusion.
- Pain usually just aching/heaviness — intense pain → suspect infection.

## Complications
- **Recurrent infection / cellulitis** — most troublesome; worsens edema.
- Malnutrition + immunodeficiency (protein-losing enteropathy, chylous ascites/thorax).
- **Lymphangiosarcoma** — rare malignancy, may need amputation.

## Diagnosis
- Exam (Stemmer, skin), **isotopic lymphoscintigraphy** (key test), near-infrared fluorescence, MRI/CT, US.
- **DDx:** lipedema, venous insufficiency, systemic bilateral pitting (cardiac/renal/liver/hypoproteinemia/myxedema), Klippel-Trenaunay.

## Management (no cure)
- **Conservative first line** — **Complex Decongestive Therapy**: manual lymphatic drainage + compression bandaging + compression garments + skin care + exercise; lifelong.
- **Operative** (last resort) — volume reduction/liposuction; reconstruction (**lymphovenous anastomosis**, lymphatic grafting).

## Walk-Away Points
- **Praecox (Meige's) = commonest primary** (94%), puberty, F:M 10:1.
- **Secondary is commonest overall**; breast-cancer axillary surgery (arm) + **filariasis** (tropics) are top causes.
- **Stemmer's sign** = the clinical hallmark.
- Staging: pits (G1) → non-pitting fibrosis (G2) → elephantiasis (G3).
- Lifelong **complex decongestive therapy**; surgery last.
